Benefits & credits: Adult Dependants' Grant

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Full-time students in higher education with an adult who depends on them financially can apply for an Adult Dependants’ Grant of up to £2,642.

You can apply for this grant on top of any other student finance and you don’t have to pay it back.

Part 2: What you'll get

The maximum Adult Dependants’ Grant is £2,642 a year. You don’t have to pay this money back.

The amount you get depends on:

  • your income
  • the dependant adult’s income

The money is paid each term in 3 instalments directly into your bank account.

The Adult Dependants’ Grant will affect any income-related benefits and tax credits you might get.

Part 3: Eligibility

Who counts as a dependant adult

Usually the dependant adult will be a member of your family, including your husband, wife, civil partner or partner.

Grown up children can’t be counted as dependant adults.

An unmarried partner can only be claimed as a dependant adult if you’re over 25.

Part 4: How to claim

  1. Fill in the Adult Dependants’ Grant section on your main student finance application - you’ll need to give estimates of your household income.

  2. Student Finance England will assess your application and contact you if they need more information.

  3. Student Finance England will send you a letter telling you if you qualify and how much money you’ll get.

  4. At the end of the academic year Student Finance England will send you an income confirmation form to check if your actual outgoings match your estimates in the application. They’ll then adjust your payments accordingly.

Part 5: Further information

Documents you may need to provide

As part of your application Student Finance England may contact you and ask for further information on your financial circumstances. Proof they might ask for can include:

  • your P60
  • Child Benefit details
  • family tax credits details
  • financial information from your partner – eg, bank statements

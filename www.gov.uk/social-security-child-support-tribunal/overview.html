Part 1: Overview

If you disagree with a decision about benefits, tax credits or a child support payment, you can appeal to the Social Security and Child Support Tribunal.

The tribunal is independent and can change the decision if they think it’s wrong.

It can take the tribunal a few months to deal with your case.

Who to appeal to

You need to appeal to the organisation that made the decision:

Decision you’re appealing against Who to appeal to
Benefits like Jobseeker’s Allowance or Employment and Support Allowance Department for Work and Pensions
Child support payments Child Maintenance Group
Tax credits HM Revenue and Customs
Housing Benefit your local council
Council Tax Benefit your local council

Before you appeal, ask the department or council if they can look at the decision again, and reconsider it.

How to appeal

You can appeal by letter or filling in a form - details will be on the letter giving you the decision.

Include any evidence or information supporting your appeal - eg a letter from your doctor.

You usually have one month to appeal against a decision - if you miss the deadline, explain why you’re late.

What the department or council will do with your appeal

After you send your appeal, the council or department may change their decision - eg let you have the benefit or tax credit.

If they disagree with your appeal, they will forward the documents in the case to you and the tribunal.

The tribunal will let you know if it can look into your case. If it can’t, it will explain why.

Quick answer Change your name (by deed poll)

You don’t have to follow a legal procedure if you want to start using a new name. However, you might need a deed poll to apply for or change official documents, like a passport or driving licence.

A deed poll is a legal document that proves a change of name. You can get one through a specialist agency, a solicitor or by applying to the Royal Courts of Justice.

To change your name by deed poll you can:

  • download the appropriate form from the Ministry of Justice website
  • search online for ‘deed poll’ and find a specialist agency
  • find a solicitor

You may have to pay a fee to change your name by deed poll.

Changing your name after marriage

You don’t need a deed poll if you want to take your husband’s surname after marriage. If you send a copy of your marriage certificate to record holders, with a covering letter, your documents and records will be changed for free to show your new surname.

You will need a deed poll if you get married and want to double-barrel your surname.

Changing your name after divorce

After getting divorced, you may be able to go back to your maiden name by presenting your marriage certificate and your decree absolute. However, some organisations may not agree to change your name back without a deed poll.

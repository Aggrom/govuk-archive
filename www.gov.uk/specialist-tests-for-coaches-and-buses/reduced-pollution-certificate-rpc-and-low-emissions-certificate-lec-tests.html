Part 3: Reduced Pollution Certificate (RPC) and Low Emissions Certificate (LEC) tests

If you modify a bus or coach to cut its emissions, you can get it tested by the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) for a Reduced Pollution Certificate (RPC). The certificate reduces the cost of the vehicle excise duty (commonly known as road tax).

Some RPCs also mean you can drive the vehicle in the Low Emission Zone (LEZ) without charge. The LEZ covers most of Greater London and heavy diesel vehicles are charged to enter the area.

Some vehicles that donâ€™t qualify for an RPC can still get a Low Emissions Certificate (LEC) â€“ this means they can be driven in the LEZ without charge, but their road tax will stay the same.

You can have the tests done as part of your annual test, or separately. Itâ€™s cheaper as part of the annual test.

Eligibility for the RPC test

To get an RPC, a bus or coach must either:

  • meet enhanced environmental standards, approved by the Vehicle Certification Agency
  • be fitted with an engine of a higher environmental standard
  • be converted to run on gas

Any bus or coach that was registered in the UK before October 2009 can take the RPC test.

Some vehicles that arenâ€™t eligible for an RPC can still get an LEC. Your bus or coach qualifies if it was registered in Great Britain before 1 October 2006 and has been fitted with a full flow particulate filter approved by Transport for London (TfL).

What happens at the test

The tests for both the RPC and the LEC are in 2 parts. There is a physical inspection to check any modifications, like a filter fitted to the exhaust, and then a â€˜smoke opacity testâ€™ to check emissions.

If the vehicle passes both parts of one of the tests, VOSA will issue you either an RPC or an LEC. If your vehicle is registered in Great Britain, these details are then automatically passed to TfL by VOSA so you can use the vehicle in the Low Emission Zone.

If your vehicle is registered outside Great Britain, you need to register your certificate with TfL yourself.

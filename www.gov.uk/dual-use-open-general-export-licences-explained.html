Introduction

Open General Export Licences (OGELs) are pre-published export licences issued by the Export Control Organisation. They are the most flexible type of strategic export licence available for use by exporters, as long as all pre-set conditions can be met. In each case they allow the export of specified controlled items (identified by a ‘control list entry’ heading) to specific destinations permitted by the licence. For more background, read about open general licences: an overview.

The ECO issues a wide range of OGELs, including for dual-use items (ie controlled goods and technology with both civil and military applications). Dual-use items are controlled under European Union (EU) legislation - the EU Dual-Use Regulation.

If you can meet all OGEL licensing conditions, you do not need to apply for a Standard Individual Export Licence (SIEL). If however you cannot and the goods are still controlled then you will need to apply for a SIEL.

In addition to these dual-use OGELs, published by the the UK government, you might be able to use a European Union General Export Licence (EU GEA) published by the EU.

All OGEL holders are subject to regular compliance audits conducted by the Export Control Organisation (ECO).

Your responsibilities in using an OGEL

Before using any OGEL for your export purposes, you should first check that you can meet all its defined terms and conditions. To do so, you can use the ECO’s OGEL Checker database. You should also know the control list classification or ‘rating’ of your product. If you are unsure what this is, you should consult the UK Strategic Export Control Lists. For more information, see guidance about strategic exports: when to request an export licence
It is also your responsibility as the exporter to:

  • register to use OGELs on ECO’s export licensing database, [SPIRE(https://www.spire.bis.gov.uk)
  • use OGELs within all pre-defined terms and conditions
  • keep up-to-date with any OGEL amendments - eg changes to permitted or excluded destinations or goods
  • be prepared for compliance audits

Details of amendments are published in Export Control Organisation (ECO) Notices to Exporters. You can read Notices to Exporters on the BIS website. To receive these notices directly to your email inbox, you are advised to join the ECO mailing list. You can do this and find contact details for the ECO on the BIS website.

OGEL (Chemicals)

The OGEL (Chemicals) allows, subject to conditions, certain chemicals to be exported from the UK or any other EU Member State (with the exception of some countries), so long as the exporter is established in the UK. There is a 20kg limit on shipments.

Current OGEL

You can download the current OGEL (Chemicals) (PDF, 36KB).

The current licence entered into force on 27 August 2009.

You will need to state the exact name of the licence or the SPIRE reference on any supporting documentation given to HMRC.

OGEL (Cryptographic Development)

The OGEL (Cryptographic Development) allows, subject to certain conditions and destinations, the export of certain types of cryptographic development software and technology.

Current OGEL

The current licence is dated 21 December 2010 and entered into force on 30 April 2010. All previous licences are now revoked.

You will need to state the exact name of the licence or the SPIRE Reference on any supporting documentation given to HMRC.

OGEL (Export after exhibition: Dual-Use Items)

The OGEL (Export after exhibition: Dual-Use Items) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of dual-use items after they have been temporarily imported for exhibition into the UK, or any other EU Member State, where the exporter is established in the UK.

OGEL (Export after repair/replacement under warranty: Dual-Use Items)

The OGEL (Export after repair/replacement under warranty: Dual-Use Items) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of Dual-Use items which have been imported into the UK or any EU Member State for repair or replacement under warranty.

OGEL (Export for repair/replacement under warranty: Dual-Use Items)

The OGEL (Export for repair/replacement under warranty: Dual-Use Items) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of Dual-Use items which had been previously imported into the UK, or any other EU Member State, to be subsequently returned or exported to the original country for repair or replacement under warranty, provided they are to be returned to the UK after such repair/replacement.

The exporter must have an established presence in the UK. The goods can also be exported if they are being returned as unwanted goods.

OGEL (Dual-Use Items: Hong Kong Special Administrative Region)

The OGEL (Dual-Use Items: Hong Kong Special Administrative Region) permits, without further authority but subject to certain conditions, the export for non-military use, of a number of items - but check on the licence - from the UK, or from any other EU Member State, where the exporter is established in the UK to any destination in the Hong Kong Special Administrative Region.

OGEL (International Non-Proliferation Regime De-controls: Dual-Use Items)

This OGEL allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of specified dual-use items. 

The international non-proliferation regimes (in which the UK actively participates along with other countries) have agreed that the items specifically listed on the licence should be de-controlled.

This OGEL is therefore intended as a temporary measure placing the specified items under a less restricted form of licensing until such time as the Regulation and the associated UK Strategic Export Control Lists are updated.

Background about controls on dual-use items 

The control on the export of dual-use items is applied via the EU Dual-Use Regulation. This is currently being amended to take into account changes agreed by EU Member States in the international non-proliferation regimes during 2011 and will be reflected in amendments to the UK Strategic Export Control Lists in due course.

To be aware of when the Control Lists are updated you should sign up to the ECO’s Notices to Exporters email service, as detailed below.

OGEL (Low value shipments)

The OGEL (Low value shipments) allows, subject to certain conditions, a single export order of certain goods to a named consignee in one or more consignments, the total value of which is not more than £6,000. The shipments can be exported from the UK or any other EU Member State where the exporter is established in the UK.

OGEL (Technology for dual-use items)

The OGEL (Technology for dual-use items) allows, subject to certain conditions, the export of technology for Dual-Use items from the UK or any other EU Member State where the exporter is established in the UK. Technology is the information needed for the development and production of goods such as blueprints, plans, diagrams, models, formulae, tables, designs, specifications, manuals and instructions.

This OGEL includes the transmission of software or technology by fax, telephone or other electronic media.

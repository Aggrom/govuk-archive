Service Apply for a nitrate vulnerable zone (NVZ) derogation online

Not what you're looking for? ↓

To spread more than 170 kilograms of nitrogen from manure from grazing livestock on your farm, you must apply for a ‘derogation’. If your application is approved and you meet certain conditions, you’ll be able to spread up to 250 kilograms of nitrogen per hectare per year.

You must apply for a derogation each year.

Telephone and postal applications

Call the Environment Agency’s Derogation Line to:

  • apply by telephone
  • get a paper application form

Environment Agency Derogation Line
0845 603 3113

Getting help

Contact the Defra Helpline if you have any queries about nitrate vulnerable zone regulations and the rules about derogations.

Defra Helpline
onlinehelp@defra.gsi.gov.uk
08459 33 55 77
Monday to Friday, 9am to 5pm

Eligibility

To be eligible, at least 80% of your farm must be grassland. If the derogation is approved you’ll have to comply with additional requirements.

You can also apply by telephone or get a paper application form by calling the Environment Agency’s Derogation Line on 0845 603 3113.

Scotland and Wales

If you’re in Scotland, you can apply for the derogation through the Scottish Government website.

If you’re in Wales, you can find out more about the derogation through the Netregs website or apply by calling the NVZ Wales helpline.

NVZ Wales helpline
01824 704060

Agents

Sign up as an agent if you want to apply for a derogation for more than one farmer.

Cross compliance

These standards are part of cross compliance. You must meet them to get your farming subsidy under the Single Payment Scheme.

Quick answer Handling someone's tax on their behalf

You need permission from the person you’re helping if you want to deal with HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) on their behalf.

Calls to HMRC and short-term dealings

For general calls, for example to ask for a form, you don’t need permission from the person you’re helping.

If you call HMRC to discuss personal tax information the person whose affairs you’re handling must be in the room with you. HMRC will confirm their identity and make sure they’re happy for you to represent them. You must also have their tax reference number ready.

HMRC might ask you for the phone number you’re calling from so they can call you back.

HMRC has customer services for people with a disability.

If you’re interpreting for a non-English speaker you must be over 16. HMRC’s helpline also offers foreign language services.

Contact HMRC’s helplines.

Handling someone’s affairs permanently

The person whose affairs you’re handling has to write to HMRC to ‘appoint’ you. The letter needs to include the following information:

  • their name and address
  • their tax reference or claim number
  • your name and address
  • their signature

If they can’t write the letter themselves they need to sign it. If this isn’t possible contact an HMRC enquiry centre.

Once approved, you’ll get all future correspondence from HMRC, except tax bills or refunds. These will still be sent directly to the person you’re representing.

If you have power of attorney for someone you need to write to HMRC and tell them. HMRC will then send all correspondence to you.

Appointing an agent

You need to complete form 64-8 and send it to HMRC.

Download form 64-8 ‘Authorising your agent’ (PDF, 188 KB)

Alternatively your tax adviser or accountant can use HMRC’s online authorisation service.

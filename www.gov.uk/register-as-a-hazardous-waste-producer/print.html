Hazardous waste registration (England and Wales), a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You must register any business premises in England or Wales that produces or stores more than 500 kilograms of hazardous waste a year. This is known as a ‘hazardous waste notification’.

Once registered you’ll get a ‘premises code’ and legally be able to store or move the waste off your premises.

Your registration lasts 12 months and must be renewed 1 month before it expires.

Scotland

Companies in Scotland dealing with hazardous waste (also known as ‘special waste’) may need different licences or permits.

Businesses involved in several manufacturing and farming processes will need a pollution prevention and control permit.

Businesses who store, treat or recycle waste will need a waste management licence.

Northern Ireland

Companies in Northern Ireland don’t need to register.

Part 2: How to apply

You need:

  • one application for each premises - to register more than 5 premises at a time (known as “bulk applications”) apply by post
  • the Standard Industrial Classification Code (SICC) used to classify your business type
  • your company number - if your business is a limited company

Register online with the Environment Agency.

Other ways to apply

You can also apply by post or telephone.

Part 3: Fees

The fees are:

  • online - £18 per site
  • telephone - £23 per site
  • post - £28 per site
  • to register more than 5 sites at a time - £18 per site

For online and telephone applications pay by credit or debit card (excluding American Express and Diners Club International).

For postal applications pay by cheque, postal order or BAC.

Part 4: Exemptions

You don’t need to register a premises if:

  • you produce or store less than 500 kilograms of hazardous waste a year
  • you store less than 50 cubic metres of non-liquid waste from another premises you own for less than 3 months
  • you store less than 1,000 litres of liquid waste from another premises you own for less than 3 months
  • you produce small amounts of waste at different customer premises - you only need to register your main business premises

Contact the Environment Agency to check if you need to get a hazardous waste licence.

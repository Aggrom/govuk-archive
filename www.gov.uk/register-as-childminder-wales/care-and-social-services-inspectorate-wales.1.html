Licence Register as a childminder (Wales)

From Care and Social Services Inspectorate Wales
  1. Overview
  2. How to apply
  3. How to renew
  4. How to change

Overview

You must register with Care and Social Services Inspectorate Wales (CSSIW) if you want to become a childminder in Wales.

If you provide childminding or day care services then you must be registered. A childminder is someone who gets paid for looking after children:

  • under 8
  • for more than 2 hours a day
  • in a private home

All the adults living or working at the home where you’re based must also register.

You don’t have to register to do babysitting.

How to apply

You must first sign up for a pre-registration and information session where you will find out more about becoming a childminder:

  1. Contact your local CSSIW office to find out when the next session is being held near you

  2. Visit the CSSIW website for guidance on completing the form and on the registration process

  3. Download the application form to register as a childminder (DOC, 690KB)

There is no fee to pay

After your application

Once you’ve submitted your application the CSSIW will:

  • check your references
  • carry out checks on you with your doctor, your local authority and the Criminal Records Bureau (CRB),
  • do some checks on anyone else who will be living or working at the home
  • inspect the home

If you’re approved

If your application is approved and you pass all the checks, you will receive a certificate that shows you’re a registered childminder.

This usually takes between 2 and 3 months.

Displaying your certificate

You must display your certificate of registration - or a copy of it - where it can be easily seen.

Fines and penalties

If you provide childminding or day care services without being registered, you can be fined £5,000, sent to prison, or both.

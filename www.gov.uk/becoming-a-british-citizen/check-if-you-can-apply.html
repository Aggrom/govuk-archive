Part 1: Check if you can apply

You can apply for British citizenship if:

  • you’re 18 or over
  • you’re of sound mind - eg you can make your own decisions (or have someone applying on your behalf who can)
  • you’re of good character
  • you will continue to live in the UK
  • you’re able to communicate in English, Welsh or Scottish Gaelic
  • you’ve passed the Life in the UK test or have completed an English for speakers of other languages (ESOL) course along with citizenship classes

This process is known as ‘naturalisation’.

You must also have:

  • lived in the UK for at least 5 years before the date of your application (unless your spouse or civil partner is a British citizen)
  • spent no more than 450 days outside the UK during those 5 years
  • spent no more than 90 days outside the UK in the last 12 months
  • not broken any immigration laws while in the UK

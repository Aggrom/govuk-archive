Further education courses, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Further education (FE) courses are available to people over 16.

Courses range from basic English and maths to Higher National Diplomas.

You can use them to:

  • learn new skills
  • improve your career prospects
  • prepare for higher education

Many literacy and numeracy courses are free, and you may not have to pay for tuition if you’re studying for your first qualification equivalent to GCSEs or A levels.

Part 2: Find a course

You can find a course to suit you, whether it’s part-time, distance learning or learning at work.

Further education colleges

Further education (FE) colleges in England include:

  • sixth forms within secondary schools
  • sixth form colleges
  • further education colleges
  • specialist colleges (for particular subject areas like art and design)
  • adult education institutes
  • distance learning providers

You can compare all further education colleges and how they perform on FE Choices.

Further education courses

Courses range from English, maths and IT to Higher National Diplomas (HNDs).

Use the National Careers Service course search to find courses by course name, provider or by subject.

Apply for a course

You usually need to apply directly to the college or learning provider.

You may need to apply in person to prove you’re entitled to funding.

Enrolment dates vary but you will usually need to enrol at least a week before your course starts. If you enrol early you’re more likely to get a place on the course you want.

Advice

If you need help choosing a course you can get free advice from the National Careers Service.

Part 3: If you’re 16 or 17

If you’re aged 16 or 17 you can study a further education course:

  • full-time at school or college
  • while at work

If you’re coming towards the end of a school or college course you’re guaranteed a place on an appropriate course.

Courses

If you’re working and didn’t get any Level 2 qualifications at school you may qualify for the Time Off for Study or Training (TfST) scheme. This gives you reasonable paid time off during working hours to study for an approved qualification.

Advice

Get advice to help you decide on the right course from a National Careers Service adviser.

Part 4: Financial help

You may be able to get help with the costs of:

  • your course
  • day-to-day living costs
  • childcare

Depending on your circumstances and the subject you’re studying you may qualify for the following funding:

  • discretionary Learner Support
  • Residential Support Scheme
  • Care to Learn
  • Professional and Career Development Loans

You may also be able to claim benefits like:

  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Incapacity Benefit
  • Income Support

If you’re on income-related benefits you could get a place on a free course.

You can find out more about courses and what financial help you can get through the National Careers Service.

Checks on job applicants, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Employers must check that job applicants are allowed to work in the UK before they hire them.

An employer can be fined up to £10,000 if they can’t show evidence that they checked an employee’s right to work in the UK.

An employer might also need to carry out checks on someone’s qualifications, health or criminal record.

Data protection

Employers must follow data protection rules for information on job applicants.

Part 2: Criminal records checks

The process for criminal records checks depends on what country you’re in.

Employers must tell applicants that they’re going to carry out a criminal records check, and explain why it’s necessary.

England and Wales

If a new member of staff will be working with children or vulnerable adults, the employer must get a standard or enhanced Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check for them before they can start work.

Find out what types of checks there are and how an employer can apply.

Employers must not apply for a check unless the job needs one - read ‘CRB checks: eligible positions guidance’ from the Home Office.

Basic checks

If the job doesn’t need a standard or enhanced check, employers can still get a basic check from Disclosure Scotland (even if you’re in England or Wales). This check reveals if someone has any unspent criminal convictions.

Employing ex-offenders

If employers carry out criminal record checks, they must have a policy on employing ex-offenders and show it to any applicant who asks for it.

Download ‘Sample policy on the recruitment of ex-offenders’ (PDF, 53KB).

Scotland

Get a criminal records check in Scotland from Disclosure Scotland.

Northern Ireland

Get a criminal records check in Northern Ireland from AccessNI.

Overseas applicants

Employers must get a criminal records check if the job needs one. If the worker is from another country, disclosure organisations in the UK probably won’t have any information on them. Employers can contact their country’s embassy to see if they can get a similar check done in the applicant’s country.

Part 3: Health checks

Employers can ask for a health check before hiring someone if:

  • it’s a legal requirement - eg eye tests for commercial vehicle drivers
  • it’s not a legal requirement but there is a good reason - eg because their insurers need health checks on cycle couriers

Only successful candidates should be asked to have health checks, and employers should include information about these checks in their offer letter.

Employers must have written consent from the candidate before asking for a medical report from their doctor. Candidates have the right to see the report and can ask for it to be changed or withheld from the employer.

Avoiding discrimination

Employers must make sure their checks don’t:

  • discriminate - eg by doing a health check that discriminates against disabled people and isn’t necessary for the job
  • discourage people from applying for the job

An employer who discriminates against a candidate because of a disability that doesn’t stop them from doing the job can be prosecuted.

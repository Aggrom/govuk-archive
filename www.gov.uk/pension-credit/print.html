Benefits & credits: Pension Credit

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Pension Credit is extra money for pensioners to bring your weekly income up to a minimum amount.

There are 2 parts to Pension Credit - you could get either one or both:

  • Guarantee Credit tops up your weekly income
  • Savings Credit may provide extra money if you’re over 65 and have saved towards your retirement

You can get an idea of how much Pension Credit you could get from the Pension Credit calculator.

You could get more Pension Credit if you’re severely disabled, a carer or are paying housing costs.

Part 2: What you'll get

The amount you might get for both parts of Pension Credit will depend on how much money you have coming in and how much savings you have.

Guarantee Credit

Guarantee Credit tops up your weekly income to:

  • £142.70 (single people) 
  • £217.90 (couples)

Savings Credit

Savings Credit is extra weekly payments of up to:

  • £18.54 a week (single people) 
  • £23.73 a week (couples)

You can get an idea of how much Pension Credit you could get from the Pension Credit calculator.

You could get more Pension Credit if you have special circumstances, for example, if you’re a carer, severely disabled or have housing costs.

Part 3: Eligibility

Guarantee Credit

You can claim Guarantee Credit if:

  • you live in Great Britain
  • you’ve reached your Pension Credit qualifying age
  • your weekly income is less than £142.70 (single people) or £217.90 (couples)

If your weekly income is more, you may still qualify if:

  • you’re entitled to more Pension Credit (eg because you’re severely disabled, a carer or have housing costs)
  • some of your income can be ignored (certain benefits and payments don’t count)

Savings Credit

You may be eligible for Savings Credit if you:

  • live in Great Britain
  • are 65 or over (or your partner is)
  • have a weekly income (not counting any Guarantee Credit) of more than £111.80 (£178.35 if you have a partner)

You could get up to:

  • £18.54 a week if you are single
  • £23.73 a week if you have a partner

You might still get Savings Credit if the amount of weekly income you have coming in is up to:

  • £189 a week if you are single
  • £277 a week if you have a partner

If your weekly income is more, you may still qualify if you are severely disabled, a carer or have housing costs.

The Pension Service will work out how much Savings Credit you could get when you claim.

Part 4: How to claim

Contact The Pension Service by phone to apply. You’ll need:

  • your National Insurance number
  • information about your savings, investments and income
  • your bank account details

Pension Service helpline
Telephone: 0800 99 1234
Textphone: 0800 169 0133

You can download the Pension Credit application form if you prefer. Fill it in and send to your Pension Centre (the form explains where).

Download ‘Pension Credit application form PC1’ (PDF, 945KB)

When to claim

The earliest you can start your application for Pension Credit is 4 months before you reach the qualifying age. Use the State Pension age calculator to find out when you may be able to get your Pension Credit from.

If you’re in a couple, one of you must have reached the qualifying age.

You can claim Pension Credit any time after you, or your partner have reached the qualifying age, but it can only be backdated for 3 months.

Part 5: Further information

Income that counts toward Pension Credit

The following counts as your income when working out your Pension Credit entitlement:

  • State Pension (basic and additional)
  • other pensions
  • most social security benefits eg Carer’s Allowance
  • £1 a week for every £500 (or part of £500) of capital (savings, investments) you have over £10,000
  • earnings

Income that can be ignored for Pension Credit

Some social security benefits don’t count as income, including the following:

  • Attendance Allowance
  • Christmas Bonus 
  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Housing Benefit
  • Council Tax Benefit

Effect on other benefits

Pension Credit doesn’t affect the amount of benefit you’re already getting.

If you qualify for Pension Credit, you may be eligible for extra benefits including:

  • Housing Benefit
  • Council Tax Benefit
  • Cold Weather Payment
  • Funeral Payments
  • Community Care Grant
  • Budgeting Loans
  • Crisis Loans
  • Winter Fuel Payment
  • Sure Start Maternity Grant
  • free school meals

Couples

You’re classed as a couple for working out Pension Credit if you live with your husband, wife or civil partner, or with someone as husband, wife or civil partner.

If your circumstances change

If your circumstances change eg your capital goes up or down, contact The Pension Service.

Pension Service helpline
Telephone: 0800 99 1234
Textphone: 0800 169 0133

If you’re unhappy with the decision

If you’re refused Pension Credit or think it’s been worked out incorrectly, ask the office that dealt with your claim to reconsider. If you’re still unhappy you can appeal to an independent Tribunal.

Self-assessment taxpayers

You must tell The Pension Service how much Income Tax you expect to pay for the current tax year - this affects how much Pension Credit you’ll get.

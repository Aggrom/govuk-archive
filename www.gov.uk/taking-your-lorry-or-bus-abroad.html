Lorries and buses: driving overseas for business

Licences, permits and documents you or your driver must have to use your vehicles when abroad for work - and how to apply for them.

Introduction

If you operate a lorry or bus abroad in connection with your business you will need a number of permits and licences. Your lorry or bus driver will need to keep many of these in the vehicle at all times during the journey abroad. You may be required to keep some documents at your operating centre.

Some permits and licences will allow travel in all EU countries but you may need special licences for certain loads or for travel in certain countries. This guide also directs you to information on laws that apply to drivers and vehicles travelling internationally.

Vehicle registration, licensing and insurance

When you take your lorry or bus abroad you will have to prove that you have registered, licensed and insured your vehicle correctly.

For information on the international vehicle registration, licensing and insurance documents that you will need to apply for see Vehicle documents required for international road haulage.

International authorisations, permits and licences

If you are operating a haulage vehicle abroad for hire or reward under contract you may need to apply for certain permits in order to cross borders or travel within certain countries. You may find different regulations apply to laden and empty vehicles and if you are making a trip for your own business (own account) rather than under contract. See International authorisations and permits for road haulage. 

Dangerous goods and special loads

The movement of dangerous goods by road is governed by international regulations which are strictly enforced. Most European countries are signatory to the European Agreement concerning the International Carriage of Dangerous Goods by Road (ADR).

This regulation will almost certainly require you to apply for additional licences and permits if you are carrying dangerous goods and certain special loads in your lorry. Depending on the nature of your business you may also be required to employ a Dangerous Goods Safety Adviser. For information on the extra permits that you and your vehicle driver may need, see Driving dangerous goods and special loads abroad.

Customs requirements and duties

Many of the documents that you need in order to take a commercial vehicle abroad will need to be shown to customs authorities at national borders. If you are carrying loads you will need to prove that the correct duties have been paid on the goods you are importing or exporting and that you are following rules on labelling, storing and packing. For information and access to documents that you may need when clearing customs in a commercial vehicle, see Customs procedures when transporting goods by road.

International requirements - vehicles

The increase in commercial traffic on the roads means there is a need to restrict the impact that large vehicles have on the environment. The regulations which apply to vehicle construction, size and emissions, for example, are strictly enforced. Incentives that have been introduced in some countries to encourage the use of cleaner vehicles could have an impact on the cost of your transport operations. See The essentials of international road haulage.

Claim a tax refund, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you think you’ve paid too much tax, how you claim a refund depends on your circumstances.

Sometimes HM Revenue & Customs will send you a refund straight away.

But if you’ve overpaid tax and started a new job, they’ll usually ask your employer to pay it back to you.

If you’ve paid too much tax on a pension, your pension provider may pay back the tax to you.

Part 2: Too much tax taken from your pay

You can claim a tax refund if you’ve overpaid in the current tax year or any of the previous 4 tax years.

How to claim

Call or write to HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) and explain why you think you’ve overpaid.

HMRC Income Tax enquiries

0845 300 0627
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

If you want to write to HMRC, tell them in your letter:

  • your National Insurance number
  • about the jobs you had or State benefits you were getting at the time

If you’ve got documents like your P45 or P60 from your employer, send those to HMRC too.

HMRC will write back to you. They will either:

  • send you a refund by cheque
  • tell you why you’re not due a refund

Part 3: You've stopped work

You were made redundant

If you’ve overpaid tax because of your redundancy payment, call HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC). They may be able to refund you before the end of the tax year.

HMRC Income Tax enquiries

0845 300 0627
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

You weren’t made redundant

You can’t get a tax refund straight away if you’re getting any of the following:

  • Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Carer’s Allowance
  • Incapacity Benefit, if you’ve been getting it for more than 28 weeks

Give the Jobcentre Plus parts 2 and 3 of your form P45. If you’ve overpaid tax, you may get a refund either:

  • at the end of the tax year
  • when you start a new job

If you’re out of work for less than 4 weeks, you’ll get a tax refund through your wages from your new job - you can’t claim one.

If you’re not claiming the benefits above and not going to work for at least 4 weeks (eg you’re retired, still looking for a job or returning to study) fill in form P50 and post it to HMRC.

Part 4: You get a pension

Too much tax taken from your pension

Call HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) and tell them why you think you’ve overpaid.

HMRC Income Tax enquiries

0845 300 0627
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

You’ve retired and only get the State Pension

Fill in form P50 and post it to HMRC.

You bought an annuity before April 2007

If you bought a retirement annuity before April 2007 and didn’t fill in a form to get the annuity payment tax-free, you may have paid too much tax on it.

To reclaim tax you’ve overpaid from April 2007 onwards, call HMRC.

HMRC Income Tax enquiries

0845 300 0627
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

You can’t reclaim tax you overpaid before April 2007.

You’ve taken pensions as lump sums

If you’ve taken more than 1 pension as a lump sum instead of a monthly payment (called ‘trivial commutation’), you may have paid too much tax.

If you fill in a Self Assessment tax return each year, you’ll get a refund when you’ve sent your return.

If you don’t, call HMRC and tell them - they will send you a form to claim a refund.

HMRC Income Tax enquiries

0845 300 0627
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

Part 5: You fill in a Self Assessment tax return

If you’ve made a mistake on your latest return and paid too much tax, you can correct it.

HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) will send you a refund.

If you’ve overpaid on an earlier tax year’s return, write to HMRC and explain why you think you’ve overpaid. You can claim back an overpayment up to 4 years after the end of the tax year you overpaid for.

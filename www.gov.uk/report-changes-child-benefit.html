Quick answer Report changes that affect your Child Benefit

You need to tell the Child Benefit Office as soon as possible about any changes to your circumstances that could affect your Child Benefit. If you don’t, your payments could be wrong.

Changes to your circumstances

Family changes

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if:

  • you marry, form a civil partnership or start living with a partner - and you’re both getting Child Benefit
  • you stop living with a partner and you’re both getting Child Benefit
  • you have a baby
  • another child comes to live with you
  • you start getting paid by a local council (or Health and Social Services Board or Trust in Northern Ireland) for a child you’re looking after

Bank account, name or address changes

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if:

  • you want to change the account your Child Benefit is paid into
  • you’ve changed your name or address

Contributions you make for a child change or stop

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if you stop paying money or making contributions for a child who doesn’t live with you - or what you pay or contribute changes.

Absence from the UK

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if:

  • you or your partner are due to leave the UK permanently or you’re likely to be away for more than 52 weeks
  • you’re due to leave the UK temporarily for more than 8 weeks, or more than 12 weeks because you or a member of your family is ill or has died
  • you lose your right to reside in the UK
  • your immigration status is changed by the Home Office

Other changes

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if:

  • you or your partner start to pay social security contributions to, or get social security benefit from, a foreign country
  • you leave England, Scotland or Wales to go to Northern Ireland permanently
  • you leave Northern Ireland to go to England, Scotland or Wales permanently

Changes to your child’s circumstances

Your child’s education or training

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if your child:

  • leaves school or college and starts paid work for 24 hours a week or more
  • leaves school or college earlier than the date you told the Child Benefit Office
  • stays on at school or college after the date you told the Child Benefit Office they would leave
  • starts education that’s been provided by an employer or as part of any other job or role they have
  • reduces the time they get supervised study at school or college to 12 hours a week or less
  • starts a higher education course leading to a qualification like a degree
  • starts being educated at home and is 16 or over
  • starts training that’s provided by their employer
  • starts a training course that’s not approved

Your child’s away from home for more than a week

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if your child:

  • has lived away from you for more than 56 days in any 16-week period
  • is expected to live away from you for more than 8 weeks
  • is expected to be in hospital or residential care for 12 weeks or more because they’re ill or disabled and you won’t be regularly spending money on them

Your child’s going abroad

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if your child is due to:

  • leave the UK permanently or they’re likely to be away for more than 52 weeks
  • leave the UK temporarily for more than 12 weeks
  • leave England, Scotland or Wales to go to Northern Ireland permanently
  • leave Northern Ireland to go to England, Scotland or Wales permanently

Other changes

Tell the Child Benefit Office straight away if your child:

  • changes their name
  • starts getting benefits in their own right
  • marries, forms a civil partnership or starts to live with a partner
  • is expected to be held in legal custody for more than 8 weeks
  • goes missing
  • is looked after by a local authority (in Northern Ireland, a Health and Social Services Board or Trust), a voluntary organisation, or held in secure or non-secure accommodation

How to report changes

You can do this:

  • online
  • by calling or writing to the Child Benefit Office

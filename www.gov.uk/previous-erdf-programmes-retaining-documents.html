Previous ERDF programmes: retaining documents

Guidance about retaining documents for previous European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) programmes.

Only applies to England

Contents

  1. Pre-2000 ERDF programmes
  2. 2000 to 2006 ERDF programmes
  3. Contact
  4. See more like this

Pre-2000 ERDF programmes

For projects which received grant from ERDF programmes before 1999 to 2000, applicants are advised that records for revenue-only projects can now be destroyed (given that the 3-year period for retaining records has now passed).

However, where the applicant has received funding for a capital project, records should be kept for the economic life of the project as outlined in the offer letter.

2000 to 2006 ERDF programmes

For projects that received revenue and/or capital grants from the 2000 to 2006 ERDF programmes, many of those programmes are still in the process of being closed and the document retention period has not yet been set.

Those programmes which have now been closed are shown below and the date for retention of documents for revenue only projects is shown. This list will be updated and extended as further programmes are closed.

  • East of England Objective 2: retain revenue documents until 31 January 2015
  • Peterborough Urban: retain revenue documents until 31 January 2014
  • Derby Urban: retain revenue documents until 31 December 2014
  • Stockwell Urban: retain revenue documents until 31 May 2014
  • Burnley Urban: retain revenue documents until 31 January 2014
  • South West Objective 2: retain revenue documents until 30 November 2014

In common with other programmes, records for capital projects should be kept for the economic life of the project as outlined in the offer letter.

Contact

For more information regarding document retention on ERDF programmes which operated in 2000 to 2006 or earlier please send your questions to the ERDF Closure Team via email at ERDF00-06Correspondance@communities.gsi.gov.uk.

Benefits & credits: Statutory Adoption Pay

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Statutory Adoption Pay is a weekly payment you get from your employer to help take time off work when you adopt a child.

Part 2: What you'll get

If your average weekly earnings are more than £107 (before tax) Statutory Adoption Pay is £135.45.

If you earn less than £107 a week before tax you’ll get 90% of your average weekly earnings.

Statutory Adoption Pay is payable for 39 weeks.

Your employer will pay Statutory Adoption Pay to you in the same way and at the same time as your normal wages.

Statutory Adoption Pay is treated as normal pay, so your employer will deduct tax and National Insurance as usual.

Part 3: Eligibility

To get Statutory Adoption Pay you must have been:

  • matched with a child for adoption by a UK adoption agency
  • employed by your present employer without a break for at least 26 weeks (up to and including the week the adoption agency told you that you had been matched with a child for adoption)
  • earning an average of at least £107 a week (before tax)

If you earn less than £107 a week before tax you’ll get 90% of your average weekly earnings

If you’re not eligible

Your employer must give you form SAP1 explaining why you can’t get Statutory Adoption Pay.

You may get support from your local council instead.

Download ‘Form SAP1, Non-payment of Statutory Adoption Pay (PDF, 30KB)’

If you disagree with your employer’s decision

If you think your employer’s decision is wrong or you think you’re not getting the right amount of Statutory Adoption Pay, contact HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC).

Part 4: How to claim

You must give your employer documentary evidence from the adoption agency. This will usually be a ‘matching certificate’.

You must also tell your employer when you want to take leave. This must be within 7 days of being told by the adoption agency that you’ve been matched with a child.

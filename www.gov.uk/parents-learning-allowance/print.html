Benefits & credits: Parents' Learning Allowance

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you’re a full-time student with children you may qualify for help with your learning costs on top of the standard student finance package.

How much you get depends on your household income.

Parents’ Learning Allowance can help with the costs of:

  • books
  • study materials
  • travel

It doesn’t have to be paid back.

Part 2: What you'll get

Depending on your household income, in 2012/13 you could get between:

  • £50-£1,508 a year

It’s usually paid in 3 instalments direct to your bank account, one at the start of each term.

Part 3: Eligibility

If you’re a student from England with dependent children you may qualify if you’re taking:

  • a full-time undergraduate course
  • an Initial Teacher Training (ITT) course

You don’t need to be paying for childcare to qualify.

Part 4: How to claim

You can apply for the Parents’ Learning Allowance when you apply for student finance.

Part 5: Further information

Help

Download Childcare Grant and other support for full-time student parents in higher education 2012/13 (PDF, 538KB)

After you apply

After you apply you’ll get a letter telling you how much you can get.

At the end of the academic year you’ll be asked to complete a Confirmation of Income form. This is to make sure you’ve been paid the right amount.

Benefits

Benefits and tax credits aren’t affected by Parents’ Learning Allowance.

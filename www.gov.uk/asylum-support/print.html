Benefits & credits: Asylum support

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You may be able get housing and money to support you and your family while you’re waiting to find out if you’ll be given asylum.

Your children will go to a free state school and you’ll get free healthcare from the National Health Service (NHS).

If you’ve been refused asylum, you can still apply for short-term support.

Part 2: What you'll get

Housing

You’ll be given somewhere to live, which could be in a flat, house, hostel or bed and breakfast.

You can’t choose where you live, and may have to live in:

  • East or West Midlands
  • North East England
  • North West England
  • Scotland
  • Wales

You won’t be able to live in London, and there isn’t much housing in South East England.

Cash support

You’ll be able to collect money from a local post office for things you need like food, clothing and toiletries.

Status Weekly payment
Married couple or couple in civil partnership £72.52
Lone parent aged 18 or over £43.94
Single person aged 18 or over £36.62
Aged 16-18 £39.80
Aged under 16 £52.96

Extra money for mothers and young children

If you’re pregnant or a mother of a child under 3, you’ll get extra money to buy healthy food:

Status Extra payment per week
Pregnant mother £3
Baby under 1 year old £5
Child aged 1-3 £3

Maternity payment

You can get a one-off £300 maternity payment if your baby is due in 8 weeks or less, or is under 6 weeks old.

Healthcare

You’ll get free National Health Service (NHS) healthcare, eg seeing a doctor or hospital treatment.

You’ll also get:

  • free prescriptions
  • free dental care
  • free sight tests
  • help paying for glasses

Education

Any children aged 5 to 16 must attend school. All state schools are free and your children may be able to get free school meals.

If you’ve been refused asylum

If you’ve been refused asylum, you can ask for:

  • short-term housing
  • help with prescriptions, dental care, sight tests and glasses
  • a payment card for food and toiletries

You won’t be given any cash.

Part 3: Eligibility

You can apply for asylum support if you’re homeless and don’t have money to buy food.

This is known as being ‘destitute’.

To qualify, you usually need to have applied for asylum shortly after you came to UK.

Part 4: How to claim

Housing and cash support

To get housing or cash support, you can speak to your case owner or apply online.

You can also fill in the form below:

Return the form to your local asylum support team.

If you’re refused asylum

If you’re refused asylum, you must return to your country of origin as soon as possible.

You can apply for short-term support using the form below.

Use the medical declaration if you can’t return because of a health reason.

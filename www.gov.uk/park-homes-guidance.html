Residential property tribunals

In April 2011, the government introduced a new system for dealing with disputes under the Mobile Homes Act 1983 if a resident and site owner can’t agree on a settlement. Since then, most types of disputes have been dealt with by residential property tribunals.

This download tells you about what residential property tribunals can deal with and how they handle applications made to them.

Consolidated implied terms in park home pitch agreements

Implied terms are contractual terms which are implied by law (Chapter 2 of Part 1 of Schedule 1 to the Mobile Homes Act 1983) into the pitch agreement between a resident and a park owner.

They are the minimum rights and obligations that all residents of park homes in England have. This download explains what these rights are.

The rights do not apply to agreements to occupy pitches on local council traveller sites.

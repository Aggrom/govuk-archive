Automatic enrolment into a pension at work, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: How auto-enrolment works

You’ll be automatically enrolled into a workplace pension between 2012 and 2018 if you:

  • don’t already have a workplace pension
  • earn more than £8,105 a year
  • are 22 or over
  • are under State Pension age
  • work (or usually work) in the UK
  • don’t opt out

When auto-enrolment starts

Your employer will tell you when auto-enrolment begins for your organisation.

Large employers start auto-enrolment in late 2012 and smaller organisations will follow between 2013 and 2018.

Your pension is protected by the government if your employer goes out of business - you should get any money you’re owed.

Part 2: How much auto-enrolment costs

You and your employer pay money each month into your pension. Most people will also get tax relief (money you would have paid as tax) from the government.

Use the Money Advice Service’s auto-enrolment calculator to work out how much you and your employer contribute to your pension.

Example

John earns £12,000 a year (£1,000 a month) so he’ll get the following towards his pension:

  • £30 a month from his employer (3% of John’s income)
  • £40 a month from his monthly pay (4% of John’s income)
  • £10 a month from the government in tax relief (1% of John’s income)

Although John puts in £40, the total contribution to his pension is £80 a month.

Part 3: Opting out of auto-enrolment

You don’t have to join your employer’s workplace pension scheme, and you can leave (‘opt out’) at any time. This won’t change under auto-enrolment, but if you do leave you won’t get the employer contribution or tax relief from the government.

To opt out, contact the people who run your employer’s pension scheme and tell them you want to leave. Your employer will provide you with their contact details.

If you do this within a month of joining the scheme, you’ll be treated as if you never joined in the first place and you’ll get any payments you’ve made back.

Opting back in

Contact your employer if you’ve left their pension scheme and want to rejoin.

Voting in the UK, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Before you can vote in UK elections or referendums you need to register to vote. Your name will then appear on the electoral register.

You can vote:

  • at a polling station
  • by post
  • by proxy (getting someone else to vote for you)

Who can vote in UK general elections?

You’re eligible to vote in the UK if you’re on the electoral register and:

  • aged 18 or over on polling day
  • a British citizen, Commonwealth citizen or a citizen of the Irish Republic
  • not legally excluded from voting (eg if you’re in prison)

You can’t vote in a UK general election if you are:

  • under 18
  • a member of the House of Lords
  • a European Union citizen (and not also a British, Irish or Commonwealth citizen)
  • a prisoner (apart from remand prisoners)
  • or have been found guilty of breaking election law in the last 5 years

Part 2: Polling stations

Your council will send you a poll card just before an election telling you where and when to vote.

Polling stations are open from 7am to 10pm on polling day, and are usually public buildings like schools or local halls.

When you get to the polling station

You can take your polling card with you to show who you are, but you don’t have to.

Give your name and address to the staff inside the polling station when you arrive.

You’ll be given a ballot paper containing a list of the people, parties or other options you can vote for.

Filling in your ballot paper

  1. Take your ballot paper into a polling booth.

  2. Follow the instructions on the notices in the polling booth and on the top of the ballot paper to vote.

  3. Fold your ballot paper in half and put it in the ballot box.

You can still vote even if you’ve lost your polling card.

If you haven’t received a poll card but think you should have done, then contact the Elections Office at your local council.

Part 3: Postal voting

You can vote by post if you live in the UK or abroad. Anyone can apply for a postal vote – you don’t need to give a reason. A postal vote can be sent to any address you choose.

You can apply to vote by post:

  • for a single election on a specific date
  • for a specific period
  • permanently

You can also apply by contacting your local electoral registration office.

To prove your identity, you’ll need to give your date of birth and signature when you apply and again whenever you vote by post.

When voting by post, you should:

  • complete your ballot paper in secret
  • complete the postal voting statement
  • put the ballot and statement in the envelope provided
  • seal the envelope yourself

Post your ballot back as quickly as possible to ensure it is counted.

Part 4: Voting by proxy

Voting by proxy means asking someone to vote on your behalf.

You can apply to vote by proxy:

  • for a single election on a specific date
  • for specific period
  • permanently

You can only apply for a proxy vote under certain circumstances, including:

  • being abroad on election day
  • having a disability
  • not being able to vote because of work reasons

You’ll need to give the reason for applying for a proxy vote when you apply.

In most cases, you’ll need someone to sign your application form to confirm your reasons for wanting a proxy vote. The application form will tell you who’ll need to sign it.

Part 5: Voting when you're abroad

You can vote in general elections and European parliamentary elections for up to 15 years after moving abroad.

If you’re temporarily abroad on election day you can vote by post or proxy.

You can apply to be an overseas voter if you:

  • are a British citizen living abroad
  • have registered to vote within the past 15 years

Overseas voters can vote by post or by proxy - or in person if you happen to be in the right place on polling day and aren’t voting by post or proxy.

Serving abroad in the Armed Forces

You can register as a service voter if you’re a member of the Armed Forces. If you’re the spouse or civil partner of a members of the Armed Services you can also register as a service voter.

You must renew your service declaration every 5 years.

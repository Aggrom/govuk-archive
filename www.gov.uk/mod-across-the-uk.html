Ministry of Defence across the UK

The MOD has dozens of major defence sites spread across the UK and provides jobs and work to many thousands of people.

Overview

The Ministry of Defence (MOD) is a truly national organisation with land and military bases spread right across the UK. It is also recruits regionally and provides work and contracts to many supplier organisations. The North West and South West regions have a particularly strong MOD presence.

North West

North West is one of the UK’s pre-eminent defence industrial areas and home to the Typhoon aircraft and the Astute submarine project. At least 17,000 jobs in the region - around 14% of the UK defence workforce - are directly linked to defence.

BAE Systems, Europe’s largest defence contractor, has a strong presence in the region and its Lancashire-based Military Air Solutions has supplied most of the country’s fixed-wing aircraft.

It is the home of the Typhoon fighter aircraft and more than 10,000 people work on this and other projects at the company’s Warton and Samlesbury sites.

Other important air programmes at BAE Systems include the F-35 Lightning II at Samlesbury and a series of unmanned air systems at Warton: HERTI (High Endurance Rapid Technology Insertion), Taranis and Mantis.

Barrow-in-Furness is the home of BAE Systems Submarine Solutions which is building the Astute class submarine and built the Landing Platform Dock HMS Albion and HMS Bulwark, as well as every nuclear powered submarine operated by the Royal Navy. Around 5,000 people are employed at the shipyard.

Cammell Laird in Birkenhead recently won MOD contracts to overhaul the Royal Fleet Auxiliary (RFA) ship Fort Rosalie, and to maintain the bulk of the RFA fleet.

North West is the base for around 2,000 regular and 3,600 Territorial Army soldiers, hosts dozens of Royal Navy ship visits every year and is home to several notable MOD agencies.

Army recruitment in the region is very strong: the service recruits around 15% of its soldiers from the region. The Duke of Lancaster’s Regiment, an infantry unit, recruits exclusively from across the region.

The cadet community is also large with 4,300 members of the Army Cadet Force, nearly 4,000 Air Cadets, more than 2,000 Sea Cadets and 3,300 members of the Combined Cadet Force.

Defence presence in the North West

Cheshire

Royal Navy assault ship HMS Albion is affiliated to the city of Chester.

The 1st Battalion The Mercian Regiment (Cheshire) recruits its soldiers from within the traditional Cheshire boundaries; the regiment has its area HQ at The Castle in Chester. The battalion is the successor to the 22nd (Cheshire) Regiment.

The region’s bomb disposal squad is Chester Troop from 521 Squadron, Dale Barracks, Chester. Part of the Royal Logistic Corps. The squadron deals with about 100 call-outs to unexploded ordnance across the region every year.

The 1st Battalion The Royal Welsh (The Royal Welsh Fusiliers), Dale Barracks, Chester, moved from their base in Cyprus in the summer of 2008.

75 Engineer Regiment, Warrington. TA unit.

The Mercian Regiment’s volunteer 4th Battalion has bases in Widnes and Crewe.

208 Field Hospital has a squadron in Ellesmere Port.

Cumbria

The patrol ship HMS Biter is affiliated to Silloth-on-Solway, while the patrol ship HMS Charger is affiliated to Maryport.

12 Regiment Royal Artillery - the Lancashire and Cumbrian Gunners - recruits in the county.

The 4th Battalion The Duke of Lancaster’s Regiment has Territorial Army centres in Carlisle, Workington and Barrow.

RAF (Royal Air Force) Spadeadam, north Cumbria. Electronic warfare tactics facility, where aircrews can practise tactics against a variety of threats. Used by the RAF, British Army, Royal Navy and NATO forces. The survival of aircrews over Iraq, Afghanistan or elsewhere, and the soldiers on the ground who depend on air support, are reliant on the training provided here.

Military low flying takes place over Cumbria and north Lancashire.

Warcop training area, east Cumbria. The region’s largest training area, it covers around 24,000 acres in an Area of Outstanding Natural Beauty.

Greater Manchester

Hunt class mine countermeasures ship HMS Middleton is linked to Middleton in the borough of Rochdale. The crew make frequent visits, travelling the length of the Manchester Ship Canal to berth in Salford quays.

The patrol vessel HMS Biter provides training for the Manchester and Salford Universities Royal Navy Unit.

RMR Merseyside, the region’s Royal Marines Reserves unit, has had a Manchester detachment for more than 50 years. The current detachment - known as Mandet - is based in Whalley Range, and many of its members specialise in heavy weapons.

The 4th Battalion The Duke of Lancaster’s Regiment’s has Territorial Army centres in Ardwick Green in Manchester and Bury.

The Royal Regiment of Fusiliers has its North West HQ in Bury and recruits in Bury, Bolton, Rochdale and Oldham.

The 3rd Regiment Royal Horse Artillery and the Scots Guards also recruit from Manchester.

207 Field Hospital, Stretford. TA unit. Saw service in Iraq in 2004.

The Lancashire Artillery (Volunteers) Band is based in Bolton.

TA soldiers of The Mercian Regiment’s 4th Battalion have a base in Stockport.

Other volunteer units with a presence in the county include 103 Regiment Royal Artillery (V) in Bolton, 75 Engineer Regiment in Failsworth and 156 (North West) Transport Regiment in Salford.

Manchester and Salford University Officer Training Corps, Manchester. Volunteer unit.

Holcombe Moor Camp and Training Area, near Bury. A 750 acre (30 hectare) general purpose training camp.

Lancashire

Royal Navy type 23 frigate HMS Lancaster is affiliated to the city of Lancaster.

42 (North West) Brigade, Preston. The headquarters of the Army in the region

The 2nd Battalion The Yorkshire Regiment (Green Howards), Weeton Barracks, Preston. Weeton Barracks is also the base of the only full-time Army band in the region, the Band of the King’s Division.

The 4th Battalion The Duke of Lancaster’s Regiment has Territorial Army centres in Preston, Lancaster, Blackburn and Blackpool.

The King’s Royal Hussars recruit in Lancashire and the regiment’s northern HQ is at Fulwood Barracks.

12 Regiment Royal Artillery recruits in the county and the regiment is popularly known as the Lancashire and Cumbrian Gunners.

Halton Camp and Training Area, near Lancaster. A 47 acre (19 hectare) site used by the regular Army, the TA and cadets.

The Service Personnel and Veterans Agency (SPVA), Norcross, Blackpool. Defence Agency. Provides ‘through-life’ support to around 900,000 serving personnel and veterans. This includes pay and human resources for the services, and pensions schemes and compensation schemes for those disabled or bereaved through service.

Merseyside

Liverpool hosts dozens of ship visits every year and the city is a favoured destination of even the largest ships.

Royal Naval Headquarters (RNHQ) Northern England and Isle of Man, Liverpool. Under the command of a commodore, the HQ handles Royal Navy affairs throughout the North West, North East, Yorkshire and Humberside.

RNHQ at Brunswick Dock, Toxteth. Hosts a number of units, including HMS Eaglet (Liverpool), the region’s Royal Naval Reserve unit. Eaglet is one of the largest such units in the country with around 200 members and regularly sends personnel to support operations in Iraq and Afghanistan.

Royal Marines Reserve Merseyside, Liverpool. Has around 200 members, most of whom have served in Iraq or Afghanistan in the last five years.

HMS Biter and Charger (Liverpool), Brunswick Dock. Fast patrol vessels providing maritime experience to students at Manchester, Salford and Liverpool universities.

Offshore patrol ship HMS Mersey is affiliated to the borough of Sefton.

The Royal Navy’s new aircraft carrier HMS Prince of Wales will be affiliated to the city of Liverpool, while the first-of-class attack submarine HMS Astute has formal links with the borough of Wirral.

The 4th Battalion The Duke of Lancaster’s Regiment has a Territorial Army centre in Norris Green, Liverpool.

The 1st Battalion Mercian Regiment, the 3rd Regiment Royal Horse Artillery, The Rifles and Irish Guards also recruit in Merseyside.

208 Field Hospital, Liverpool. Among the many Territorial Army units in Merseyside. Its volunteers saw service in Afghanistan in 2007.

RAF Woodvale, Formby. Home to the Liverpool University Air Squadron and the Manchester and Salford Universities Air Squadron.

The Reserve Forces and Cadets Association for the North West of England and the Isle of Man (NWRFCA), Liverpool. Owns Altcar training camp.

Altcar training camp. A 620 acre (251 hectare) site, with a series of firing ranges and nearly 500 bed spaces.

The Financial Management Shared Service Centre (formerly the Defence Bills Agency), Liverpool. Defence agency. Pays billions of pounds of invoices for the Ministry of Defence every year.

Isle of Man

The Royal Navy’s strategic missile submarine HMS Vigilant is affiliated to Douglas.

Sandown class minehunter HMS Ramsey has formal links with Ramsey.

103 Regiment Royal Artillery (V) is the affiliated TA unit of the Isle of Man.

North West Military Museums

King’s Own Royal Border Regiment Museum, Carlisle Castle.

Museum of the Manchesters, Ashton-under-Lyne.

King’s Own Royal Regiment Museum, Lancaster.

The Queen’s Lancashire Regiment Museum, Preston.

The Fusiliers Museum, Bury.

Cheshire Military Museum, The Castle, Chester.

Imperial War Museum North, Trafford Park, Greater Manchester.

Western Approaches Museum, Liverpool.

South West

Since the 16th century the South West has played a vital role in the defence of this nation. There are almost 42,000 service personnel and 22,000 MOD civil servants in the region, demonstrating that those historic links between the South West and defence remain as relevant as ever.

There are 31,000 full-time jobs in the South West directly funded through MOD contracts - the highest figure outside London and the South East.

Sites range from western Europe’s largest naval base to the military’s main UK training area - the iconic Salisbury Plain. The key RAF airfield at Lyneham in Wiltshire is the current home of the C-130 Hercules fleet, and all the UK’s defence equipment is bought and supported by the military/civilian project teams who have their headquarters at MOD Abbey Wood near Bristol.

With other sites at Corsham in Wiltshire and at Ensleigh in Bath, Defence Equipment & Support (DE&S) employs around 20,000 staff, more than half of them based in the SW - the biggest single customer of British industry.

South West is home to over 20% of the British Army including the headquarters of the Allied Rapid Reaction Corps, 3 (UK) Division, 1 Mechanised Brigade, 12 Mechanised Brigade, 43 (Wessex) Brigade, a number of combat and logistic support brigades, the Army Recruiting and Training Division.

South West is also home to 3 Commando Brigade and the bulk of the Royal Marines.

The region includes a wide range of Territorial Army and Reservist units including The Royal Wessex Yeomanry (headquartered in Bovington), 6th Battalion The Rifles (headquartered in Exeter), 155 Wessex Transport Regiment (headquartered in Plymouth), 39 Signals Regiment, 243 Field Hospital and a number of smaller supporting units. In addition, two University Officer Training Corps units are centred on Exeter and Bristol.

Defence presence in the South West

Wiltshire

Wiltshire is home to many of the Army’s main garrisons which are clustered around the Salisbury Plain military training area.

Salisbury Plain training area is the largest military training estate in the UK, covering 38,000 hectares. Its continued use for military training since the early parts of the 20th century has kept the site as ancient chalk grassland, and allowed the area’s selection as a Special Area of Conservation.

HQ 3 Division and 12 Mechanized Brigade, 1 Mechanized Brigade and 43 (Wessex) Brigade are based in the neighbouring towns of Bulford and Tidworth.

There are several other major Army units based at key sites across South Wiltshire including at Larkhill, Warminster and Upavon plus dozens of minor units situated in the area.

The Defence Support Group (DSG) site at Warminster provides essential maintenance and upgrades for armoured vehicles in support of current frontline operations.

The Defence Science and Technology Laboratory (DSTL) has their corporate headquarters at Porton Down in Wiltshire. An agency of the MOD, DSTL is the national centre of scientific and technological excellence with a workforce of 3,500 spread across three UK sites.

RAF Lyneham is base for the RAF’s 39 strong fleet of C-130 Hercules aircraft. Situated near the village of Wootton Bassett it employs over 2,500 military and civilians. It is also home to the headquarters of the RAF’s Tactical Medical Wing which provides medical aid to UK forces around the globe.

The RAF has an important presence at RAF Boscombe Down where the MOD carries out many of its aviation tests, trials and evaluations.

Some 2,700 military and civilian staff from DE&S are based at Corsham - most specialising in defence communications.

Gloucestershire

South Gloucestershire is home to the headquarters of DE&S at Abbey Wood - where around 8,500 military and civilian staff, out of a total of around 20,000 in the organisation, are responsible for managing a £13 billion per year spend - equipping and supporting the UK’s forces for both current and future operations with world class equipment.

The organisation supplies all combat and support equipment to British Forces on operations in Afghanistan and is responsible for all other equipment programmes, including aircraft carriers and nuclear submarines, armoured fighting vehicles and advanced missiles, small arms and ammunition, body armour and combat clothing and satellites and fighter and surveillance aircraft.

The 1st Battalion The Rifles are based at Beachley Barracks near Chepstow, Gloucestershire. Commando trained, they are part of the SW based 3 Commando Brigade.

In 2010, Imjin Barracks in Gloucester (formerly RAF Innsworth) became home to the Allied Rapid Reaction Corps (ARRC) - a deployable NATO HQ which relocated from Germany. Elements of the HQ were deployed to Afghanistan for the whole of 2011 to reinforce HQ ISAF (Intermediate Security Assistance Force) Joint Command for 12 months. HQ ARRC will assume the role of NRF LCC (Land Component Command) HQ (the NATO Response Force HQ) in January 2013 for one year.

The MOD’s Service Personnel and Veterans Agency (SPVA), provides a range of ‘through life’ support functions direct to around 900,000 serving and retired personnel and supports the 4.8 million strong Veterans Community. Services provided from it base at Imjin Barracks Gloucester are the MOD Medal Office and the Joint Casualty & Compassionate Centre (JCCC) which is manned 24-hours-a-day, seven-days-a-week.

The Joint Casualty and Compassionate Centre, part of the SPVA, provides a joint casualty reporting service for the Armed Forces. The centre, based in Gloucester, is manned 24-hours-a-day, seven-days-a-week.

The RAF site at RAF Fairford is home to the world famous Royal International Air Tattoo.

The major defence storage site at Ashchurch, maintains and then prepares for operations many of the Army’s fleet of land vehicles.

Devon

Devon is home to Devonport Naval Base. Some 5,000 military and civilian personnel work there with thousands more people employed in the dockyard privately run by Babcock. Vessels based at Devonport include the Royal Navy’s largest ship Ocean and the survey launch HMS Gleaner, which at 26-tonnes is the smallest vessel in service with the Navy.

It is estimated that the base generates about 10% of the income for Plymouth supplying business opportunities for hundreds of local firms. The Army also have an important footprint inside the city with 29 Commando (Royal Artillery) being based in Plymouth along with a number of Territorial Army units.

The majority of the Royal Marines, including 3 Commando Brigade Headquarters, are based in Devon. Over 6,000 Marines are based and train in the region, with sites in Plymouth, Turnchapel, Lympstone, Instow and Chivenor. RMB Chivenor is home to the Commando Logistics Regiment, and 24 Commando (Royal Engineers) and an RAF Search and Rescue unit.

Flag Officer Sea Training is based at Devonport and is responsible for the operational training of crews of all surface ships and submarines of the Royal Navy and Royal Fleet Auxiliaries.

All Royal Navy Officers do their basic training at the historic Britannia Royal Naval College in Dartmouth.

Many units also train on Dartmoor Training Area’s rugged terrain - an important training area for the UK’s military. 43 (Wessex) Brigade organise the annual Ten Tors challenge on the moor - an annual event which involves thousands of young people from across the region.

The Meteorological Office, part of the MOD, is one of the world’s leading providers of environmental and weather-related services and has its headquarters in Exeter. Around 1,100 jobs were created there when they relocated from Bracknell in 2004.

Somerset

Yeovilton is home to Royal Navy Lynx Helicopters and Royal Navy Commando Helicopter Force. The station is one of the busiest military airfields in the UK and among the largest in Europe, normally housing 7 squadrons and around 100 aircraft of varying types. It is manned by over 3,000 service and civilian personnel.

Based in Yeovil, AgustaWestland are a key contractor, with approximately 30% of their total business in 2007 coming from MOD. They are the prime contractor for the Wildcat (Future Lynx Helicopter) programme.

Norton Manor Camp just north of Taunton is home to 40 Commando Royal Marines who returned from their last tour of Helmand in late 2010.

Taunton is also home to the Hydrographic Office, part of the MOD. They have been expert nautical chart makers for hundreds of years and produce more than £50 million worth of charts every year.

Cornwall

HMS Seahawk at Royal Navy Air Squadron, Culdrose contains 11 squadrons, employing some 3,000 people and supports training and military operations across the globe.

It has over 80 aircraft with specialist search and rescue squadrons making over 200 rescue flights every year. In 2008, the search and rescue squadrons answered 204 callouts, assisting 169 people.

All Royal Navy ratings do their initial training at HMS Raleigh at Torpoint.

RAF St Mawgan in Cornwall provides first class support to a number of regular and cadet units including RAF Portreath and the Defence Survival, Evasion, Resistance and Extraction Organisation.

Dorset

Bovington is the home of the Royal Armoured Corps. Soldiers are trained on armoured vehicles here and on the ranges at Lulworth.

There is also a key Defence Support Group site at Bovington specialising in armoured vehicles.

Blandford Camp is the home of the Royal Signals, leaders in information technology and communications for the British Army. It also houses the Royal Signals museum, the national museum of Army communications.

Dorset is home to a number of key defence contractors, including BAE INSYST and Cobham in Wimborne.

Bath and Bristol

The cities host a range of units including the Royal Marines Reserves headquarters in Bristol, the headquarters of 243 Field Hospital (TA) in Keynsham, and the Wales and South West Naval Regional Headquarters at HMS Flying Fox in Bristol.

The MOD’s pay and personnel, financial management and equipment support organisations also have sites located across Bath.

Military units with strong South West connections

The Army recruits heavily in the South West. The local infantry regiment is The Rifles, which maintains a strong link to the region through one of its Territorial Army battalions, 6 Rifles.

The Navy has strong connections with affiliations including:

  • Bridport - Sandown class mine countermeasures vessel HMS Bridport.
  • Bristol - HMS Defender the fourth in class of the new Daring class of destroyer.
  • Cattistock, Dorset - Hunt class mine countermeasures vessel HMS Cattistock.
  • Cornwall - Type 22 frigate HMS Cornwall.
  • Exeter - Type 42 destroyer HMS Exeter.
  • Gloucester - Type 42 destroyer HMS Gloucester.
  • Somerset - Type 23 frigate HMS Somerset.
  • Taunton - Survey vessel HMS Echo.
  • Teignbridge and Newton Abbot, Devon - Trafalgar class submarine HMS Triumph.
  • Tiverton - Survey vessel HMS Enterprise.
  • Torbay - Trafalgar class submarine HMS Torbay.
  • Weymouth and Portland - Type 23 frigate HMS Portland.

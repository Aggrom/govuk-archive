Expenses and benefits: personal bills, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations if you cover the costs of your employees’ personal bills for goods or services.

What’s included

What you need to report and pay depends on:

  • who arranges for the supply of goods or services
  • who pays the supplier
  • if your employee pays and you pay them back

Part 2: What's exempt?

You don’t have to report anything to HMRC or pay tax and National Insurance for employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year if:

  • you arrange the goods and services
  • you pay the supplier directly

Part 3: What to report and pay

If the bills you pay aren’t exempt, you may have to report them to HMRC. You may have to deduct and pay tax and National Insurance on the costs.

You arrange the goods or services and pay the supplier directly

For employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year, or directors:

  • report on form P11D, section K
  • pay Class 1A National Insurance on the value of the benefit

Your employee arranges the goods or services, but you pay the supplier directly

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees at a rate of less than £8,500 per year Report on form P9D, section A(2) Add the full cost to earnings and deduct Class 1 National Insurance through payroll
Employees earning at a rate of more than £8,500 per year Report on form P11D, section B Add the full cost to earnings and deduct Class 1 National Insurance through payroll
Directors Report on form P11D, section B Add the full cost to earnings and deduct Class 1 National Insurance through payroll

Your employee arranges and pays the supplier, but you reimburse them

The amount you reimburse counts as earnings, so:

  • add it to your employee’s other earnings
  • deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance through payroll

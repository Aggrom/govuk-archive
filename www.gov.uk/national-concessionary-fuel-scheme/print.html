Benefits & credits: National Concessionary Fuel Scheme

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get free solid fuel or a cash allowance for fuel if you’re an ex-employee of the National Coal Board (NCB) or British Coal Corporation (BCC).

You need to qualify to get the fuel allowance through the National Concessionary Fuel Scheme, and you can only get the cash allowance if you’ve previously had fuel through the scheme.

You may be eligible for the allowance if you’re the widow of an ex-employee.

Part 2: What you'll get

Solid fuel

If you qualify for the allowance you’ll get a delivery of solid fuel every 4 or 5 weeks, depending on where you live.

You may be able to change to ‘cash in lieu’ (or a cash allowance) if you already get fuel through the scheme and your circumstances haven’t changed.

How you’re paid

The cash allowance is paid every 3 months into your bank, building society or Post Office account.

Change in circumstances

Your fuel allowance might stop if your circumstances change, eg the ownership of your house changes, or you move house

Part 3: Eligibility

Contact the National Concessionary Fuel Office (NCFO) to to check if you’re eligible - you usually are if you’re an ex-employee of the National Coal Board (NCB) or British Coal Corporation (BCC).

Widows of ex-employees may also qualify.

ncfo@capita.co.uk
0845 759 0529
Monday to Friday 8:45am to 5:15pm

Change of circumstances

Your allowance might be affected if your circumstances change, this includes if:

  • who owns or rents your property changes
  • you change your heating arrangements
  • you remarry, marry or move in with a partner (widows and dependents only)
  • who lives in your home changes (family or lodgers moving in)
  • you take up employment or self-employment

Part 4: How to claim

Contact the National Concessionary Fuel Office (NCFO) to apply.

Switching from fuel to cash allowance

Contact NCFO with your bank, building society or Post Office account details to switch from fuel to cash.

Applying on behalf of someone else

You can apply for the fuel allowance on behalf of someone else if they’re unable to. You’ll need authorisation.

Starting to export

Introduction

Strategic export control legislation does not stand still. Both UK and EU legislation can be updated and changed in the form of amending orders and regulation from time to time. These changes reflect the addition or removal of controls on specific goods or editorial corrections and drafting changes.

This guide provides a brief summary of amending legislation only. Amendments have been made to the Export Control Order 2008, the EU Dual-Use Regulation (Council Regulation (EC) No 428/2009, the EU Torture Regulation and the Radioactive Sources (Control) Order 2006 - which are all elements of strategic export control legislation.

Read the relevant guides about the export control order 2008, controls on dual-use goods, controls on torture goods and controls on radioactive sources.

A separate guide provides an overview of strategic export control legislation.

You should also be aware that the UK Strategic Export Control Lists are periodically amended and updated.

Amending orders

Amendments to Export Control Order 2008

The original legislation has been amended by the following amending orders.

Name of amending order Amendment details
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No 2) Order 2012 (SI 2012/1910) on the Legislation.gov website Implements Directive 2009/43/EC (Intra-Community Transfer (ICT) Directive), includes a new revised version of the UK Military List and introduces an export licence exception for historic military vehicles that are temporarily exported to Belgium, France or Germany (subject to meeting terms and conditions). Read more about the Intra-Community Transfer Directive 2009/43/EC
Read the full text of the Export Control Amendment Order 2012 (SI 2012/929) on the Legislation.gov website  Prohibits the export of human and veterinary medicinal products containing pancuronium bromide. The prohibition only applies to the export of these products where they are in a form suitable for injection or for preparation of an injection and the final destination is the United States of America. Revokes Export Control (Amendment) (No.3) Order 2011 which expired on 13 April 2012.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No.3) Order 2011 (SI 2011/1127) on the Legislation.gov website Revokes the Export Control (Amendment)(No 3) Order 2010 and further amends the Export Control Order 2008 to introduce new controls on the export to the USA of cetain specified human and veterinary medicinal products which can be used to execute prisoners on death row by lethal injection. The specified drugs are those that contain the following active ingredients: thiopental sodium, pancuronium bromide, potassium chloride and sodium pentobarbital.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No.2) Order 2011 (SI 2011/580) on the Legislation.gov website  Revokes the Export Control (Amendment) Order 2011. Inclusion of article 4B which specifies that subject to article 26, no person shall export unissued Libyan bank notes or unissued Libyan coins.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) Order 2011 (SI 2011/543) on the Legislation.gov website   Amendment related to Libyan bank notes
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No.3) Order 2010 (SI 2010/2843) on the Legislation.gov website.  Controls placed on exporting Sodium Thiopental to the United States of America (US), including where the destination is not the US but an exporter knows the final destination of the goods is the US.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No.2) Order 2010 (SI 2010/2007) on the Legislation.gov website.  Number of changes made to the UK’s military and national dual-use control lists and to the scope of the trade controls, as well as consolidating other drafting changes. As a result the existing strategic goods Control Lists in Schedules 1,2 and 3 of the 2008 Order have been replaced with new Schedules. Eritrea is made an “embargoed destination” following adoption of stricter United Nations and EU sanctions.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) Order 2010 (S.I. 2010/121) on the Legislation.gov website. Electro-statically powered explosive detectors are added to the list of dual-use goods in Schedule 3 to the 2008 Order which are goods subject to UK export control for strategic purposes. The newly controlled detectors will require a licence to be exported to any destination in Afghanistan or Iraq. Also clarifies that goods, software and technology listed in Schedule 3 require a licence to prohibited destinations even if going via a third country.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No.4) Order 2009 (SI 2009/2969) on the Legislation.gov website.  Change to make Guinea an embargoed destination
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No.3) Order 2009 (SI 2009/2151) on the Legislation.gov website. Removes overlap with controls listed in Council Regulation (EC) No.428/2009 (Recast Dual-Use Regulation). Provides, where necessary, implementing powers in respect of licensing, enforcement and penalties.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) (No.2) Order 2009 (SI 2009/1852) on the Legislation.gov website Clarifies the scope of the prohibitions on exportation of goods.
Read the full text of the Export Control (Amendment) Order 2009 (SI 2009 No.1305) on the Legislation.gov website. Replaces entry ML9 in Schedule 2. Extends trade controls in Category B in Part 2 of Schedule 1. Adds Angola and Namibia to the list of destinations subject to enhanced transit controls in Part 4 of Schedule 4. Corrects a misleading parenthetical explanation in article 35 of the 2008 Order.

Read more about the Export Control Order 2008.

Amendments to Council Regulation (EC) No 428/2009 (‘the re-cast Dual-Use Regulation’)

This Regulation is the main legal basis for controls on dual-use goods. It is directly applicable in all European Union (EU) countries, including the UK.

Download Council Regulation (EC) No.428/2009 from the Europa website (PDF, 173K).

The re-issued legislation entirely replaced the previous EU Dual-Use Regulation (Council Regulation (EC) No 1334/2000), as well as all its associated amendments.

This regulation has subsequently been amended:

  • by Council Regulation (EU) No 1232/2011 published on 8 December 2011 which issues new EU General Export Authorisations
  • by Council Regulation (EU) No 388/2012 published on 16 May 2012 and which came into force 30 days later on 15 June 2012. This amendment includes the revised version of the EU Dual-Use List

For more details (including links to the regulations) see the guides on controls on dual-use goods) and European Union General Export Authorisations  (EU GEAs).

Other amendments

The EU Torture Regulation (or Council Regulation (EC) No 1236/2005) was amended by Council Regulation (EU) No 1352/2011 on 20 December 2011 which came into force on 21 December 2011. This amendment includes new measures on drugs used for lethal injection purposes. Read more about controls on torture goods.

The Radioactive Sources (Control) Order 2006 came into force on 1 October 2006. This order was subsequently amended in 2009 by the Radioactive Sources (Control) (Amendment) Order 2009. Read more about controls on radioactive sources.

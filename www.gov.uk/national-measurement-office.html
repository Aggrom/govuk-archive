Introduction

The National Measurement Office (NMO) - previously known as the National Weights and Measures Laboratory (NWML) - regulates the development and use of weighing and measuring equipment to ensure that weights and measures are accurate, fair and legal.

To explain and enforce weights and measures regulations, the NMO works with local authorities’ trading standards departments and inspectors, local weights and measures authorities and approved verifiers of weights and measures equipment.

The NMO’s consulting services help small and medium-sized enterprises (SMEs) to develop specific measuring products, processes or services. It also helps SMEs to understand the regulations covering their weighing and measuring instruments and how to comply with the regulations.

The NMO still provides services under the name of NWML including product certification, quality management systems certification and calibration services to several sectors of industry and local government, all or most of which have some element of weighing or measuring either in their manufacturing processes or in their work.

The NMO works with WELMEC (the European co-operation in legal metrology) and OIML (the International Organisation of Legal Metrology) to maintain UK consumer and business confidence in legal measurement abroad. It also takes part in preparing OIML recommendations and represents the UK at the OIML.

This guide also outlines the work of the NMO Enforcement Authority, the part of the NMO responsible for enforcement work.

The work of the National Measurement Office

The National Measurement Office (NMO) - previously known as the National Weights and Measures Laboratory (NWML) - is an executive agency of the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills.

NMO is responsible for:

  • policy on measuring instruments that are used in trade - eg preparing legislation under the Weights and Measures Act 1985 and European Communities Act 1972, and proposals for reforming Weights and Measures Act
  • ensuring that the UK implements European directives on measuring instruments
  • legal metrology - ie requirements for legal verification of measuring instruments - in the UK

Legal metrology makes it possible to buy and sell accurate quantities of goods. Without accurate measurements, there would be no possibility of a fair marketplace.

The NMO helps ensure the accuracy of the majority of measuring instruments used for trade and pre-packaged goods in the UK - including pre-packaged goods in supermarkets, check-out scales, petrol pumps and beer glasses.

The NMO provides guidance to industry and the public, and enforces UK and European Union weights and measures legislation. Key regulations include:

  • the Weights and Measures Act 1985 and secondary legislation, including the Weights and Measures (Packaged Goods) Regulations 2006
  • the Deregulation (Weights and Measures) Order 1999
  • the Measuring Instruments Directive 2004/22/EC and the Non-Automatic Weighing Instruments Regulations

Within the NMO, the National Measurement System (NMS) unit is responsible for scientific and industrial metrology. Scientific metrology deals with the organisation, development and maintenance of measurement standards. Industrial metrology ensures the adequate functioning of measurement instruments used in industry, production and testing processes. The NMS carries out its tasks in a UK network of measurement laboratories, which include the National Physical Laboratory.

Read information on the National Measurement system on the NMO website.

NWML Services

The NWML Calibration Team, approved under the UK Accreditation Service maintains standards for mass, volume and length measurement. In addition to calibration, NWML provides testing, product and QMS certification services for industry and local authorities - eg local authority Trading Standards. For more information, see the page in this guide on product certification.

It also provides a consultancy service to manufacturers that are developing new weighing and measuring equipment. For more information, you can also see the page in this guide on consultancy and consulting services.

Businesses should be aware that NWML Certification Services is changing its name to NMO Certification Services as of 1 April 2011.

Access the industry service forms offered from the NWML.

NMO Enforcement Authority

In recent years, the role of the National Measurement Office (NMO) has expanded to include the enforcement of regulations aimed at protecting the environment.

The NMO Enforcement Authority, part of the NMO, is responsible for enforcing environmental legislation under the Restriction of the Use of Certain Hazardous Substances in Electrical and Electronic Equipment Regulations 2008 (RoHS Regulations) which limits the levels of certain potentially hazardous substances in new electric and electronic equipment for sale in the European Union (EU). Find out about RoHS on the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) website.

The NMO Enforcement Authority also acts as the UK Batteries Regulations enforcement body, to ensure compliance with the Batteries and Accumulators (Placing on the Market) Regulations 2008. This limits the levels of cadmium and mercury in new batteries for sale within the EU, controls the marking of batteries and sets design requirements for battery-operated electrical and electronic equipment.

Find out about the Batteries Regulations on the BIS website.

It is also the Market Surveillance Authority (MSA), under the EU Energy Using Products (EuP) and Energy Labelling Framework (ELF) Directives. This means the NMO ensures that manufactures meet their product claims under energy labelling - eg A-rated fridges - and minimum energy standards.

Find out about EuP on the BIS website.

Find out about ELF on the Energy Labelling website.

NMO enforcement partners

NMO works with various other public and private sector organisations that help to enforce weights and measures regulations.

At the local level, the NMO works with the Local Government Regulation (formerly the Local Authorities Coordinators of Regulatory Services), the local government central body responsible for overseeing local authority regulatory and related services in the UK, the Trading Standards Institute and regional groups of local authorities’trading standards departments and inspectors.

Notified bodies are local weights and measurements authorities (LWMA) that assess whether weighing and measuring equipment meets CE marking requirements. CE marking is a manufacturer’s declaration that the product complies with the standards set out in relevant European directives.

Find a local notified body to evaluate your weighing/measuring equipment with the NMO.

Approved verifiers have permission from the Secretary of State to test, pass, stamp and verify weighing and measuring equipment that they manufacture, install or repair. Approval may be for non-automatic weighing instruments or for measuring instruments that come under the EU Measuring Instruments Directive. A condition of approval is that the verifier operates an acceptable quality system and receives regular audits and inspections from both the LWMA and the NMO.

Apply to become an approved verifer with the NMO.

Legal and regulatory activities

The National Measurement Office (NMO) works with trading standards departments in local authorities to deliver enforcement of most of the UK’s weights and measures legislation.

Weights and measurements regulations help ensure consumers have accurate information about their purchases. This protects them from being sold short measures. The regulations also protect businesses against dishonest competitors.

You can find weights and measures legislation guidance notes on the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) website.

Search for legislation on weights, measures and hallmarking which may affect your business with the NMO.

Hallmarking

The NMO is responsible for the sponsorship of the British Hallmarking Council. This involves the testing and marking of items of precious metal to indicate they conform to legal standards of purity (fineness), across four assay offices in the UK (London, Birmingham, Sheffield and Edinburgh).

Water, gas and electricity metering

The NMO also regulates the metrology of water, gas and electricity meters.

Better regulation

The NMO complies with Statutory Code of Practice for Regulators (the Regulators’ Compliance Code). This requires regulators to plan regulation and inspections in a way that causes the least disruption to businesses, to impose sanctions and penalties in a transparent and proportionate way and to report annually on their enforcement activities.

You can download Regulators’ Compliance Code from the BIS website (PDF, 107K).

Consultancy and consulting services

The National Measurement Office (NMO) offers individually tailored consultancies for UK and international organisations, providing a range of highly skilled engineers and a network of specialist collaborators covering the whole range of legal metrology.

It carries out consultations on weights and measures policy and regulatory issues. It has signed up to the government’s Code of Practice on Consultation. This outlines best practice in running public consultation exercises.

You can download the Code of Practice on Consultation from the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) website (PDF, 223K).

You can find out about previous consultancy projects on the NMO website.

Measurement for Innovators scheme

The Measurement for Innovators (MfI) scheme is a BIS programme helping small and medium-sized enterprises to develop and improve new weighing and measuring products, processes and services.

Find MfI information on the NMO website.

Product certification

As part of the National Measurement Office (NMO), NMO Certification Services provides product certification services to businesses and public sector organisations in the UK and abroad.

NMO Certification Services is a notified body under European directives relating to Non-Automatic Weighing Instruments (NAWI) 2009/23/EC and Measuring Instruments (MID) 2004/22/EC. As a notified body, it can issue:

  • European Commission (EC) Type Approval Certificates (TACs) for weighing and measuring instruments
  • a range of the International Organisation of Legal Metrology (OIML) certificates of conformity - which bring instrument approval in many countries
  • UK National Approvals - under section 12 of the Weights & Measures Act 1985

NMO’s product certificates cover a wide range of weighing and measuring equipment. If you are unsure whether your product will pass the certification process, you can submit your instruments to NMO’s pre-assessment service.

You can apply for pre-assessment with the online industry service forms from the NMO.

To find out about notified bodies, see the page in this guide on legal and regulatory activities.

NAWIs

NMO can issue European TACs for all electronic and most mechanical NAWIs. These are valid throughout Europe. If you want to sell your NAWI outside Europe, NMO can approve it in accordance with the relevant OIML recommendations. In this case, NMO issues an OIML Certificate of Conformity and test report. These are valid in countries which are signed up to the OIML mutual acceptance arrangements (MAAs).

If you want to submit your NAWI for NMO certification processes, you will need to:

  • fill in an application form - which you can do online
  • forward your supporting documentation
  • send in your equipment for testing

To simplify the process, NMO can test separate modules of a NAWI and issue individual Test Certificates (TCs). You can quote these TCs in a TAC. This modular approach is useful for indicators, load cells and point of sale devices, and peripheral devices, such as computers and printers.

You can apply for product certification with the online industry service forms from the NMO.

Measuring instruments covered by MID include:

  • automatic weighing instruments
  • fuel dispensers
  • material measures
  • water meters

For a period of 10 years from 30 October 2006, existing European Economic Community and UK National TACs will remain valid for instruments that now come under the MID Directive. You can contact the NMO to find out how to convert an existing TAC to an MID approval, as well as applying for approval for a new instrument under MID. However, you should note that the NMO is not a notified body for gas and electricity meters.

Temperature and humidity testing equipment

NMO can test and certify temperature testing equipment from 55°C to -25°C, and humidity testing equipment up to 95 per cent relative humidity. You can access the NMO’s environmental test chambers for research or pre-compliance testing.

UK National Type approvals

All measuring instruments that are used for trade purposes in the UK require a UK National Type Approval certificate. These include measuring instruments for:

  • intoxicating liquor
  • dynamic axle weighers - automatic vehicle weighbridges
  • cubic measures

You can apply to the NMO for product certification or for pre-assessment.

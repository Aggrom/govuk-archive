Fish and shellfish farm authorisation and registration

How to apply for legal authorisation from the Fish Health Inspectorate to run a fish, shellfish or crustacean farm, and your legal responsibilities.

Introduction

All fish, shellfish and crustacean farms are legally required to be authorised by the Fish Health Inspectorate (FHI) under the Aquatic Animal Health (England and Wales) Regulations 2009. Before setting up a new fish, shellfish or crustacean farm, or before starting any related development, you must apply to the FHI to have it authorised. Once you have met all of the FHI’s requirements and have an approved and documented biosecurity measures plan in place, you will be issued with a certificate of authorisation, and be able to stock and trade from your farm.

There are conditions of authorisation that your farm will have to observe, such as keeping records of all movements in the prescribed format and following good hygiene practice. The FHI can suspend or revoke your authorisation if they believe you aren’t complying with the conditions of your authorisation.

This guide explains how to apply for authorisation for your farm. It also sets out your legal responsibilities and tells you how to make changes to your authorisation if necessary.

Fish and shellfish farm authorisation

Under the Aquatic Animal Health (England and Wales) Regulations 2009, all fish, shellfish and crustacean farms - known as aquaculture production businesses - must be authorised by the FHI.

How to apply for authorisation

Before setting up a new fish farm or beginning any development, you must apply for authorisation. To apply for authorisation, complete form AW1 and return it to the FHI.

If your application is for a new farm, you should only fill in sections 1, 4 and 5.

Some of the details that you provide about your farm will form part of your authorisation. This includes the:

  • species farmed
  • number and type of holding facilities
  • Ordnance Survey grid reference showing the location of your farm

So it is very important that you include all of these when you fill in section 4 of form AW1. Download FHI form AW1 to apply for authorisation from the Aquatic Animal Health and Movements (AAHM) website (PDF, 65KB).

What happens after you apply

Once the FHI has received your application form, they will confirm they have received it and inform you that an inspector will visit your site. They will also give you guidance on developing a biosecurity measures plan (BMP). The FHI will consult with other government bodies regarding the species you’re intending to farm and the facilities that will be developed and included in the authorisation.

The fish health inspector who is responsible for the area where your farm is situated will contact you directly to make arrangements to visit. During the visit the inspector will discuss and document your farm.

Following the visit the inspector will pass their report and the data they collect to an authorisation officer. The authorisation officer will act on their recommendations, which will result in one of the following:

  • authorisation being granted because your site is complete and has an approved BMP in place
  • authorisation in principle being granted: This may be because your site is complete but your draft BMP has not yet been submitted for approval. Once this happens authorisation will be granted. Or it may be because yours is a new site in the ‘paper’ planning stage. Once a consultation with other government bodies has taken place and the FHI has approved your plans, your site will be granted authorisation in principle. Full authorisation will only be granted after a full site and BMP assessment
  • authorisation is not granted: It may be that it is more appropriate to register your site as a fishery or aquatic animal holding - see the page in this guide on registering a fishery - or it may be that your proposed plans or species are not permitted under current legislation

Your application for authorisation will be handled according to FHI standards of customer service. For guidance on what standards to expect when you apply for authorisation, download the FHI citizen’s charter from the AAHM website (PDF, 51KB).

What happens if your application is unsuccessful

If the FHI turns down your application they will tell you the reasons why, and how you can appeal against their decision.

Requirements for authorised fish and shellfish farms

Fish, shellfish and crustacean farms need to be authorised to prevent infectious diseases being introduced and spread within the UK.

Therefore, as a condition of authorisation you must:

  • keep movement records in the prescribed format
  • follow good hygiene practice
  • comply with any surveillance requirement requested by the Fish Health Inspectorate (FHI)
  • comply with the procedures specified in your site’s approved biosecurity measures plan

Details of your farm will be kept in a public register of authorised businesses maintained by the FHI.

Record keeping requirements

Owners and operators of authorised fish, shellfish and crustacean farms are required to keep certain records in a format specified by the FHI. The records that you must keep are:

  • details of movements of any aquaculture animal, or aquaculture animal product, into or out of your premises - see the guide on moving fish and shellfish
  • the number of any aquaculture animals that have died in each of your units
  • the results of any surveillance you carry out
  • the results of any surveillance carried out by the FHI which you are notified about

Changes to an existing authorisation

If you are already operating an existing aquaculture production business (APB) there may be certain occasions when your authorisation needs to be changed. This might be because you need to make changes to the way your farm operates, such as the species farmed or the facilities used.

How to apply to make changes to your details

You must tell the FHI in advance if:

  • the owner or operator of the farm changes
  • the farm is no longer operational and your authorisation needs to end
  • the species being farmed changes
  • the number of tanks, ponds or other holding facilities changes

To apply to make the required changes, you must complete Section 2 or 3 of form AW1 as appropriate and send it to the FHI. Download FHI form AW1 to make changes to your authorisation details from the Aquatic Animal Health and Movements (AAHM) website (PDF, 65KB).

FHI enforcement of non-compliance by an APB

The FHI will, in the first instance, issue an enforcement notice to sites that have not complied with a condition of authorisation and will agree the appropriate follow up action. For continued non-compliance the FHI can suspend or revoke an authorisation if they believe that a farm has not complied with either the conditions of authorisation or another legal requirement. The FHI will send a written notice of suspension or revocation with details of:

  • the reason for the suspension or revocation
  • the date that the suspension or revocation starts, and its duration if they are issuing a suspension
  • your right to appeal

How to register a fishery

Under the Aquatic Animal Health (England and Wales) Regulations 2009, all stocked and cropped waters - apart from rivers and canals - must be registered with the FHI. Until your fishery or cropping water is registered, you will not get consent from the Environment Agency to stock your waters or remove fish from them. See the guide on moving fish and shellfish.

Registration requirements

The process of registering a fishery requires a single named person to take responsibility for the waters. This could be the owner, lease holder, operator or manager. The person responsible must:

  • give the FHI all reasonable help and access in the event of fish mortality
  • report any suspicion of a notifiable disease

How to apply to register your waters

There are two types of registration:

  • fisheries registration
  • aquatic animal holdings registration (cropping waters)

To register a stocked and cropped fishery, you must use form RW1. Download FHI form RW1 to register your fishery from the AAHM website (PDF, 184KB).

To register an aquatic animal holding, you must use form RW2. Download FHI form RW2 to register your aquatic animal holding from the AAHM website (PDF, 98KB). Registration of aquatic animal holdings is the minimum required under the legislation and applies only to low risk cropping and harvesting waters, not fisheries.

When you fill in the application form, you must include a map of your waters. You must use one application form for each complex of waters.

Once the FHI has received your application, they will register your waters and provide you with your registration number on a registration document.

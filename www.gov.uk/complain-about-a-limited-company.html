Quick answer Complain about a limited company

You can complain to the Insolvency Service if you suspect a limited company or its directors of fraud or serious misconduct.

If the Insolvency Service decides that your complaint is serious enough, it will carry out a confidential investigation.

If they find wrongdoing and have enough evidence, they may ask a court to:

  • close the company down
  • disqualify the company’s directors

What can be investigated

The Insolvency Service must have reasonable grounds to suspect a company of:

  • causing significant harm to customers, suppliers etc
  • breaking the law, eg fraud
  • serious misconduct
  • having a significant irregularity in its affairs

What can’t be investigated

The Insolvency Service won’t usually investigate complaints where:

  • there’s no public interest (eg an internal company dispute)
  • an investigation by somebody else is already underway
  • the main issue is that the company not paying its debts

How to complain

Use the Insolvency Service’s Investigations Hotline.

You can also write to them:

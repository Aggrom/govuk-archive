Benefits & credits: Paternity pay

Notes

Part 1: Overview

When your wife, partner or civil partner gives birth or adopts a child, you could get Ordinary Statutory Paternity Pay (OSPP).

The most OSPP you can get is £135.45. It’s paid for 1 week or 2 consecutive weeks, depending on how much leave you take.

Extra pay

You could also get Additional Statutory Paternity Pay (ASPP) if your partner’s returning to work. The weekly rate is the same as OSPP but can be paid for a longer period of time.

Part 2: What you'll get

The weekly rate of Ordinary Statutory Paternity Pay (OSPP) and Additional Statutory Paternity Pay (ASPP) is £135.45, or 90% of your average weekly earnings - whichever is lower.

OSPP is paid for 1 week or 2 consecutive weeks, depending on how much leave you take.

ASPP is paid during the period of your partner’s 39-week Maternity Allowance, Statutory Maternity or Statutory Adoption Pay period.

You’ll only get OSPP or ASPP if your average weekly earnings are £107 (or more) before tax.

How you’re paid

OSPP and ASPP are paid by your employer in the same way and at the same time as your normal wages. Tax and National Insurance will be deducted.

Part 3: Eligibility

To get Ordinary Statutory Paternity Pay (OSPP) or Additional Statutory Paternity Pay (ASPP) you must be 1 of the following:

  • the biological father of the child
  • the mother’s husband or partner (including same-sex relationships)
  • the child’s adopter
  • the husband or partner (including same-sex relationships) of the child’s adopter

You must have worked for your employer for at least 26 weeks without a break by either:

  • the end of the 15th week before the start of the week when your baby is due
  • the end of the week you’re told you’re matched with your adopted child
  • the date your child enters the UK (if you’re adopting from overseas)

You must also:

  • be earning an average of at least £107 a week (before tax)
  • continue to work for that employer without a break up to the date the child is born, placed for adoption or enters the UK (overseas adoptions only)

Additional Statutory Paternity Pay

To get ASPP, you must be taking time off (Additional Paternity Leave) to care for the child. The child’s mother or adopter must have been entitled to 1 or more of the following:

  • Statutory Maternity Leave
  • Statutory Maternity Pay
  • Maternity Allowance
  • Statutory Adoption Leave or Pay

The child’s mother or adopter must have started working again so that any relevant pay has stopped.

Additional Statutory Paternity Pay is only payable to you during the period of your partner’s 39-week Maternity Allowance, Statutory Maternity or Statutory Adoption Pay period.

Part 4: How to claim

Ordinary Statutory Paternity Pay (OSPP)

To claim, you must tell your employer when you want to take leave. You must do this:

  • by the 15th week before your baby is due
  • within 7 days of your wife, partner or civil partner being told by the adoption agency that they’ve been matched with a child

Your employer might ask you to do this using form SC3.

Download ‘Form SC3, notice for Ordinary Statutory Paternity Pay and Leave’ (PDF, 100KB)

If you change your mind you must give your employer at least 28 days’ notice.

Additional Statutory Paternity Pay (ASPP)

To claim, you must tell your employer at least 8 weeks before you want to take your additional leave. To do this, use the forms below (or your employer’s version).

Download ‘Form SC7 - for births’ (PDF, 46KB)

Download ‘Form SC8 - for UK adoptions’ (PDF, 45KB)

Download ‘Form SC9 - for overseas adoptions’ (PDF, 45KB)

Your employer may ask you for:

  • a copy of your child’s birth certificate or adoption matching certificate
  • the employment details of the mother or adopter

You’ll need to provide this information within 28 days.

If you change your mind about when you want to claim ASPP you must give your employer at least 6 weeks’ notice.

Part 5: Further information

If your employer refuses to pay you Ordinary Statutory Paternity Pay (OSPP) or Additional Statutory Paternity Pay (ASPP) they must give you a form explaining why.

Download ‘Form OSPP1, Non-payment of paternity pay’ (PDF, 100KB)

Download Form ASPP1 - non-payment of Additional Paternity Pay (PDF, 45KB)

Disputes

If you’re unhappy with your employer’s decision or think the pay you’re getting is wrong contact the HM Revenue and Customs (HMRC) employees’ enquiry line.

Part 5: Driving with disabilities

Blue Badge scheme

The Blue Badge scheme helps disabled drivers park closer to their destinations.

Find out more information about the Blue Badge scheme from your local council.

Apply for a Blue Badge.

Tax disc exemptions

You may be entitled to a free tax disc if you’re disabled.

Getting your first free tax disc

If you have a new vehicle take your exemption certificate and valid insurance certificate to the dealership where you bought the vehicle.

With a used vehicle you can change to a free tax disc at a Post Office branch.

Download the leaflet for information on how to apply for your free tax disc for the first time

Find your nearest Post Office that issues tax discs - choose ‘driving licences’ from the drop down menu. Or you can telephone 0845 722 3344 giving your postcode.

Renewing your free tax disc

Renewing your free tax disc can be done online or by phone.

Renew your tax disc online or by phone.

Refunds on your current tax disc

Once you’ve got your disabled tax disc, you can apply for a refund if you’ve got any complete months remaining on your old tax disc.

Motability scheme

The Motability scheme is run by the independent charity Motability. It gives disabled people the chance to lease a car at an affordable price.

To find out more about the scheme contact Motability directly.

Motability
Telephone: 0845 456 4566
Textphone: 0845 675 0009
www.motability.co.uk

Apply for a dropped kerb

A ‘dropped kerb’ may make it easier to get from your car to your house. It involves lowering the kerbstones and making a small ramp from the road to your driveway.

You can apply for a dropped kerb outside your home if you live in England.

Part 8: Getting your driving licence back after disqualification

If you’ve been disqualified for 56 days or more you need a new licence before driving again.

You can apply to renew your licence up to 56 days before your disqualification ends.

Renewal reminder

You’ll be sent a D27P ‘Driving licence application for renewal after disqualification’ form 56 days before your disqualification ends.

If you have a non-Great Britain licence you’ll get your old licence back on the date your disqualification ends.

Renewing your licence if you’ve got a photocard driving licence

You’ll need to:

  • complete your D27P form

  • enclose the correct payment (this will be shown on the reminder)

  • send your application to DVLA, Swansea, SA99 1AB

If your name has changed you’ll also need to include:

  • a completed D1 form, available from the DVLA form ordering service or selected Post Offices

  • original documents showing your new name

Renewing your licence if you’ve got a paper driving licence

You’ll need to:

  • complete your D27P form

  • send original documents confirming your identity

  • include a passport-type photo

  • enclose the correct payment (this will be shown on the reminder)

  • send all your documents to DVLA, Swansea, SA99 1AB or use the premium checking service at selected Post Offices or DVLA local offices

Renewing without a D27P form

If you don’t get the D27P reminder form, order a D1 ‘application for a driving licence’ form from the DVLA form ordering service or get one from selected Post Offices.

Send the completed form, documentation and fee to DVLA, Swansea, SA99 1AB.

If you need to renew your lorry or bus entitlement order a D2 ‘application for Lorry, Bus or Minibus driving licence’ form from the DVLA form ordering service or get the form from a DVLA local office. You don’t need to complete a D1 form.

When to expect your new driving licence

DVLA aims to deliver your driving licence within 3 weeks of getting your application. Wait at least 3 weeks before contacting DVLA.

It’ll take longer if your medical or personal details have to be checked, eg if you were disqualified for certain alcohol-related offences.

Set up an MOT test station, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you want to set up an MOT test station, you’ll have to meet a number of requirements.

You’ll need:

  • Authorised Examiner (AE) status
  • approved MOT equipment
  • suitable premises

The equipment and premises you’ll need will depend on the vehicle classes you want to test.

Part 2: Your application

Authorised Examiner (AE status)

You must have AE status to set up an MOT test station.

An AE can be an individual, partnership or company authorised by the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA).

The AE is responsible for making sure that:

  • the test facilities and equipment are checked and well-maintained
  • the Nominated Testers are assessed correctly and complete training and quality checks
  • VOSA staff have access to the premises for checks on staff and equipment
  • MOT documents are correctly stored and access to electronic MOT test systems is only given to eligible users
  • MOT tests are properly conducted
  • VOSA is informed about significant changes to the business within 7 working days

There’s more information about the AE’s responsibilities in VOSA’s guide to becoming an AE.

Download ‘So you want to become an Authorised Examiner?’ (PDF, 361KB)

To apply for AE status, send form VT01 to VOSA. Use the same form if you already have AE status and want to open a test station. The guide to requirements for authorisation of AEs tells you more about the application process.

Download ‘Form VT01 - application for AE status / opening an MOT test station’ (PDF, 274KB)

Download ‘Requirements for authorisation of AEs’ (PDF, 114KB)

Send the form to your local VOSA office.

If your application is refused, VOSA will write to you - you can appeal the decision and ask for a hearing by writing to VOSA within 14 days.

AE status doesn’t transfer with a business. If you’re buying an existing MOT station, you need to apply for AE status in your own right.

Changes to your company

You may need to apply for AE status again if:

  • your company is reconstructed in a way that means it’s given a new company registration number
  • a partner leaves or joins a partnership

Premises and equipment

Different classes of vehicle require different test equipment and testing bay sizes. You’ll need to make sure you have at least the minimum level of acceptable equipment for each vehicle class you’re approved to test.

Download ‘Requirements for authorisation for a vehicle testing station’ (PDF, 47KB)

You can ask for your premises to be approved in principle when applying for AE status. This can help you avoid committing to expensive work or alterations before your premises are approved.

If you’ve got AE status but want to make changes to the test facilities, write to VOSA before any changes are made, with supporting drawings, to show that the changes won’t affect the testing station’s approval.

Part 3: Vehicle classes

When deciding which class of vehicle you’d like to test, you’ll need to consider:

  • the equipment needed to test the vehicles
  • the size and layout of your premises
  • whether the Nominated Tester is approved to test the different vehicle types you want to MOT

Part 4: Meeting ongoing standards

The Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) will carry out regular risk-based site assessments of your station to make sure you continue to meet the standards.

This will involve checking it’s well maintained and that it offers clean and comfortable facilities, like suitable customer waiting areas.

You can find out more about the risk assessments VOSA will carry out in the guide below.

Download ‘VOSA’s guide to MOT risk reduction’ (PDF, 2.36MB)

Withdrawal of Authorised Examiner (AE) status

If you’re approved as an AE but the service you’re giving is found to have shortcomings, VOSA may take disciplinary action or stop you operating as a testing station.

If you lose AE status for disciplinary reasons, anyone applying for AE status at the same test station must be able to prove that they’re sufficiently independent from whoever the disciplinary action was taken against.

Special notices

VOSA uses ‘special notices’ to tell you about changes to the MOT scheme. AEs receive these automatically. You can read the latest special notices on the VOSA website.

Part 5: Additional phone lines and VTS devices

Depending on the number of MOT tests your vehicle testing station (VTS) performs, you may be eligible for a dedicated phone line and/or an additional dedicated computer for recording MOT tests, known as a VTS device.

What you’ll get

Average number of MOT tests per day What you get
7 or more dedicated phone line
15 or more additional VTS device

You’ll qualify for third and subsequent devices once you exceed multiples of 15 completed MOT tests per day - that is 30, 45, 60, etc.

If you think your test centre qualifies, you’ll need to make a request to the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA). They will then do a review based on your test volumes for the last 6 complete calendar months.

Contact the VOSA service desk to request a dedicated phone line or additional VTS device.

Help with childcare costs, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Free childcare and Tax Credits

There are different kinds of help you can get with the cost of childcare.

Free education for 3 and 4 year olds

All 3 and 4 year olds are entitled to 15 hours of free education for 38 weeks of the year. This can be at:

  • nursery schools
  • children’s centres
  • day nurseries
  • playgroups and pre-school
  • childminders

For help and advice, contact your local Family Information Service.

Sure Start Children’s Centres

Sure Start Children’s Centres offer a lot of help to families including:

  • early years education for children
  • parenting advice
  • training and employment advice for parents

Find your local Sure Start centre.

What it costs

If you want more childcare than you’re entitled to for free (for 3 or 4 year olds), there will be a charge.

After school clubs

Some schools offer activities outside normal school hours. Schools can charge for this (as much as the activity costs to organise) but you might be able to get Tax Credits.

Childcare tax credits

If you work and pay for childcare you may be able to get extra Tax Credits to help with the costs.

Part 2: Childcare from your employer

Exempt from tax and national insurance

There are types of childcare support your employer might provide that you don’t have to pay tax or national insurance contributions on:

  • childcare vouchers
  • directly contracted childcare (childcare arranged by your employer with a specific provider)
  • workplace nurseries

Not exempt from tax and national insurance

You have to pay tax and national insurance on what you get if your employer offers:

  • cash to pay for childcare
  • childcare fees paid direct to the childcare provider
  • school fees paid direct

Part 3: Support while you study

Discretionary Learner Support

If you’re 19 or over and on a further education course you may be able to get Discretionary Learner Support to pay for childcare. Each college has its own scheme so contact the college direct to find out more.

Childcare Grant

If you’re in full-time higher education you can apply for a Childcare Grant to pay for childcare costs for children under 15, or under 17 if they have special needs.

Care to Learn

Care to Learn payments go direct to your childcare provider to pay for childcare costs. You may be able to get Care to Learn if you’re under 20 and caring for your own child.

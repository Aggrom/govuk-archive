Expenses and benefits: holidays, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer providing holidays for your employees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

What’s included

What you have to report and pay depends on whether you:

  • provide a holiday, or vouchers that can be exchanged for a holiday
  • arrange the holiday yourself, or your employee does
  • pay for the holiday directly, or your employee pays for it and you pay them back

Part 2: What's exempt

If you arrange and pay for a holiday yourself for an employee who earns at a rate of less than £8,500 a year, you don’t have to pay or report anything to HMRC.

Part 3: What to report and pay

If any of the holidays you provide aren’t exempt, you may have to report the costs to HMRC, and deduct or pay tax and National Insurance on them.

Holiday vouchers

You’ll always need to report and pay if you provide your employees with vouchers they can exchange for a holiday.

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 Fill in form P9D, section B Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) on the cost to you of the vouchers
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more Fill in form P11D, section C Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) on the cost to you of the vouchers
Directors Fill in form P11D, section C Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) on the cost to you of the vouchers

Holidays you pay for directly

If you arrange the holiday:

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more Fill in form P11D, section K Pay Class 1A National Insurance on the cost to you
Directors Fill in form P11D, section K Pay Class 1A National Insurance on the cost to you

If your employee arranges the holiday:

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 Fill in form P9D, section A(2) Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) on the cost to you
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more Fill in form P11D, section B Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) on the cost to you
Directors Fill in form P11D, section B Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) on the cost to you

Holidays your employees arrange and pay for, and you pay them back

This counts as earnings, so you’ll need to:

  • add the amount you pay the employee to their other earnings
  • deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance through payroll

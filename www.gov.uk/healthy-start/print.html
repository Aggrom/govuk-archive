Benefits & credits: Healthy Start

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you’re pregnant or have a small child, the Healthy Start scheme can help you and your children stay healthy. If you’re eligible you can get vouchers to buy basic foods, such as milk and fruit.

You can also get coupons to swap for free vitamins suitable for:

  • pregnant women
  • breastfeeding women
  • growing children

Part 2: What you'll get

If you qualify, you’ll get vouchers worth £3.10 each to spend on:

  • milk
  • fresh fruit
  • vegetables
  • infant formula

You get 1 voucher a week if you:

  • are a pregnant woman
  • have a 2 or 3 year old child

You get 2 vouchers a week if you have a:

  • child under 1

You can also get free vitamin supplements.

Part 3: Eligibility

Check your eligibility.

You may qualify for the Healthy Start scheme if:

  • you’re pregnant
  • you have at least 1 child under 4

In addition, you also need to be receiving any of the following:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Child Tax Credit
  • Income-related Employment and Support Allowance

If you’re pregnant and under 18 you may also be eligible for the Healthy Start scheme.

You may also qualify if you’re receiving Working Tax credit run-on and have an annual family income of £16,190 or less (for 2011/2012).

Working Tax credit run-on is the payment you receive for a further 4 weeks immediately after you stop qualifying for Working Tax Credit.

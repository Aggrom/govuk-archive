Licence Denatured alcohol production and distribution

It is not currently possible to apply online for this licence. Please contact your local authority.

Overview

Denatured alcohol is commonly used for industrial purposes, eg the manufacture of cleaning fluids and perfumes – and is alcohol with chemicals added to prevent its use as a beverage. Alcohol denatured in accordance with approved formulations is not liable to excise duty.

There are three approved classes of denatured alcohol, which must be produced in accordance with specific formulations:

  • completely denatured alcohol (CDA)
  • industrial denatured alcohol (IDA)
  • trade specific denatured alcohol (TSDA)

Conditions

Producers

If you want to produce denatured alcohol you must either:

  • be a licensed distiller, rectifier or compounder with authorisation from HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) to denature alcohol
  • hold an excise licence to denature alcohol from HMRC

Distributors

If you deal wholesale in denatured alcohol you must hold an excise licence from HMRC. To deal wholesale you must sell quantities of no less than 20 litres to any one person.

How to apply

Producers: applications

To become authorised you must write to the NRU.

National Registration Unit
Portcullis House
21 India Street
Glasgow G2 4PZ 

You must explain that you want to be authorised to denature alcohol and give details of your distiller, rectifier or compounder licence.

You must also apply for a licence on Form L5 – Application for a licence to carry on an excise trade – available on the HMRC website. In the specified trade section you must enter ‘denaturer’.

Your licence can cover more than one set of premises.

Whether or not you are authorised or licensed, you must also:

  • apply to the National Registration Unit (NRU) for approval of your planned manufacturing process
  • make entry of each set of premises you intend to use to make denatured alcohol (unless your premises are approved as an excise warehouse)

Your application for approval of your manufacturing process must be made in writing (there is no set format) and include:

  • your name and contact number
  • address of the premises where you intend to denature alcohol
  • your VAT registration number (if applicable)
  • the class or classes of denatured alcohol you intend to make
  • the formulation you intend to follow
  • the process you intend to use when mixing the alcohol with the other substances specified in the formulation

HMRC’s approval will be in writing and may be subject to conditions.

To make entry of your premises you will need to obtain one of the following forms from HMRC:

  • Form EX 103 – for private traders
  • Form EX103A – for companies

Fill in the form as directed, describing all the rooms, vessels, utensils etc which you will be using for your trade. Diagrams may be required. Each room, vessel etc must be given an identifying name or number, as well as being physically marked.

The form should be sent to the NRU, along with your other application documents. No fee is payable.

An HMRC officer may inspect your premises prior to your application being granted.

Distributors: applications

You must apply for a licence on Form L5. In the specified trade section you must enter ‘distributor’.

Your application should be accompanied by a letter explaining what denatured alcohol you intend to distribute.

Your licence may cover more than one set of premises.

If you intend to hold stocks of IDA or TSDA you must:

  • apply to the NRU for authorisation using the standard wording for an application contained in HMRC guidance
  • make entry of each set of premises you intend to use to hold denatured alcohol

You must only sell IDA and TSDA to authorised users and have a copy of that authorisation.

Fines and penalties

If you don’t follow the requirements covering the production and distribution of denatured alcohol, you may be fined and any denatured alcohol on your premises may be forfeited.

If you have any queries about obtaining a licence, authorisation or approval, you can call the HMRC Excise Helpline.

0845 010 9000
Monday to Friday 8am to 8pm

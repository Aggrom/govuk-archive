Benefits & credits: Access to Elected Office Fund

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get a grant of between £250 and £10,000 if you’re disabled and standing for election or selection as a candidate to the:

  • UK Parliament
  • English local government
  • Greater London Assembly
  • mayoral elections (England only)
  • Police and Crime Commissioner

The money is paid from the Access to Elected Office Fund by Convey, an independent administrator. It’s a grant, so you don’t have to pay it back.

The fund is for disability-related costs you pay as part of standing for election - it doesn’t cover general campaigning costs (like leaflets) or living costs.

Read frequently asked questions about the fund.

Part 2: What you'll get

You could get between £250 and £10,000 in any calendar year (1 January to 31 December) to cover disability-related costs. For example:

  • transport needs (if you have difficulty using public transport)
  • sign language interpreters
  • extra travel or accommodation costs if you need a carer

You can apply more than once but together the applications must not total more than £10,000. Funds can be backdated up to 2 months before the date of your offer letter.

You can’t use the money for general campaigning or living costs. Read guidance on campaign spending and donations from the Electoral Commission website.

How you’re paid

If your application is successful, send in any original invoices or receipts for the items you listed in your application. The money will be paid after you provide proof of your spending.

Payments are made on the second and last Friday of every month.

Part 3: Eligibility

You can apply to the Access to Elected Office Fund if:

  • you’re eligible to stand for election
  • you can prove your disability - eg letter from a doctor
  • you can prove you’re involved or interested in civic, community or relevant activities - eg volunteering or student politics
  • your application is supported by a member of your political party - or an independent referee if you don’t have a political party

Part 4: How to claim

You can apply to the Access to Elected Office Fund:

  • online
  • by post, phone or textphone - contact the Access to Elected Office Fund to ask for an application form and guidance notes

What you need to know

You’ll get a decision within 1 month if your application is for £500 or less.

Applications for more than £500 need to be considered by an independent panel, which meets four times a year. You’ll be notified within a week of the panel meeting.

Deadlines for applications over £500

You need to apply by these dates for your application to be considered at the next panel meeting.

  • 22 November 2012
  • 14 March 2013
  • 13 June 2013
  • 12 September 2013
  • 21 November 2013
  • 6 March 2014

Appeal a decision

You can appeal if your application is turned down, but you must do this within 1 month of the decision. You should get a response within 4 working days.

You can only ask for the appeal decision to be reviewed if your application was for £500 or less. The decision letter you get will tell you what to do.

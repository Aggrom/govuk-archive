European Fisheries Fund: fishing industry common interests

Projects covered by European Fisheries Fund common interests funding, conditions of getting a grant and how to apply.

Introduction

If you want to invest in measures of common interest for the fisheries industry, you may be eligible for a grant from the European Fisheries Fund (EFF).

‘Measures of common interest’ are projects that benefit groups and achieve a common aim. They include large regeneration projects, upgrading professional skills, smaller harbour developments, promotional activity, protecting marine wildlife and developing the marine envrionment and pilot projects (such as testing innovative technology).

The amount of funding you could receive will depend on the type of project, the area, and whether you are applying as a public or private body. You may need to match EFF funding with private funds or funding from other public sources.

Who can apply?

You, as the applicant, must sign the application form, even if you had help completing it as you will be responsible for making sure that all of the terms and conditions of the grant are met.

There are 5 different funding streams within common interests and each has its own criteria on who can apply.

Collective actions

Grants are available to any public or private sector (or both) enterprise that work collectively to achieve a common aim and achieve added value over that reached through the sum of individual investments. Projects may be carried out with the active support of operators or by organisation acting on behalf of producers or other organisations, such as public, private, non-governmental organisations, scientific or trade organisations.

Pilot projects

Grants are available to any public or private sector (or both) enterprise that work collectively to achieve a common aim. Projects are only eligible if they are non-commercial and are carried out by approved bodies such as economic operators and recognised trade associations or any other UK designated competent body in partnership with a scientific or technical body.

Fishing ports, landing sites and shelters

Grants are available to any public or private sector (or both) enterprise that work collectively to achieve a common aim. Projects may be carried out with the active support of operators or by organisation acting on behalf of producers or other organisations, such as public, private, non-governmental organisations, scientific or trade organisations.

New markets and promotional campaigns

Grants are available to any public or private sector (or both) enterprise that work collectively to achieve a common aim. Projects may be carried out with the active support of operators or by organisation acting on behalf of producers or other organisations, such as public, private, non-governmental organisations, scientific or trade organisations.

Projects to protect and develop aquatic flora and fauna

Grants are available to public or semi-public bodies, recognised trade organisations or other bodies appointed for this purpose.

What things may be eligible for a grant?

Collective actions

The main objectives of this funding stream are to:

  • ensure a well qualified and innovative industry through developing and delivering professional, safety and specialist skills training
  • support common actions that contribute to improved management sustainable exploitation of resources, improved hygiene and minimised environmental impacts
  • provide conditions for the long-term profit and competitiveness of the fisheries industry and these benefits are felt by producers and consumers

Support will be granted to a project where measures:

  • sustainably contribute to better management or conservation of resources
  • promote selective fishing methods or gear and reduced by-catch
  • improve working conditions and safety
  • contribute to the transparency of markets in fisheries and aquaculture products – including traceability
  • improve quality and food safety
  • contribute to the improved production, processing or marketing equipment and infrastructure – including for the treatment of fisheries and aquaculture products waste
  • upgrade professional skills or develop new training methods and tools
  • promote partnership between scientists and operators in the fisheries sector
  • contribute to UK objectives for small-scale coastal fishing
  • carry out feasibility studies relating to promoting partnerships with third countries in the fisheries sector
  • create or restructure producer organisation and put into practice plans to improve product quality

Read more about eligible and not eligible costs in guidance notes on the MMO website (PDF 150KB)

Pilot projects

Pilot projects must be truly innovative and small, technical improvements of well known technologies do not qualify. The projects should have limited cost and duration in line with their experimental nature and should not be of a directly commercial nature.

Any profit generated during the pilot will be deducted from the grant given to the operation.

Read more about eligible and not eligible costs in guidance notes on the MMO website (PDF 144KB)

Fishing ports, landing sites and shelters

Projects that invest in existing public or private fishing ports with the aim of improving them, which are of interest to fish and aquaculture producers using them, may be eligible. Projects that invest in the restructuring of landing sites to improve the conditions for fish landed by coastal fishermen may be eligible.

The construction of fishing ports is excluded from support.

Projects must show that they have a broader scope than measures carried out by private enterprises and help to meet the objectives of the Common Fisheries Policy. This may include capital investments that:

  • improve the conditions that fisheries and aquaculture products are landed, processed, stored and auctioned
  • relate to providing fuel, ice, water and electricity
  • improve working or safety conditions
  • improve storing and treating of waste
  • reduce discards
  • relate to constructing, modernising and extending of quays to improve safety of landing or loading
  • relate to the repair, equipping and maintaining of fishing vessels
  • computerise the managing of fishing activities

Read more about eligible and not eligible costs in guidance notes on the MMO website (PDF 135KB)

New markets and promotional campaigns

Projects must show that they have a broader scope than measures carried out by private enterprises and help to meet the objectives of the Common Fisheries Policy. This may include projects such as:

  • market studies and studies – including those that relate to the prospect of marketing EU products in third countries
  • consumer surveys
  • test consumer reaction
  • promotion campaigns – including those that highlight quality issues
  • sales advice and aids
  • quality certification and product labelling
  • campaigns to improve market conditions
  • organising and participating in trade fairs and exhibitions
  • organising study and sales visits

Read more about eligible and not eligible costs in guidance notes on the MMO website (PDF 141KB)

Projects to protect and develop aquatic flora and fauna

Projects must show that they have a broader scope than measures carried out by private enterprises and help to meet the objectives of the Common Fisheries Policy. Projects should relate to:

  • constructing or installing static or movable facilities intended to protect and develop aquatic fauna and flora
  • rehabilitating inland waters including spawning grounds and routes for migratory species

Read more about eligible and not eligible costs in guidance notes on the MMO website (PDF 137KB)

How much grant will I get?

The EFF programme is competitive, and only projects that most effectively meet its aims will be awarded a grant. You will receive a percentage of the total eligible cost of your project if you are successful.

You cannot receive funding from more than one European Union fund. You must confirm on your application that you have not applied for funding under another scheme.

Collective actions

The minimum grant is £600 and the maximum is £500,000. This must be equally matched with money from national sources and the appropriate amount of private investment. You will only receive your money once you have spent money on your project.

How to apply for funding

You should submit the Axis 3 application form to the MMO. Find out more about Axis 2 and download the application form from the MMO website.

Your application must include a detailed business case that shows that your project:

  • has quantified outputs and clear and attainable targets
  • will provide an additional and sustainable benefit to the fishing industry
  • has a justified need for grant will provide good value for money

You should also include:

  • a full description of your project
  • an environmental impact assessment
  • the benefits of the project
  • financial, environmental and other benefits
  • a cost-benefit analysis
  • an analysis of quotes
  • the results you expect from the project
  • how you will publicise the project
  • how you will monitor the project
  • how you will manage risks to delivery
  • information about your business
  • how your project will affect the local area

You should get at least the minimum number of quotes for any individual item and include original quotes, signed and dated by the supplier with your application form (or as soon as possible if you apply while waiting for quotes).

What is in the offer letter?

The offer letter sets out details of the conditions of the grant and any agreed performance targets and outputs for the project. You must comply with the conditions along with details of eligible expenditure to allow us to pay your grant claims.

More information from European Fisheries Fund overview guide.

How to claim funding from the EFF

If you are eligible for funding under the EFF, you can claim your grant by submitting a claim form by the date specified in your offer letter to the MMO at the following address:

Business Relations Team
Marine Management Organisation
Lancaster House
Hampshire Court
Newcastle upon Tyne
NE4 7YH

Download the EFF claim form and guidance from the MMO website.

You should normally submit a single full and final claim by the date specified in your offer letter. You may be allowed to submit one or more interim claims but the final claim must still be submitted by the date specified in the offer letter.

The MMO aims to pay the grant within 8 weeks once all the necessary information to consider your claim is received. The MMO accept no liability in respect of loss attributable to any delay in the payment of claims or to any suspension, reduction or cancellation of grant.

The payment will be made electronically into your bank account.

More information on completing the claim form, supporting documentation, changing your project and how to manage your project from European Fisheries Fund overview guide.

For advice, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2505 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk

Companies House filings: an introduction for agents and advisers

Find out about filing company annual accounts and returns, and tools to help such as Companies House Software, WebFiling and WebCHeck.

Introduction

Many agents and advisers set up and file documents on behalf of companies. If you are about to start filing company documents such as accounts and annual returns with Companies House, this guide will help you to understand what you need to do and where to go for more help.

This guide will also introduce you to some of the useful online tools and resources that make filing faster and simpler. These include Companies House Software, WebFiling and the search facility WebCHeck.

Companies House annual return for agents

Every company must send an annual return to Companies House each year. It provides important company information, in a prescribed format, which is then made available to the public. Annual returns must be sent to Companies House by a specific ‘made up’ date - usually the anniversary of the company’s date of incorporation or the anniversary of the last annual return filed at Companies House.

You can find out more about the annual return in the guide: Companies House annual return.

Companies House annual accounts guidance for agents

All companies, both private and public, must keep accounting records. Limited companies and limited liability partnerships must send a copy of their annual accounts to Companies House each year. Most companies use professional firms of accountants to produce their accounts.

If you produce and file accounts on behalf of a company, you must ensure they are signed or authenticated by the accountants (if appropriate) and the company’s directors.

You can find out more about what information is needed and how to submit it in the guide on filing accounts with Companies House: dates and checklist.

Submit documents to Companies House

If you are delivering statutory information to Companies House on behalf of your clients, the most secure way is to use their electronic and online filing services. You can read more about this in the guide about using Companies House electronic and online services. Although you need to register for this, the process is straightforward and free of charge.

WebFiling forms and exceptions

WebFiling can be used to submit the following documents online to Companies House.

  • Annual Return (AR01)
  • Change of Accounting Reference Date (AA01)
  • Appointment of a Director or Secretary (AP01 - AP04)
  • Terminating Appointment as Director or Secretary (TM01 - TM02)
  • Change of Particulars for Director or Secretary (CH01 - CH04)
  • Change in Situation or Address of Registered Office (AD01)
  • Notification of single alternative inspection location (SAIL) (AD02)
  • Change of location of the company records to the single alternative inspection location (SAIL) (AD03)
  • Change of location of the company records to the registered office (AD04)
  • Return of Allotments of Shares (SH01)
  • Audit Exempt Abbreviated Accounts
  • Dormant Company Accounts (DCA)

The Annual Return allows the user to submit a snapshot of the company data, with associated documents, that update the company information. These will be displayed on the public record as separate form types.

Annual Return system boundaries

  • Companies with more than 350 individual and joint shareholders.
  • Companies with a share value of more than six decimal places.
  • Companies with an individual share value of more than £10 million.
  • Companies with more than ten joint shareholders per shareholding.
  • Companies where more than 255 changes need to be input within a single session.
  • Companies required to submit lists of subsidiary and associated undertakings.

Annual Return exception

  • The Annual Return is not available for electronic submission for companies with bulk shareholder lists.

Annual Return legal constraints

  • The Annual Return cannot be submitted for companies that are dissolved, converted or closed.

You can also deliver documents by post, courier, Document Exchange Service or Legal Post (in Scotland), and by using software from a number of commercial software suppliers. Find commercial filing software information on the HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) website.

You will receive an acknowledgement from Companies House, provided you send a copy of your covering letter and a pre-paid addressed return envelope.

Use Companies House electronic and online services

To save you time and expense, Companies House provides two electronic and online filing services - Software Filing and WebFiling. The Companies House WebCHeck service also allows you to search the company names index.

Web incorporation service

This service enables you to incorporate your company online as a private company, limited by shares, with model articles of association.

For explanations of company types and how to incorporate, see our guide on legal structures: the basics.

To use the service, you will need to register with your email address and a password of your choice.

You will then need the following information to complete the form:

  1. Company name and address
  2. Details of directors and secretary (optional)
  3. Details of each shareholder and the value of their shares
  4. Payment details - find out more about Companies House fees on the Companies House website

Any data captured through online incorporation is stored with the Registrar of Companies and gov.uk and is subject to the Companies House privacy policy

This service is delivered by Companies House in conjunction with gov.uk.

Incorporate your company with Companies House online.

Software filing

This service allows external companies to develop software packages to manage the submission of a range of forms and documents required by Companies House. Find out about the Software Filing service on the Companies House website. You can also access a list of software suppliers and formation agents on the Companies House website.

WebFiling

This is a safe and reliable way to file company information online. Most information can be filed free of charge, so it also saves money for your clients. The service can also be used seven days a week, 24 hours a day, so it is also very convenient. You can find out more information in the guide on how to use WebFiling to file company information online.

You can get extra protection against corporate identity fraud by using the PROtected Online Filing service. For more information, see the guide on how to use WebFiling to file company information online.

WebCHeck

This free service enables you to search company details and look at filing histories online for all limited companies registered in the UK. You can order documents and reports, and monitor companies, for which a small fee is charged. You can search company information using the WebCHeck service at Companies House.

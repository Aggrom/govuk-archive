Introduction

A Farm Environment Plan (FEP) is a structured survey of all the environmental features on your farm. It involves identifying and making an assessment of the condition of any features of historical, wildlife, resource protection, or access interest.

The FEP is a pre-requisite for Higher Level Stewardship (HLS) and will help to determine what level of environmental management can be achieved through this scheme. The FEP also looks at ways of creating new environmental features and habitats, improving public access, flood management and protecting natural resources.

Any applications for HLS must be accompanied by a completed FEP. If an FEP is completed to Natural England’s quality standard, and is submitted with a valid HLS application, then a payment will be made for it.

Payment for your Farm Environment Plan

The amount you get for a completed Farm Environment Plan (FEP) depends on how much land is registered on the Rural Payments Agency (RPA) Rural Land Register, as shown on the FEP/Farm Environment Record map.

You can find more information in the guide on registering your land. Your payment will include an amount to cover the fees charged for consulting the Historic Environment Record.

You will be paid for the FEP after it, and your HLS application, have been assessed by Natural England. Payment does not depend on your application to join HLS being successful.

Your Higher Level Stewardship application

Before starting on your FEP, you must contact Natural England to discuss your HLS application and obtain an application pack. The pack includes the HLS application handbook, an FEP map to be annotated with your farm’s FEP features, and a map of the farm showing what environmental information Natural England already holds.

You can find the HLS application handbook on the Natural England website.

You’ll also need an HLS FEP Manual and the FEP forms. You can find up to date information on completing your FEP on the Natural England website.

For more information about FEPs, find contact details for your local Natural England office on the Natural England website.

The Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) aims to help the farming industry operate as efficiently as possible, administering European support policies that provide around £3 billion to UK agriculture.

You can call the Defra Helpline on 08459 33 55 77.

The RPA is responsible for licences and schemes for growers as well as for running the Single Payment Scheme (SPS). For more information about SPS and how it can help your farming business you can call the RPA Helpline on 0845 603 7777.

The National Farmers’ Union (NFU) represents the farmers and growers of England and Wales. You can read about its work on the NFU website.

Farmers are likely to come into contact with local authorities over a number of farming, land use, food standards and environmental regulations. Your local authority may also be able to provide further information or resources.

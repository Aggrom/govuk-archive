Quick answer Become an appointee for someone claiming benefits

If someone can’t manage their own affairs because they’re mentally incapable or severely disabled, you can apply for the right to deal with their benefits on their behalf.

Only one appointee can act on behalf of someone who is entitled to benefits given out by the Department for Work and Pensions (DWP).

An appointee can be:

  • an individual, eg a friend or relative
  • an organisation or representative of an organisation, eg a solicitor or local council

Your responsibilities as an appointee

As an appointee you take full responsibility for making and maintaining any benefit claims. You must also manage the way the benefit is spent.

This means you must:

  • sign the claim form instead of the person claiming benefits
  • tell the benefit office of any changes which can affect the amount of benefit the claimant gets
  • claim any benefits the person is entitled to
  • spend the benefit - which is paid direct to you - in the best interests of the claimant
  • tell the benefit office if the person claiming benefit becomes capable of managing their own affairs

If benefit is overpaid then, depending on the circumstances, you could be held responsible.

How to apply to become an appointee

Contact the Benefit enquiry line and tell them that someone you know needs your help to either:

  • claim benefit - because they can’t do it themselves
  • manage their existing benefit - because they can’t do it themselves

Benefit enquiry line

BEL-Customer-Services@dwp.gsi.gov.uk
Freephone: 0800 882 200
Textphone: 0800 243 355
Monday to Friday, 8:30am to 6:30pm

Next steps

  1. The DWP will arrange to visit the claimant to assess if they’re incapable of managing their affairs and that an appointee is needed.

  2. The DWP will interview you to make sure that you’re suitable and willing to act as the appointee.

  3. During the interview, and after the visit to the claimant, an ‘Application for appointment to act on behalf of someone else’ (Form BF56) is completed by you and the interviewer.

  4. If the DWP agrees that the claimant needs help and that you’re suitable you’ll be formally appointed to act on their behalf. If this hasn’t happened, you aren’t yet an ‘appointee’.

Reviewing appointees

Once you’re authorised as an appointee the DWP will continue to monitor the situation to make sure it’s still the most suitable arrangement for you and the person you’re an appointee for.

Cancelling the right to be an appointee

Your appointment can be revoked (withdrawn) if:

  • you don’t act properly under the terms of the appointment
  • the claimant is clearly able to manage their own benefits
  • you become incapable yourself
  • you don’t want to continue as an appointee

To cancel your role as an appointee you must give 4 weeks’ written notice to the benefit office.

Further information

Full details of the appointment process can be found in the ‘Agents, appointees, attorneys and deputies guide’.

Lasting Power of Attorney, being in care and your financial affairs

  • Apply to the Court of Protection

    You need to apply to the Court of Protection to act as someone's deputy and make decisions on their behalf

  • Become a deputy

    A deputy is someone appointed by the Court of Protection to make decisions for someone who is unable to do so on their own

  • Claim Child Benefit on behalf of someone else

    Claim Child Benefit if your child has a baby, for someone who's ill or disabled, apply to be an appointee for Child Benefit

  • Court Funds Office processes

    The Court Funds Office provides a banking service for the civil courts in England and Wales for Court of Protection clients and children under 18

  • Court of Protection

    Contact information for the Court of Protection

  • Find out if someone has an attorney or deputy acting for them

    Apply to search the Office of the Public Guardian registers to see if they have a Lasting Power of Attorney or a deputy acting on their behalf

  • Getting someone to help with your tax credits claim

    Deal with the Tax Credit office for someone else - authorisation, TC689 authorisation form, appointees, if your child has a baby

  • Lasting Power of Attorney

    A Lasting Power of Attorney (LPA) lets you appoint someone to make decisions on your behalf about your finances or your health and welfare

  • Leaving foster or local authority care

    When you leave foster or local council care, the council must help you to live independently - there'll be a meeting to discuss your future

  • Mental health tribunal

    Appealing to a mental health tribunal if someone is sectioned: how to appeal, what happens at a hearing and what to do if you lose your case

  • Report a concern about an attorney or deputy

    Call the Office of the Public Guardian on 0115 934 2777 if you have concerns about an attorney, deputy or a decision they've made for someone else

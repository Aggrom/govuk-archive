Set up an Authorised Testing Facility (ATF), a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

An Authorised Testing Facility (ATF) tests that heavy goods vehicles, trailers and public service vehicles like buses are roadworthy and meet safety and environmental standards.

To set up an ATF, you must apply to the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA). They’ll carry out an assessment and probably also do a site inspection before deciding whether you can open.

You can’t test vehicles until you’re authorised - but as an ATF, you can:

  • have VOSA staff test your own vehicles
  • offer a testing service to third parties using VOSA staff - customers can test their vehicles at your ATF instead of at a VOSA test station

An ATF can’t test cars, motorbikes, small vans etc - to test these type of vehicles, you must apply to set up an MOT testing station.

Part 2: How to apply

To set up as an Authorised Testing Facility (ATF), you’ll need:

  • a testing area that’s at least 2 metres wider than the vehicles to be tested
  • a permanent weatherproof building
  • testing equipment
  • offices that Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) staff can visit and work from

For full details, read the ATF checklist before you apply. There’s also a guide on how to operate an ATF - this covers health and safety, equipment and the fees you can charge.

Application form

You can request this online. There’s no fee to apply.

What to send

Send the application form, with the required documentation eg health and safety and site layout plans, to the address on the form.

Call the VOSA helpline if you have any questions about your application.

VOSA helpline
0300 123 9000
Monday to Friday, 7:30am to 6pm

Part 3: What happens to your application

You should get a response by post within 2 weeks of sending in your application.

If your application is ‘provisionally accepted’, the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) will contact you to discuss site development.

If it’s rejected, the letter will explain why, and tell you what you need to do if you want to apply again.

Don’t start any building work or buy equipment until your application has been approved. You won’t be able to reclaim these costs if it’s turned down.

Part 4: Site inspection and approval

The Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) will normally carry out an initial site inspection and a final site inspection before you can be approved as an Authorised Testing Facility (ATF).

Site inspections

Once VOSA has visited your site, you’ll get a report - usually within a week.

If your site passes the initial inspection, there may be a final inspection before you get approved - let VOSA know when you’re ready and they’ll arrange a visit.

If it fails the inspection, you’ll be told what needs to be done and given a deadline to send in new plans.

If your site fails its final inspection, VOSA will send you a letter telling you what you need to do.

Approval

When VOSA has given final approval, you’ll get your certificate and can operate as an ATF. You’ll have to operate under the terms set out in the ATF contract.

Part 5: Operating your ATF

Once you’ve been approved to operate an Authorised Testing Facility (ATF), there are certain things you must do.

Set up a pre-funded account (PFA)

If you already have a PFA, you can use this for your ATF. If you don’t, you’ll need to set one up.

With a PFA you’ll be able to access the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency’s (VOSA’s) goods and services whenever you need them.

You need a separate PFA for each ATF site.

Download ‘ATF pre-funded account guidance and application form’ (PDF, 45KB)

Meet health and safety requirements

You must ensure your premises meet health and safety regulations. During site visits VOSA may ask to see any relevant documents, eg public liability insurance certificate, fire risk assessment etc.

Display logos and signage

You must display at least one VOSA and ATF logo even if you only service your own vehicles.

If you offer third party or open access testing the logo should be displayed as explained in the brand guidelines.

Download ‘VOSA’s ATF brand guidelines’ (PDF, 1.56MB)

It’s your responsibility to get licences or planning permission (England and Wales), Scotland to display signage on your premises.

Provide and maintain testing equipment

You must make sure your premises are adequately stocked and all your equipment is in good working order.

Working with VOSA

You must have a competent site manager available on a daily basis to work with VOSA and manage test bookings.

In return, VOSA must:

  • supply an examiner for any testing period that has a confirmed reservation
  • ensure all examiners comply with relevant statutory requirements
  • give you the relevant test documentation after testing

Part 6: Booking examiners

All testing at an Authorised Testing Facility (ATF) has to be done by Vehicle Operator Services Agency (VOSA) examiners.

You must book a minimum of 2 testing periods of 3.5 hours per test site - that’s 7 hours on each day that an examiner is needed. You can also book a third testing period if you need it.

You can:

  • book up to 12 months in advance
  • make short-term bookings up to 2 months in advance

Booking requests must be made between 15 and 30 September each year. Examiners can be booked for 12-month periods, starting in January each year.

To book, ask your local VOSA station manager for a form.

There’s more information in VOSA’s booking policy.

Download ‘VOSA ATF examiner booking policy’ (PDF, 62KB)

Paying for examiners

VOSA will confirm your booking request in writing and they’ll debit your pre-funded account 7 calendar days before the testing day.

There’s a reservation fee of £390 for each 3.5 hour testing period.

Part 7: Testing fees

If you offer third party or open access testing at your Authorised Testing Facility (ATF), you must charge fees at the rates published by the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA).

Download ‘Public service vehicle (PSV) fees from 1 April 2012’ (PDF, 130KB)

Download ‘Individual Vehicle Approval (IVA) fees from 1 April 2012 (PDF, 223KB)

Pit fees

You can charge a pit fee on top of the test fee. These are capped by VOSA. The current maximum pit fees (excluding VAT) are:

  • £55 for testing HGVs
  • £70 for testing PSVs
  • £40 for testing trailers

If you charge more than this, your ATF contract could be suspended.

Part 8: Making changes to your ATF

You can apply to make changes to your Authorised Testing Facility (ATF), eg:

  • changing your opening times
  • changing the physical dimensions of a site
  • adding categories of vehicle to be tested
  • changing site equipment
  • adding or removing third party or open access testing
  • adding another site to an existing ATF contract
  • administrative changes (eg to contact names or telephone numbers)

You can apply online to make changes.

If you’re making physical changes to your site you’ll also need:

  • a site plan for each location included in the application
  • fully dimensioned drawings of the proposed testing area(s)
  • written confirmation of planning permission (or a written explanation of why it isn’t needed)

Contact the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) helpline if you have any questions about your application.

VOSA helpline
0300 123 9000
Monday to Friday, 7:30am to 6pm

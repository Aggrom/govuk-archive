Expenses and benefits: car parking charges, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer covering the cost of parking changes for your employees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

What’s included

‘Parking charges’ include the cost of your employees’ parking at or near your workplace, and parking on ‘business journeys’.

A business journey is:

  • a trip made as part of work, eg a service engineer travelling to an appointment
  • a trip to a temporary workplace

There are different rules if you provide your employee with a parking space directly.

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to report anything to HMRC, or deduct or pay tax and National Insurance, if:

  • the charge was for parking at or near your workplace
  • your employee got the charge while on a ‘business journey’, and they earn at a rate of less than £8,500 a year
  • you have a contract with a parking provider, you pay them directly and your employee earns at a rate of less than £8,500 a year

Part 3: What to report and pay

If the parking charge isn’t exempt, you must report it to HMRC, and may have to deduct and pay tax and National Insurance on it.

Parking charges on ‘business journeys’

If your employee earns at a rate of £8,500 or more, or they’re a director, you’ll need to report on form P11D, section N. You won’t need to deduct or pay tax or National Insurance.

Other parking charges

You have a contract with a parking provider and you pay them directly

If your employee earns at a rate of £8,500 or more, or they’re a director, you’ll need to:

  • report on form P11D, section M
  • pay Class 1A National Insurance on what you pay for the parking

Your employee has a contract with a parking provider and you pay them directly

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year Report on form P9D, section A(2) Add what you pay for parking to their other earnings, and deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax)
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Report on form P11D, section N Add what you pay for parking to their other earnings, and deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax)
Directors Report on form P11D, section N Add what you pay for parking to their other earnings, and deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax)

You give your employee money to pay for parking charges

It counts as earnings and you must:

  • add it to your employee’s other earnings
  • deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance using payroll

Expenses and benefits: mobile phones, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer providing mobile phones to your employees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

This includes:

  • costs for phones you provide to employees
  • reimbursing your employee’s own phone costs

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to report anything to HMRC or deduct and pay tax and National Insurance if both the following apply:

  • you provide your employee with only 1 mobile phone or SIM card
  • the phone contract is between you and the supplier

Low-earning employees

If your employee earns at a rate of less than £8,500 a year, you don’t have to report or pay anything if either of the following apply:

  • the employee has a ‘pay as you go’ phone and you reimburse the cost of business calls
  • you pay the supplier directly for a phone that was acquired for business purposes and is only used for business calls

Part 3: What to report and pay

If telephone expenses aren’t exempt, you must report them to HMRC and may have to deduct and pay tax and National Insurance on them.

You can apply for a dispensation which means you won’t have to include telephones for employees on your end-of-year reports.

If you provide more than one phone to an employee

One of the phones is exempt from paying and reporting, but any other phones count as assets.

If your employee arranges the phone but you pay the supplier

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees at a rate of less than £8,500 a year Report on form P9D, section A(2) Class 1 National Insurance through payroll
Employees at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Report on form P11D, section B Class 1 National Insurance through payroll
Directors Report on form P11D, section B Class 1 National Insurance through payroll

The employee uses their own phone and you reimburse them

If you only reimburse the monthly phone tariff, you don’t have to report anything but you must deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance and PAYE tax through payroll.

Reimbursing call charges over the monthly phone tariff

What you reimburse What to do What to deduct or pay
Private call charges Nothing Class 1 National Insurance and PAYE tax through payroll
Business call charges for employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Report on form P11D, section N Nothing
Business call charges for directors Report on form P11D, section N Nothing

Your employee has a ‘pay as you go’ mobile and you reimburse them for business calls

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Report on form P11D, section N Nothing
Directors Report on form P11D, section N Nothing

Part 4: Work out the value

The value to use for reporting or paying National Insurance depends on who arranges and pays for the mobile phone, and whether you cover the cost of personal or business calls.

Your employee arranges the phone but you pay the supplier or reimburse them

The amount to put through payroll is the amount of monthly tariff you reimburse, plus the cost of any private calls above the tariff if you reimburse these as well.

The amount you should report is the cost of any business calls you reimburse that are on top of the monthly tariff.

Your employee has a ‘pay as you go’ mobile and you pay for business calls

The value to use is the amount you reimburse or give to the employee as a ‘top up’.

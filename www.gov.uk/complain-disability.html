Quick answer Complain about the Disability and Carers Service

If you’re unhappy with the service you’ve received, contact the office dealing with your claim - their details will be at the top of any letters from the Disability and Carers Service.

Explain what has happened, how this has affected you and what you want to happen to put things right.

If you’re not sure which office to contact, call the number below that best applies to you.

Disability Living Allowance and Attendance Allowance Helpline
Telephone: 0845 712 3456
Textphone: 0845 722 4433

Carer’s Allowance Unit:
Telephone: 0845 608 4321
Textphone: 0845 604 5312

Vaccine Damage Payments Unit
01772 899 944

The Disability and Carers Service will try to solve your issues over the phone. They’ll aim to deal with your complaint within 15 working days.

Contact Citizens Advice or your local MP if you need help making your complaint.

If you’re unhappy with the response

You’ll be asked if you want your complaint sent to the Chief Operating Officer of the Disability and Carers Service, who aims to deal with complaints within 15 working days.

If you’re still unhappy, you can then ask the Independent Case Examiner to investigate - they’ll be impartial and this is free. 

If you’re unhappy with the response from the Independent Case Examiner, you can ask your MP to send your complaint to the Parliamentary and Health Service Ombudsman.

If a mistake has been made

You may get a special payment if you’ve experienced unfair treatment or suffered financially. More information is in the Disability and Carers Service customer leaflet.

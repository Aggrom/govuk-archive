Part 2: Eligibility

The list below shows if you may be able to get National Insurance credits. You may get your credits automatically, but in some cases you’ll have to apply for them

Group Who qualifies How to get credits
Unemployed You can’t be in education or working more than 16 hours a week You get credits automatically
Illness, disability, maternity leave You must be getting one of these: Employment and Support Allowance, Unemployability Supplement or Allowance, Maternity Allowance You get credits automatically
On maternity, paternity, adoption or sick pay You must get one of these: Statutory Maternity/Paternity/Adoption Pay, Additional Statutory Paternity Pay, Statutory Sick Pay Call the helpline to apply for credits - see number below this table
Men approaching 65 You must live in the UK at least 183 days a year and one of these must apply: you don’t work, you don’t earn enough to make the ‘qualifying year’ for the State Pension, you’re self employed and have a Small Earnings Exception Certificate You’ll get credits automatically
Parents and carers One of these must apply: you’re a registered carer, you’re caring for someone sick or disabled at least 20 hours a week Download form CF411A, application for credits (PDF, 56KB)
Parents and carers One of these must apply: you’re on Carer’s Allowance, Income Support or getting Child Benefit for a child under 12 You get credits automatically
On a government approved private training course All of these must apply: you’re over 18, your course is government approved and no longer than 1 year Write to: HMRC, National Insurance Contributions and Employers Office, Benton Park View, Newcastle upon Tyne, NE98 1ZZ
On a government sponsored training course Anyone You get credits automatically
Working Tax Credit You must get Working Tax Credit and have a Small Earnings Exception certificate if self-employed You get credits automatically
Partners of members in Armed Forces You must be married or a civil partner and going with your partner on an overseas posting Download form MODCA1, application for credits (PDF, 80KB)
On jury service You must have attended court. If you’re self-employed, you can’t get credits Call the helpline
Wrongly imprisoned Your conviction must have been quashed by the Court of Appeal (or the Court of Criminal Appeal in Scotland) Call the helpline

From the 2011 to 2012 tax year there are new credits for people aged 16 and over who care for a child under 12. You have to be under State Pension age to qualify. You don’t need to do anything yet if you want to apply.

If you have any questions about National Insurance credits, call the helpline:

National Insurance Helpline

Telephone: 0845 302 1479
Textphone: 0845 915 3296
Monday to Friday, 8am to 8pm
Saturday, 8am to 4pm

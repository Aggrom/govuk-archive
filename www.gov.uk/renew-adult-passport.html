Quick answer Renew or replace your adult passport

You can renew or replace your UK adult passport up to 9 months before your current one runs out. It costs £72.50 to renew by post or £81.25 through the Post Office Check & Send service.

How long it takes to renew or replace a passport

It will take at least 3 weeks to get your passport - use a different service if you need your passport more urgently.

Renewals and replacements can take longer between April and September. Apply early whenever you can.

Time left on your existing passport will be added to your new one - up to a maximum of 9 months.

When to replace a damaged passport

If your passport is damaged and has more than reasonable wear and tear, you should replace it as soon as you can - you may not be allowed to travel with it.

Get the application form

If you’re in the UK, you can:

  • fill in the form online - the Identity and Passport Service (IPS) will post it back to you to check and sign
  • get one from a Post Office that offers the Check & Send service
  • call the Passport Adviceline and get them to post you one

If you’re outside the UK, you must apply to the Foreign and Commonwealth Office.

Help filling in the form

The form comes with notes to help you fill it in. You can download a copy of the notes.

Download ‘Guidance notes - Applying for a passport’ (PDF, 495KB)

Photos

You must include 2 identical new photos of yourself.

Follow the rules about passport photos or your application may be rejected.

Countersignatories

You must get your application form and 1 of your photos signed by someone else to prove your identity if either:

  • you can’t be recognised from the photo in your existing passport
  • you’re replacing a damaged passport

Check who can countersign your application and photo and what they have to do.

Send your application

You can either:

  • use the Post Office Check & Send service
  • post your application using the pre-printed envelope that comes with the form and include your existing passport, photos and supporting documents.

You can pay by debit or credit card (by completing the form in the application pack) or make a cheque payable to ‘Identity and Passport Service’.

What happens next

The IPS will check your application. Your new passport will be sent to your home address. You may have to sign for it.

If you want to check your application’s progress, call the Passport Adviceline - you can do this 2 weeks after you’ve made your application.

When you call, you need either:

  • the barcode number from your application form
  • the number on your receipt if you used Check & Send or applied at a Passport Customer Service Centre

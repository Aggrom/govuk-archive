Benefits & credits: Disability Premiums (Income Support)

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Disability premium is an extra amount automatically added to your Income Support if you qualify for it.

You could get at least:

  • £30.35 a week - single person
  • £43.25 a week - couples

You might get more if you qualify for the extra parts of the disability premium.

Any money you get is added to your Income Support payments automatically so usually you don’t have to apply for a disability premium.

Part 2: What you'll get

You can get the basic disability premium and extra amounts on top of this.

The basic disability premium is:

  • £30.35 per week for a single person
  • £43.25 per week for a couple

Extra amounts

Extra premium Rate if you qualify Rate if you and your partner qualify
Severe disability premium £58.20 per week £116.40 per week
Enhanced disability premium £14.80 per week £21.30 per week

Use the benefits adviser to work out how much you can get. ^

How you’re paid

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg your bank account.

From April 2013 a limit will be put on the total amount of benefit that most people aged 16 to 64 can get. This is called a benefit cap.

Part 3: Eligibility

To qualify for a disability premium you must get Income Support.

Also, you or your partner must be under pension credit age and either registered blind or getting any of these benefits:

  • Disability Living Allowance (DLA)
  • Disabled Person’s Tax Credit
  • Attendance Allowance
  • Constant Attendance Allowance
  • War Pensioners Mobility Supplement
  • Severe Disablement Allowance
  • Incapacity Benefit

If you don’t qualify, you may still get the premium if you’ve been unable to work for at least a year.

Use the benefits adviser to check your eligibility.^

Severe disability premium

You may get this if you get the disability premium or the higher pensioner premium.

There are 2 rates of severe disability premium - lower and higher.

Lower rate

You get this if any of the following apply:

  • you live alone
  • no-one gets Carer’s Allowance for looking after you
  • you get the middle or highest care component of DLA
  • you have a partner, you both get the middle or highest rate of the care component of DLA and Carer’s Allowance is paid for only one of you
  • you have a partner, you get the middle or highest rate of the care component of DLA, your partner gets Attendance Allowance and Carer’s Allowance is paid for only one of you
  • you have a partner who is registered blind, you get the middle or highest rate of DLA care component and no-one gets Carer’s Allowance for looking after you

Higher rate

You get this if you have a partner, no-one gets Carer’s Allowance for looking after you and any of the following apply:

  • you both get the middle or highest rate of the care component of DLA
  • you get the middle or highest rate of the care component of DLA and your partner gets Attendance Allowance

Enhanced disability premium

You get this if you’re under 60 and get the highest rate of DLA.

Part 4: How to claim

You don’t have claim disability premium, it’s automatically added to your Income Support if you’re eligible.

If it hasn’t been paid contact your local local Jobcentre Plus.

Appeal a decision

You can appeal a disability premium decision if you’re unhappy with it.

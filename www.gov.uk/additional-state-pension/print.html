Benefits & credits: Additional State Pension

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Additional State Pension is extra money on top of your basic State Pension. It used to be called the State Second Pension or State Earnings-Related Pension Scheme (SERPS). You can choose to contract out of the additional State Pension.

Part 2: What you'll get

You can find how much additional State Pension you can get with a State Pension forecast.

Part 3: Eligibility

You can get Additional State Pension if you’re employed by a company and earning over £5,564.

You may also be able to get Additional State Pension if you’re getting some benefits, for instance, if you’re:

  • looking after children under 12 years old and claiming Child Benefit
  • caring for a sick or disabled person for more than 20 hours a week and claiming Carer’s Credit
  • a registered foster carer and claiming Carer’s Credit
  • getting illness or disability benefits

You don’t normally get Additional State Pension for when you’re:

  • self-employed
  • unemployed
  • in full-time training

You can claim your Additional State Pension when you reach your State Pension age.

Your Additional State Pension will be reduced, perhaps to nothing, if you’re contracted out.

Part 4: How to claim

You get your Additional State Pension when you claim your State Pension.

Part 5: Further information

Contracting out of the Additional State Pension

You can contract out of the Additional State Pension by joining a company, stakeholder or personal pension instead.

Company pension

Both you and your employer will pay lower, reduced rate National Insurance contributions. When you retire, you’ll get a pension from your employer’s (or company) scheme

Stakeholder or personal pension

You won’t pay lower National Insurance contributions. Instead, HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) will pay a rebate of your National Insurance contributions directly into your pension once a year.

Changes to pensions contracted out on a defined contribution (or money purchase) basis from 2012

The rules for contracting out of the Additional State Pension on a defined contribution basis will change in 2012.

If you haven’t already contracted out

The changes mean that you won’t be able to contract out of the Additional State Pension and get one of the following instead:

  • a money-purchase company pension
  • a personal pension
  • a stakeholder pension

You’ll still be able to contract out of the Additional State Pension into a defined benefit company pension.

If you’re already contracted out

If you’re already contracted out into a money-purchase company pension, personal or stakeholder pension, you can continue (until 5 April 2012) to:

  • make your own contributions to your pension
  • benefit from any employer contributions to your pension
  • benefit from lower NI contributions

If you’re contracted out through one of these schemes on 6 April 2012, you’ll automatically be brought back into the Additional State Pension and may, depending on your earnings, begin to contribute to Additional State Pension from this date.

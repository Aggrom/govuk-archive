HIVES are information centres for forces personnel and their families and this is a list of army HIVES in Northern Ireland with contact details that includes phone numbers and opening times.

Aldergrove HIVE

Serving JHC Flying Station Aldergrove and Antrim.

Antrim is located in the heart of Northern Ireland at the northeast corner of Lough Neagh, the largest freshwater lake in the British Isles. An excellent road network gives easy access to the main tourism attractions throughout Northern Ireland. Antrim Borough is a scenically beautiful area with an abundance of forests, rivers, trails and woodlands.

The Lough itself boasts an exceptional variety of wildlife and plants and its gentle beauty offers a unique tranquillity. For an activity holiday, some of the finest sporting and leisure activities in Northern Ireland are on offer. If you are interested in history, then you will certainly not be disappointed by the variety of heritage sites. For shopping you can visit Northern Ireland’s largest international outlet centre, Junction One, on the edge of Antrim town centre.

Aldergrove has many amenities including a gym, hobby centre, various sport and recreational clubs, swimming pool, cinema, library, interactive learning centre, hairdresser, coffee shop, Navy, Army and Air Force Institutes (NAAFI) bar and shop, launderette, gift shop, mess facilities and a full time nursery for children from 4 months to school age. The HIVE provides a free internet, fax and photocopying service.

Ballykinler HIVE

Serving Ballykinler.

Please note: Ballykinler receives an outreach service from the Lisburn HIVE. The outreach service is on site at Ballykinler each Wednesday and Thursday.

Ballykinler is on the southeast coast of Northern Ireland. It is a picturesque rural area right on the coast with a blue flag beach and is overlooked by the Mourne Mountains. Ballykinler village has limited resources. The nearest town is Downpatrick, which is approximately 7 miles away and provides us with a small selection of services, which include: shops, supermarkets, hospital (with A&E), local college and leisure centre. Both Newcastle and Ballynahinch are approximately 15 miles away in opposite directions and again offer a small selection of amenities. Our nearest city is Lisburn (approximately 25 miles), which is an up and coming area with a wide selection of amenities and facilities.

Within the camp there is a selection of facilities which includes: a swimming pool, gym, library, crèche/playgroup, small post office, Dunes Bar, Sandes restaurant, barbers and church. In the centre of the camp near to the family accommodation there is a NAAFI shop and also a families community centre, where many events are held both day and night. There is also a very well equipped youth club for children 4+.

Ballykinler HIVE
Abercorn Bks
BFPO 805

  • Email: lisburnhive@armymail.mod.uk
  • Phone: 02844 610 389

Holywood HIVE

Serving Holywood and Kinnegar.

Palace Barracks is within walking distance of the small community town of Holywood, providing small independent shops, boutiques in addition to supermarkets and chain stores. There are a number of award winning restaurants and hotels. One of the few remaining maypoles in the UK is in the centre of the town which holds a number of festivals throughout the year.

The vibrant and busy city of Belfast is only a short drive providing large shopping centres and high street shops, as well as a wealth of entertainment venues. The Odyssey Centre, built as a Millennium project, houses cinemas, restaurants, pubs, a bowling alley, ice rink, and the Titanic Experience. The centre is also a venue where many visiting top stars perform.

The unit provides many amenities including a gym, swimming pool, and various sport and recreational clubs. There is also a learning centre for personnel and dependants, hairdresser, coffee shop, free internet service (in the HIVE) and a youth club .There is a parent and toddler group and a crèche and nursery school, all inside the barracks. There are the usual mess facilities and Holywood also has a families bar. The BFBS studio broadcasts daily to the Holywood area.

Kinnegar HIVE

Serving Kinnegar Logistic Base.

An outreach service to Kinnegar is provided from Holywood HIVE. The Holywood HIVE information support officer is at Kinnegar on Wednesdays. Call for opening hours.

Kinnegar is situated on the shores of Belfast Lough and on a clear day the views are amazing. The nearest small town is Holywood which has everything you need for your convenience. There is a Tesco 24 hour mega store and a large Sainsbury’s and B&Q slightly out of the town centre. Holywood itself still maintains an old fashioned charm; there is a maypole and a number of bistros and small quirky shops along with very smart restaurants and bars and all the usual high street stores.

Belfast is only a few miles away and has everything that you might expect in a capital city. The city airport is right on your doorstep and Belfast docks are only minutes away for convenient travel back and forwards to the rest of the UK.

Bangor is a Victorian seaside town and is approximately 8 miles away. There are various coastal walks, a marina and a seafront play area and boating lake for the children as well as numerous restaurants and shops.

Personnel who are unaccompanied will be living within Kinnegar itself. All of the married quarters are on Palace Barracks which is a very short journey away.

Palace Bks boasts a full range of facilities eg, HIVE, gymnasium, library, hairdresser, stables and swimming pool. There is a NAAFI which is a well stocked convenience store and there is a family’s bar which is used for various activities.

Kinnegar is rather smaller with fewer facilities. The HIVE is open every day and most evenings and weekend for internet access. A small NAAFI bar and shop also operate most evenings.

Lisburn HIVE

Serving Thiepval Barracks.

The City of Lisburn is just off the M1 motorway and the A1 main arterial route connecting Belfast to Dublin and is the perfect base for visiting both the North and South of Ireland. Set in the beautiful Lagan Valley, Lisburn, which was awarded City Status in 2002, has beautiful scenery and many picturesque towns and villages. The City offers many activities from the multi-screen cinema to the unique LeisurePlex with many slides for all ages.

The unit boasts many amenities. The leisure centre has a swimming pool, gym, climbing wall and sports hall. Clubs include sub aqua, ju-jitsu and climbing. There is a NAAFI shop, gift shop, barbers and forces post office on camp along with an excellent library and education centre for both personnel and dependants. The HIVE offers free broadband internet service, fax, photocopier, laminator, fax-bluey and storybook soldiers facilities. There is a youth club, a parent and toddler group, crèche and nursery. There are the usual mess facilities,bars for the junior ranks and café on the camp.

Theipval Barracks
Lisburn Station

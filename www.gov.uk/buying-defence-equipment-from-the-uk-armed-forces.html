Introduction

The procurement of well maintained defence equipment from the UK armed forces offers an extremely attractive, cost effective alternative to the purchase of new equipment. Through our relationship with UK industry, the Disposals Services Authority (DSA) Capital Equipment Sales team can also facilitate through-life support packages to provide a complete capability.

Equipment is only available for sale on a government to government basis.

Land equipment sales: COBRA (Counter Battery Radar)

Details are:

  • navigation/coverage - COBRA INU error is less than 10 metres in 20 kilometres; INU also provides antenna attitude information; detection coverage is 90 degrees out to more than 40 kilometres, corresponding to 1,200 square kilometres
  • antenna technology - fully-active phased array; electronic scanning in azimuth and elevation (phase/phase); 2780 T/R modules containing MMICs
  • antenna sector - electronic scan 90 degrees, within 270 degrees
  • processing - agility in waveform and frequency (C-Band); digital pulse compression/Doppler filtering; clutter map, CFAR and censor map; target recognition and weapon classification; battery location, impact locations, battery width and orientation
  • range - more than 40 kilometres
  • shelter - shell fragment protection using Aramid Laminat Layer
  • air conditioner - controlled air temperature with available gas, chemical and particle filtration (NBC)
  • power supply - diesel generator, 47 kVA
  • carrier vehicle - FODEN 4x4, but radar unit can be mounted on other configurations

DSA capital equipment sales

The Capital Equipment Sales team, part of the Defence Equipment & Support (DE&S), manages the sale and transfer of land, sea and air defence equipment from the UK Ministry of Defence to foreign governments worldwide on behalf of the Disposals Services Authority (DSA).

Key business objectives

  • sales - to measure the gross revenue generated from the disposal of surplus government owned equipment, stores and repayment business and to achieve a net return of £34 million
  • service provision - to achieve 90% success in the collection of surplus assets
  • delivery of outputs - to ensure that the DSA is at or above the Public Sector benchmark for customer satisfaction
  • environmental - to ensure that 90% of surplus arisings are either sold for further use or recycled

DSA contractors

Below is some key information about the contractors used by the DSA for the sale of all MOD surplus items.

Azcom Business Solutions Ltd

Azcom Business Solutions Ltd specialises in environmentally friendly business clearance and disposal services of unwanted furniture and equipment arising from office reorganisations, consolidations and relocation assignments. We clear and relocate office furniture, equipment and waste from offices, stores, factories and warehouses all over the UK for public and private sector clients. From one item to whole sites we provide these services efficiently, cost effectively and with full accountability.

Disposals Services Ltd

Shipping British military surplus equipment worldwide, DSL (Field Textiles Ltd) specialises in army surplus clothing and textile accessories, army boots, army combats, ceremonial and paintball clothing, hunting, shooting and fishing kit and military camping equipment. In addition a range of medical and dental equipment is also available through specialist medical sub-contractor Lundy.

DSL (Ramco) have been handling surplus equipment since 1992, having become a well established name within the government surplus sector. We offer to the public a wide and diverse range of quality surplus miscellaneous and catering equipment, small vessels, gifts and memorabilia. These are available to purchase direct or through our regular tender sales and an ever expanding ebay store.

DSA Connect

The Disposals Services Authority (DSA) founded in 1994, manages all disposals for the MOD. Income produced from the sale of surplus equipment is then fed back to the MOD.

The DSA has spent the past 16 years developing its skills in disposals management and its network of specialist disposal contractors. The experience and facility of the DSA is second to none when it comes to organising disposals and identifying income from items which could often be deemed either worthless or cost negative.

The benefits of the DSA, a pan-government service, are now available to the public sector. If you are responsible for the disposals in your organisation then DSA Connect will help you access those benefits.

Military Spares UK (MSUK) Ltd

MSUK offers an extensive range of equipment specialising in components and spares for the military and civilian Aviation and Land based equipment markets.

MSUK also offer a one-stop shop covering all our customers naval spares and material needs either from our extensive inventory or commercial sources plus a full spectrum of supply services to deliver real benefit to our customers.

Office Green

Since the early 1990s, Office Green has been the UK’s leading office products waste solution provider and has truly been ‘at the heart of recycling’.

Our ethos is that recycling should be easy to encourage the majority to participate by eliminating the hassles that deter many people from recycling.

To this end we strive to provide the simplest and most up to date legislative recycling solutions, backed up by a friendly and knowledgeable customer services team that can help all our partners and clients with minimal effort make a difference to the world of tomorrow.

VEBEG

VEBEG disposes of surplus equipment for the German government.

Veolia Environmental Services

Veolia Environmental Services is the leading recycling and waste management company in the UK. We provide our commercial, industrial and local authority customers with sustainable recycling and waste services to minimise their impact on the environment.

Witham (Specialist Vehicles) Ltd

Witham (Specialist Vehicles) Ltd is one of the largest companies in Europe, specialising only in the sale and marketing of purely ex-Ministry of Defence vehicles, plant and equipment.

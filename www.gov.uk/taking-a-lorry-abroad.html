Quick answer Taking a lorry abroad

You need a valid passport, the correct driving licence and must comply with EU rules on drivers’ hours and tachograph use.

Passports

All vehicle drivers, passengers and any crew members must have a valid passport.

Visas

If you hold a EU passport you don’t need a visa for entry into other EU member states, but you may need a visa if you’re travelling beyond the EU.

Drivers’ hours and tachographs

If you’re driving a goods vehicle within the EU you must comply with EU rules on drivers’ hours and tachograph use.

However, working time rules vary and there are certain tachograph exemptions.

Download ‘VOSA’s guide to drivers’ hours rules for goods vehicles in GB and Europe’ (PDF, 4.57MB)

The International Driving Permit (IDP)

You’ll need an IDP to drive in certain countries. This shows the details from your driving licence in other languages. It includes:

  • your driving entitlements
  • your validity periods

Check whether you need a permit for the country you’re travelling to with the AA or the RAC.

You still need to carry your driving licence at all times - an IDP is not a replacement.

Carrying goods

If you’re using your lorry to transport goods within certain EU member states, you’ll need to check the rules on cabotage.

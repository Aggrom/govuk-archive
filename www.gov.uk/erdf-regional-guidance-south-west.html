ERDF regional guidance: South West

Introduction

The ERDF is a funding stream which is allocated to regions by the European Union to stimulate regional economies.

The South West is the only English region to qualify for both convergence funding (in Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly) and competitveness and employment funding across the rest of the region.

The ERDF South West competitiveness region covers the unitary authority areas of Devon, Plymouth, Torbay, Somerset, Bournemouth, Dorset, Poole, Bristol, Bath and North East Somerset, North Somerset, South Gloucestershire, Gloucestershire, Wiltshire and Swindon. The area qualifies for ERDF investment for the 2007 to 2013 programming period of €124.7 million.

Priorities

The South West competitiveness operational programme document (see ‘Programme documents’ below) outlines the investment priorities for the competitiveness and employment programme in the region. It focuses on 3 main priorities:

Priority 1: innovation and knowledge

The aim of this priority is to improve the region’s overall performance with regard to innovation and close the intra-regional gap in innovation performance. The priority has a key role to play in maximising the economic contribution from the region’s higher and further education institutions and helping companies exploit their own ideas and innovations.

Priority 2: enterprise and growth

The aim of this priority is to increase the contribution that new businesses make to the economy and reduce the intra-regional disparities in enterprise rates. This priority has a key role to play in addressing the low-earnings, low-skilled nature of the economy.

Priority 3: urban enterprise

The aim of this priority is to find new approaches to addressing the problems in the region’s most deprived neighbourhoods through enterprise creation. Resources will be focused on activities to benefit the deprived neighbourhoods of Bristol, Plymouth and Torbay.

Programme documents

Operational programme and annexes

The South West competitiveness programme aims to increase the prosperity of the region by supporting enterprises and individuals in delivering projects which drive up productivity and competitiveness. The operational programme sets out the strategy, aims and targets for the programme.

ERDF partners seek to ensure that all commissioning and investment decisions are aligned to the operational programme priorities and contribute to achieving its targets.

Annual implementation reports

Regulations set down by the European Commission (EC) governing the management of the ERDF programmes require the programme partners to produce a yearly progress report known as an annual implementation report. Each report covers a calendar year and includes progress made during that period in areas such as:

  • finance
  • communications
  • achievements
  • monitoring and any programme-related issues arising during the year

The programme monitoring committee (local monitoring committee after 2011) agrees the content of the report and it is then submitted to the EC by 30 June of the following year. Reports are scrutinised to ensure the programme is being effectively managed. Once each report has been accepted by the EC, it will be published below.

Local ERDF guidance

Project guidance

The South West Programme Delivery Team provide guidance and support to project applicants and grant recipients in relation to ERDF requirements throughout the course of a project’s development.

All projects are required to fulfil a number of requirements relating to the cross-cutting themes of equality and diversity and the environment. Guidance relating to these requirements is available below.

From 1 April 2012, the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) adopted a standardised approach to the management and delivery of the ERDF programmes across England. This includes the introduction of a new suite of documentation - including forms and guidance - which projects approved to receive funding after 1 April 2012 will need to use and follow.

For projects required to use the new documentation see the guide on ERDF: national guidance.

All forms and guidance in existence before 1 April 2012 that ERDF-supported projects have used or are currently using are available from your local programme delivery team.

Please ensure that you are using the correct forms and guidance for your project. If you have any questions, please contact the South West Programme Delivery Team at SWcomp.ERDFenquiries@communities.gsi.gov.uk.

Details of the outputs that projects supported by the North East ERDF programme are required to deliver are set out in the document below.

Local management committee

The South West Competitiveness ERDF local management committee is the partnership body which provides strategic leadership of the programme. It sets direction, helps drive the programme to achieve its targets and monitors performance and compliance.

Following a change in the delivery arrangements for European programmes in England, the local management committee replaced the former Convergence Joint ERDF and European Social Fund (ESF) programme monitoring committee. ESF duties previously undertaken by the joint programme monitoring committee are now undertaken by a single national competitiveness monitoring committee administered by the Department for Work & Pensions. The inaugural meeting of the local management committee took place on 4 November 2011.

Membership of the local management committee includes representatives from local authorities, the private sector, co-financing organisations, the voluntary sector, trades unions, skills, environment and equalities interest. The group is chaired by the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) and its deputy chair is the Chairman of the West of England local enterprise partnership representing the local enterprise partnerships in the competitiveness area.

Local management committee sub-group

The local management committee has one sub-group, the Competitiveness Operations Group, which makes recommendations about investment decisions and delivery methods.

Competitiveness and employment joint ERDF and ESF monitoring committee

Prior to the introduction of local management committees, South West competitiveness partners provided programme management through the South West competitiveness and employment joint ERDF and ESF monitoring committee. The final meeting took place on 17 June 2011.

The committee was set up in accordance with European regulations, and members were drawn from a range of partners across the public, private, and community and voluntary sectors. Alongside the ERDF and ESF managing authorities, the committee had a key strategic role in implementing the South West competitiveness programme by monitoring the quality and effectiveness of investments made.

Approved minutes for the South West Competitiveness and Employment Programme Joint ERDF Monitoring Committee and ESF Regional Committee meetings can be obtained from the South West Programme Delivery Team.

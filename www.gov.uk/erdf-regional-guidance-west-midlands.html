Introduction

The European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) is a funding stream which is allocated to regions by the European Union to stimulate regional economies.

The West Midlands is one of 9 regions to qualify for Competitiveness and Employment funding.

Under the 2007 to 2013 programme, the region can receive €400 million of investment.

Priorities

There are currently 2 live calls for applications under the West Midlands ERDF programme:

  • priority 2: stimulating enterprise development
  • priority 3: achieving sustainable urban development

Further information is available in the guide to applying for ERDF funding.

The West Midlands operational programme document (see ‘Programme documents’ below) outlines the investment priorities for the region. It focuses on 4 main priorities:

Priority 1: promoting innovation and research and development

Aims to improve the region’s capacity to translate innovation into commercial outcomes that result in a step change in business productivity and performance. A particular focus is on the region’s high class science base, notably its universities and research institutions.

Priority 2: stimulating enterprise development

Aims to increase enterprise in the West Midlands in order to improve regional economic performance. This is being achieved through the provision of tailored business support together with a limited range of integrated access to finance measures.

Priority 3: achieving sustainable urban development

Aims to address the significant concentrations of economic need in certain areas of the region and seeks to generate major employment opportunities accessible to those communities.

Priority 4: developing inter-regional activity

Aims to support the 3 main priorities in tackling problems that are applicable to the region and to maximise learning from current and future inter-regional activity by developing mutually beneficial relationships with partners in the EU via:

  • study visits
  • work shadowing
  • exchange of professional expertise
  • workshops
  • case studies
  • conferences

In addition a fifth priority, ‘technical assistance’ provides a resource which supports and contributes towards delivering the overall regional strategy by assisting the necessary activities associated with programme management. This includes programme preparation, implementation, monitoring, evaluation and publicity.

ERDF projects

The West Midlands ERDF programme 2007 to 2013 provides funding to a range of initiatives and projects that stimulate local growth and regeneration, create jobs and support local businesses.

Business support guide

To help local businesses, ERDF funding is awarded to business support providers, who can directly help individual businesses. To capture details of all the current business support initiatives being delivered in the region and to provide guidance on where businesses seeking assistance can go for more information, a West Midlands ERDF business support guide has been developed.

A searchable speadsheet is also available which businesses can use to find the information directly relevant to their requirements. The spreadsheet allows businesses to search by category and type of business support.

Local management committee

Partnership plays a key role in the delivery and management of the ERDF programme. To reflect this, 2 groups have been established, each with a specific role.

The ERDF programme for the West Midlands is overseen by a local management committee (formerly called the programme monitoring committee). This is composed of senior representatives of the bodies of the regional partnership with the membership reflecting the sectoral and sub-regional interests in the programme.

To assist the committee in effectively carrying out its full range of strategic and regulatory functions, it is supported by a sub-committee.

The committee is responsible for ensuring the programme’s overall strategic direction, driving it forward so that it meets agreed targets and redirecting the work of the programme as and when necessary over its lifetime. Its key focus is on setting programme direction and strategic review.

Local management sub-committee

The aim of the sub-committee is to support the local management committee in carrying out its responsibilities. Specifically, the principal role and function of the group is to agree and commission, in partnership, the necessary activity to ensure that the different priority areas of the programme may be fulfilled and activity delivered.

The sub-committee, chaired by the deputy chair of the local management committee, will input into the investment frameworks which helps them deliver each priority and meet its objectives, stating the route for project development, implementation details and the timing and sequence of events.

The group is also tasked with raising awareness of the ERDF programme and European funding opportunities and encouraging partners in the sub-regions to apply for ERDF where this is a strategic need.

Quick answer Passports: change your name and personal details

Not what you're looking for? ↓

To change your name or other personal details on your passport, you need to renew your passport and send supporting documents and evidence. Sometimes the Identity and Passport Service (IPS) may need to contact you for more information.

When you need to renew your passport

You’ll need a new passport if you’ve changed your:

  • name (by marriage, civil partnership or deed poll)
  • appearance and you can’t be recognised in your current passport photo (eg because of major facial surgery or trauma, piercings or tattoos)
  • gender (even if your name remains the same)
  • nationality status (eg from a British subject to a British citizen)

You follow the same application process for renewing your passport, but you’ll need to include specific documents with your application form - check below for what you need.

You get up to 9 months extra time on a new passport to cover time left on your old one.

Sign your application form using your new name.

When you don’t need to renew your passport

You don’t need a new passport if you:

  • change your job, title, address or contact details
  • emigrate
  • change your marital status but keep your name
  • get nationality of another country as well as your British nationality

If you’re getting married or forming a civil partnership

Decide whether to change your name before or after the ceremony - the name on your passport must be the same one you use when you book your travel.

Before the ceremony

You can change the name on your passport up to 3 months before the ceremony. Your old passport will be cancelled.

The new passport is ‘postdated’ - you can’t use it before the ceremony.

Some countries won’t issue visas for post-dated passports - check with the country’s consulate.

You must send a standard passport application form, along with the form ‘Passports for newly weds and civil partners’, signed by the religious minister or registrar who will conduct the ceremony.

Download ‘Passports for newly weds and civil partners form’ (PDF, 112KB)

Download ‘Passports for newly weds and civil partners leaflet’ (PDF, 144KB)

After the ceremony

Send the standard passport application along with your marriage or civil partnership certificate.

Any unexpired visas in your old passport may become invalid - check with the country’s consulate.

Going back to your unmarried (maiden) name

You must send with your application form:

  • your birth certificate
  • a signed statement saying you’ve gone back to your maiden name for all purposes
  • a decree absolute showing both names if you’ve divorced
  • a marriage certificate showing both names

If you can’t be recognised in your current passport photo

Make sure that your application form and 1 of your passport photos is countersigned by an appropriate person.

Titles you can use on your passport

You can ask to include titles on your passport like:

  • professional titles - eg doctor, judge, minister of religion, professor or QC
  • honours or military decorations
  • JP if you’re a magistrate

Put the details in section 2 of the application form and enclose evidence of your title.

Change of gender

Send one of the following with your application and supporting documents:

  • a letter from a doctor or chartered psychologist who practices in gender dysphoria stating that you have a need to live in a different gender, and evidence of your change of name (eg your deed poll)
  • a gender recognition certificate
  • a new birth or adoption certificate showing your acquired gender

You can download more advice about passports for transsexual and transgender people.

Download ‘Applying for a passport - additional information for transgender and transsexual customers’ (PDF, 235KB)

Changing your name by deed poll

Send your change of name deed with your application form.

Name changes that don’t match birth, naturalisation or registration certificates

You must send extra evidence with your application. The IPS will need to see documents showing both the date of and reason for your name change.

You must send original documents, not copies. Laminated documents can be used as proof for name changes.

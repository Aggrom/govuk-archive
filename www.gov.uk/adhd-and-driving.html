Quick answer Attention deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD) and driving

You must tell DVLA if you have attention deficit/hyperactivity disorder (ADHD).

You can be fined up to £1,000 if you don’t tell DVLA about a medical condition that affects your driving. If you’re involved in an accident, you may be prosecuted.

Car or motorcycle licence

You must tell DVLA. Fill in form M1 and send it to DVLA. The address is on the form.

Download ‘M1 - Confidential medical information’ (PDF, 252KB)

Bus, coach or lorry licence

You must tell DVLA. Fill in form M1V and send it to DVLA. The address is on the form.

Download ‘M1V - Confidential medical information’ (PDF, 243KB)

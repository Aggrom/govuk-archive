Benefits & credits: Guardian's Allowance

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get Guardian’s Allowance if you’re bringing up a child whose parents have died.

Depending on the circumstances, you could also be eligible if there is one surviving parent.

You’re paid Guardian’s Allowance at a rate of £15.55 a week. You get it on top of Child Benefit.

You must tell the Guardian’s Allowance Unit about certain changes to your circumstances.

Guardian’s Allowance is a tax-free payment and isn’t counted as income for other benefits. However, it will count towards the benefit cap when this is introduced in April 2013.

Part 2: What you'll get

From April 2013 a limit will be put on the total amount of benefit that most people aged 16 to 64 can get. This is called a benefit cap.

You’ll get £15.55 a week per child.

This is on top of your Child Benefit payments.

Guardian’s Allowance is paid with your Child Benefit every 4 weeks into your bank account.

Part 3: Eligibility

To get Guardian’s Allowance all of the following must apply:

  • you’re bringing up someone else’s child
  • the child’s parents are dead (see conditions for one surviving parent below)
  • you qualify for Child Benefit
  • one of the parents was born in the UK (or was living in the UK since the age of 16 for at least 52 weeks in any 2-year period)

One parent survives

You could get Guardian’s Allowance if one of the following is true:

  • you don’t know where the surviving parent is
  • the parents were divorced or their civil partnership had dissolved, the surviving parent doesn’t have custody and isn’t maintaining the child and there isn’t a court order in place saying they should
  • the parents weren’t married, the mother has died and the father is unknown
  • the surviving parent will be in prison for at least 2 years from the date of death of the other parent
  • the surviving parent is in a hospital by court order

Use the benefits adviser to check your eligibility.

Part 4: How to claim

To avoid losing money, claim Guardian’s Allowance as soon as the child comes to live with you.

  1. Download and complete the claim form (PDF, 201KB).

  2. Send it to the Guardian’s Allowance Unit with the child’s birth certificate and the parents’ death certificates (or certificate if one parent has died) - send originals.

Part 5: Further information

Changes to your circumstances

The following changes could affect your entitlement to Guardian’s Allowance or stop your payments:

  • the child goes to live with someone else
  • you go abroad
  • the child leaves full-time education or approved training
  • you find out where the surviving parent is
  • the surviving parent comes out of hospital or prison (or has their sentence shortened)
  • the surviving parent makes a payment towards their child’s upkeep
  • you change your bank details or move house

You must tell the Guardian’s Allowance Unit about any of these changes straightaway.

Effect on other benefits

Guardian’s Allowance doesn’t count as income if you’re claiming tax credits, Income Support, income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance or income related Employment and Support Allowance.

You might be able to get other benefits too - use the benefits adviser tool to find out what you could get.

Appealing the decision

Contact the Guardian’s Allowance Unit within a month of the decision and ask them to check your claim again. If you’re still not happy you can appeal again - your decision letter will explain how.

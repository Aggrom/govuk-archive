Expenses and benefits: club membership, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer covering the cost of club membership for your employees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

What you have to report and pay depends on who arranges the membership, and who pays the club.

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to pay or report anything to HMRC if both the following apply:

  • you arrange your employee’s membership and pay the club directly
  • the employee earns at a rate of £8,500 or less a year

Part 3: What to report and pay

If the club membership isn’t exempt, you must report it to HMRC, and you may have to deduct and pay tax and National Insurance.

This depends on who arranges the membership and who pays the club.

If you pay fees for an employee

For employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year, or directors, report the amount you pay on form P11D, section M and pay Class 1A National Insurance.

If your employee arranges membership but you pay the fees

Who gets the benefit What to do What to deduct or pay
Employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year Report on form P9D, section A(2) Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) through payroll
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Report on form P11D, section B Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) through payroll
Directors Report on form P11D, section B Deduct and pay Class 1 National Insurance (but not PAYE tax) through payroll

If your employee pays fees but you reimburse them

This counts as earnings. Add the amount to your employee’s other earnings, and deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance through payroll.

Licence End-of-life vehicles producer registration

Apply for this licence

Get started on the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) website

Overview

To market any passenger carrying vehicle that can seat eight passengers excluding the driver (M1 category) or any goods vehicle having a laden mass up to 3.5 tonnes (N1 category), as a manufacturer or professional importer, you need to register with the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS). You will need to declare responsibility for the end-of-life treatment of your brands of vehicles.

Your application must be made in writing and contain the following information:

  • the name of your business
  • the name, address and telephone number of your registered office or principal place of business
  • the address for serving notices - if different from the above
  • a description of the vehicles that you are declaring responsibility for placing on the market

Your information will be entered onto a public register, which will be updated with whether you meet your end-of-life vehicle (ELV) requirements. You must comply with all ongoing ELV requirements, in particular:

  • using the allowed levels of heavy metals in your vehicles - eg cadmium, lead, mercury and hexavalent chromium
  • marking and coding plastic and rubber materials for dismantling and recovering purposes
  • providing a BIS-approved ‘free take-back system’ - you must apply to BIS for approval of your system (including all relevant details) within six months of placing your vehicles on the market
  • meeting the reuse, recovery and recycling targets for scrapped vehicles - to recover 85 per cent (by weight) and recycle at least 80 per cent (by weight) of your ELVs

Conditions

You must keep technical documents to show compliance with all ongoing requirements for four years from the date you put the vehicles, materials and components on the market. The Vehicle Certification Agency is the enforcement authority for this, and can ask to see these documents, at any time, to prove your compliance.

You must ensure that your take-back system is reasonably accessible to any person who owns a vehicle for which you have declared responsibility. However, you may make alternative arrangements - if necessary - for the collection of ELVs where not accessible.

You must also submit details to BIS of the reuse, recovery and recycling rates that you achieve on an annual basis. You must return this information to BIS by 1st July for each preceding year. Any compliance information you provide to BIS will be kept on a public register for no longer than four years.

If you produce fewer than 500 vehicles a year in each European Union member state, you only need to comply with the restrictions on heavy metals. However, you must still keep technical documents to prove compliance.

BIS may at any time enter and inspect your premises to check compliance with any ELV requirements.

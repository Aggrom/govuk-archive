Benefits & credits: Jobseeker's Allowance

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get £56.25, £71 or £111.45 a week Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) to help you while you’re looking for work.

How much you get depends on your circumstances and the type of JSA you qualify for.

To qualify you usually have to be over 18 years old, able and available for work.

You must apply for JSA with Jobcentre Plus and go to an interview to agree what steps you’ll take to find work.

To keep getting JSA you must report to Jobcentre Plus every 2 weeks about how your job search is going.

Part 2: What you'll get

There are 2 types of Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA). Jobcentre Plus will work out which type you’ll get and how much you’ll be paid.

Contribution-based JSA

Age Weekly amount
16 to 24 Up to £56.25
25 or over Up to £71

You get this if you’ve got enough National Insurance contributions (NICs). Usually, self-employed Class 2 NICs don’t count.

Income-based JSA

Status Weekly amount
Single (under 25) Up to £56.25
Single (25 or over) Up to £71
Couples and civil partnerships (both aged 18 or over) Up to £111.45
Lone parent (18 or over) Up to £71
Lone parent (under 18) Up to £56.25

You get this if you’ve not paid enough National Insurance (or you’ve only paid self-employed Class 2 NICs) and you’re on a low income.

Use the benefits adviser to work out how much you can get.

How you’re paid

Usually payments are made every 2 weeks and are backdated to the date you made your claim.

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg a bank account.

To keep getting JSA you must report to Jobcentre Plus every 2 weeks about how your job search is going.

From April 2013 a limit will be put on the total amount of benefit that most people aged 16 to 64 can get. This is called a benefit cap.

Part 3: Eligibility

To qualify for Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) you must be:

  • looking for work, able to work and available for work
  • aged 18 or over but below State Pension age - there are some exceptions for 16 to 17 year olds
  • working less than 16 hours a week
  • living in England, Scotland or Wales

Extra rules for income-based JSA

Usually, to qualify for income-based JSA:

  • your partner (if you have one) must work less than 24 hours a week
  • you and your partner should have less than £16,000 in savings

Your income can affect how much you get too. This includes money from savings (over £5999), pensions and your household income.

Use the benefits adviser to check your eligibility.

16 to 17 years olds

JSA isn’t usually paid to 16 or 17 year olds or people in full-time education, except in special cases. Contact your local Jobcentre Plus for advice.

Your circumstances change

Tell Jobcentre Plus if your circumstances change as this can affect how much money you get. For example, you start working or your income changes.

Volunteering won’t affect your JSA but you must report that you’re doing it.

To keep getting JSA you must report to Jobcentre Plus every 2 weeks about how your job search is going.

Part 4: How to claim

You can claim Jobseeker’s Allowance:

  • online
  • by phone - if you can’t apply online

What you need to know

You must attend an interview at your local Jobcentre Plus to to complete your claim. If you apply online, you’ll get a text or phone call within 2 days to arrange this.

Joint claims

You must make a joint claim with your partner if:

  • you both want income-based JSA
  • at least one of you is aged 18 or over
  • both of you are under State Pension age
  • neither of you are responsible for a child

If you’re not sure whether you can get JSA, contact Jobcentre Plus.

Appeal a decision

You can appeal the decision about your JSA if you’re unhappy with it.

Part 5: Further information

Jobseeker’s Allowance (JSA) interview

To complete your claim you have to go to an interview at your local Jobcentre Plus.

At the interview you have to agree things like:

  • what steps you can to take to find work - eg improving your skills
  • how you can improve your chances of getting a job - eg get help on writing a CV, preparing for interviews, looking for work

What you agree to is recorded in a ‘Jobseeker’s agreement’. Every 2 weeks you’ll have a review meeting to check if your doing the things in your agreement.

If you don’t attend your interview or your review meetings you won’t get JSA.

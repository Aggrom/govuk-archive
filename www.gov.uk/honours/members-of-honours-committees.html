Part 6: Members of honours committees

Honours committees are made up of senior civil servants (â€˜official membersâ€™) and people who are independent of government (â€˜independent membersâ€™). All honours committees have a majority of members who are independent.

Each honours committee has an independent chairperson. A representative from 10 Downing Street is invited to attend all committee meetings

Honours committees review honours nominations for people involved in specific activities (like arts and media or sport) which are then sent to the main committee.

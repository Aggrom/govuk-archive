Defence and the National Security Strategy

The Armed Forces are a fundamental component of the UK's National Security Strategy.

Contents

  1. Introduction
  2. See more like this

Introduction

The Armed Forces are the ultimate guarantors of national security and, if necessary, they must be ready to protect the United Kingdom and its interests overseas.

The Government will continue to use a variety of levers to promote international security, including military power, and there are likely to be circumstances in the future where British forces will again be required to intervene decisively abroad. Such interventions will only be successful if they are part of a wider political strategy to stabilise and rebuild countries damaged by conflict.

Priorities for Defence include:

  • ensuring that the Armed Forces are equipped with the capabilities they need for the range of operations they have to carry out from war fighting and peacekeeping through to humanitarian tasks and conflict prevention operations
  • strengthening support for service personnel and their families (housing, medical, pay and conditions) in the light of the recommendations in the forthcoming Command Paper on Service Personnel
  • maintaining the ability of our Armed Forces to reconfigure quickly to face changing threats
  • ensuring that NATO and the EU’s European Security and Defence Policy (ESDP) evolve in a complementary manner, developing capabilities for intervention, peacekeeping, stabilisation and reconstruction

Current operational priorities include:

  • promoting an Afghan-led security effort within ISAF, with continuing NATO support, while simultaneously giving increased emphasis to political and economic means of undermining the insurgency and the narcotics trade - this will require more effective coordination of all the international actors involved;
  • improving the ISAF’s methods of communicating with key stakeholders, both within Afghanistan and abroad, and encouraging Afghanistan’s neighbours to have a greater stake in the creation of a stable, prosperous and secure Afghanistan. Simultaneously, the UK will seek a more equitable sharing of the military and financial burden of rebuilding Afghanistan among the international community; and
  • in Iraq, continuing to support the process of political reconciliation and economic reconstruction and transferring security responsibility to Iraqi control. That will include continuing to support the Iraqi security forces in Basra province and maximising economic development in the region to give the people of Basra a stake in their future.

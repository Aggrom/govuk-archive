Quick answer Corporation Tax rates

Your Corporation Tax rate depends on how much profit your company makes and may change on April 1 each year.

Rate From April 1 2011 From April 1 2012 From April 1 2013
Profits £300,000 or less 20% 20% To be announced
Profits above £300,000 26% 24% 23%

Profits below £1.5 million

If your company’s profit is between £300,000 and £1.5 million, you can claim ‘Marginal Relief’. This is a sliding-scale reduction on your company’s tax bill. It means that the Corporation Tax rate rises gradually for profits between £300,000 and £1.5 million.

Work out your Corporation Tax using HMRC’s Marginal Relief calculator.

You claim Marginal Relief when you send your Company Tax Return to HMRC.

Profits above £1.5 million

Your company pays the main rate on profits over £1.5 million. There is no Marginal Relief.

If your accounting period involves a change of Corporation Tax rate

Work out how many days each rate applied, then work out the tax due for each. For example, if your accounting period is 1 March 2011 to 28 February 2012, you pay:

  • the 2010-11 rate for 31 days (1 March to March 31)
  • the 2011-12 rate for 334 days (from April 1 to February 28)

If your accounting period is shorter than 12 months

The thresholds of £300,000 and £1.5 million are for a whole year, so these are reduced in line with your accounting period. For example, if your accounting period is 6 months, the £300,000 threshold is halved to £150,000.

Associated companies

Associated companies (where one company controls others) share thresholds. For example, if 1 company owns 5 others, divide the thresholds by 6 when working out the Corporation Tax for each company.

Ring fence companies

There are different rates for ring fence companies (companies involved in oil extraction or oil rights in the UK or UK continental shelf).

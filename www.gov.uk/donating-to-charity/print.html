Donating to charity, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Different ways to donate

Most charities let you make a donation through their website. If you pay tax you can give direct from your pay packet, pension, or when you make a self assessment tax return. You can also leave money or assets (like land or buildings) to charity in your will.

The Charities Aid Foundation (CAF) website can help you find a charity and make a donation.

Part 2: Donating straight from your wages or pension

If you pay tax through Pay As You Earn (PAYE), ‘Payroll Giving’ lets you reduce the cost to you of making regular gifts to UK charities.

If your employer, company or personal pension provider runs a Payroll Giving Scheme, you simply tell them to make the donation from your wages or pension before taking off any tax. 

Example: You pay tax at the basic rate of 20% and make a monthly donation of £10. That means you save £2 tax (20% of £10). The actual cost of the donation to you is £8.

For more help you can contact the HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) Charities Helpline.

Part 3: Donating through your self assessment tax return

Choosing a charity

To use Self Assessment Giving, enter a charity reference code on the ‘Giving your tax repayment to charity’ form that comes with your tax return. To get a charity’s reference code, you can:

  • search the HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) online database
  • telephone the HMRC Charities Helpline
  • ask the charity

You can only choose one charity to give your repayment to each year.

If your charity isn’t listed, you’ll have to make a donation yourself once you’ve received your refund.

How much can you give?

You can give some or all of your tax refund. If you don’t want to donate the whole amount you can set an upper limit. If your repayment is more than this, the rest will be paid to you.

HMRC will let you know how much your repayment is and when it was sent to the charity.

How to make your donation

Fill in form SA100.

You can do this online if you use HMRC’s Self Assessment online service.

Part 4: Leaving gifts to charity in your will

If you will pay Inheritance Tax, you could pay less by choosing to give money to charity.

You can either leave a fixed amount, or some or all of what’s left after other gifts have been given out.

You can do this through your will. Make sure it’s clear which charity you want to receive your gift.

Part 5: Keeping records

You must keep records of your donations to charity to make sure that you claim the correct amount of tax relief and pay the right amount of tax.

For every tax year, you should keep:

  • details of Gift Aid donations – showing the date, the amount and the charities involved
  • legal documents showing the sale or transfer of assets (eg land or buildings) to charity
  • any documents from a charity asking you to sell land or shares on its behalf

Allocation of accommodation: guidance for local housing authorities in England

New statutory guidance on social housing allocations for local authorities in England.

This replaces Business Link

This guide helps take advantage of the provisions detailed in the Localism Act 2011 which gives local authorities the freedom to manage their own waiting lists, and make it easier for them to move existing social tenants to more suitable accommodation.

Authorities are encouraged to make use of the existing flexibilities within the allocation legislation to ensure that social homes go to people who need and deserve them the most, such as hard-working families and members of the armed forces.

This new guide replaces all previous guidance on social housing allocations and is an important part of the government’s commitment to making the social housing system more flexible, more responsive, and fairer for all.

Benefits & credits: Community Care Grants

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get a Community Care Grant to help you:

  • return to the community after being in care
  • stay in your home rather than go into care
  • care for a prisoner or young offender when they leave prison on temporary licence
  • pay for certain travel expenses, eg going to a family funeral or visiting someone who’s ill

How much money you get depends on your circumstances - there’s no maximum amount.

Any money you get doesn’t have to be paid back and won’t affect your other benefits.

Part 2: What you'll get

How much money you get depends on your circumstances - there’s no maximum. However a grant of less than £30 can only be paid for travel costs or living expenses.

Savings

You may get less if you have savings:

  • over £500 - where you and your partner are aged under 60
  • over £1,000 - where you and or your partner are aged over 60

Savings over these limits will reduce what you can get, eg savings of £501 will reduce your grant by £1.

How you’re paid

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg a bank account. You may be asked to provide receipts if the grant is £500 or more.

Part 3: Eligibility

You may be able to get a grant if you’re getting one of these benefits (or are likely to get one in the next 6 weeks if you’re due to leave care):

  • Income Support
  • income-related Employment and Support Allowance
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Pension Credit

Also, one of the following must apply, you:

  • are leaving residential or institutional care - eg a care home or prison
  • are being resettled into a new home by your council or a voluntary organisation following an unsettled period in your life
  • are looking after someone who is ill, disabled or just out of prison
  • need help to stay in your home and not go into residential care or hospital
  • need help with expenses to go to a relative’s funeral or visit someone who is ill
  • need help because you or your family face exceptional pressure - eg because of a family breakdown or illness

You might get a Crisis Loan if you’re not eligible for a grant.

Part 4: How to claim

Fill in form SF300 and send it to your local Jobcentre Plus.

You can get the form from your local Jobcentre Plus or download it. The form includes notes to help you fill it in.

Download ‘Form SF300 - Community Care Grant and Crisis Loan applications’ (PDF, 264KB)

If you’re unhappy with a decision

You can ask for the decision about your grant to be reviewed if you’re unhappy with it. To do this, send a signed letter to Jobcentre Plus within 28 days of their decision letter. Say why you don’t agree with the decision.

If you’re still unhappy after the review you can ask the independent Social Fund Inspector to review the decision.

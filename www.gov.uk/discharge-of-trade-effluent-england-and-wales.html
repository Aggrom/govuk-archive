Licence Discharge of trade effluent (England and Wales)

Overview

You’ll may need an environmental permit if you are discharging trade effluent into inland waters or the sea.

If the effluent you’re discharging could harm the environment, then you’ll need to get an environmental permit from the Environment Agency. These permits cover:

  • rivers, lakes or streams
  • groundwater (such as boreholes)
  • estuaries
  • coastal waters

Some low-risk activities don’t need a permit, like the discharge of clean, uncontaminated water. However, they may still need to be registered with the Environment Agency, who will issue you with an exemption. Most exemptions are free.

Conditions

The 2 types of environmental permit are:

  • standard permits, which have a set of fixed rules for common activities and fixed charges
  • bespoke permits, which are written specifically for your business and vary in cost, depending on the activity

Contact the Environment Agency to find out which permit you need, or if you qualify for an exemption.

ERDF: national guidance

The Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) has adopted a standardised approach to the management and delivery of the ERDF Programmes.

Introduction

In this section of the website you will find a range of documents which relate to the management and delivery of European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) supported projects and also details of State Aid schemes running in England.

In addition, a National ERDF Handbook has been developed which provides an overview to the ERDF Programme and project lifecycle, and also information and guidance for applicants, grant recipients and DCLG on how to apply for ERDF funding and develop and manage compliant supported projects. This needs to be used in conjunction with the detailed rules and regulations against which projects are audited.

Access to the past ERDF User Manual can also be found in this section through the National ERDF Handbook page.

All documents are accessible through the sections below.

State Aid: schemes notified under the General Block Exemption Regulations 2008

State Aid is an area of competition law which applies to public funding within the European Union.

The Department for Business, Innovation and Skills (BIS) State Aid Branch has lead responsibility within UK government for co-ordination and development of State Aid policy. Further information on State Aid can be found on the BIS State Aid Branch website.

An ERDF State Aid Law Requirements Guidance Note is available to download below.

The following schemes have been notified by DCLG to take advantage of the State Aid exemptions available under the General Block Exemption Regulations 2008.

The schemes are only available for use by the named organisations. Aid may only be provided where the implementing body has satisfied all the terms of the scheme, including the reporting requirements.

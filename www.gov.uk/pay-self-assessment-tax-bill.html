Quick answer Pay your Self Assessment tax bill

Pay your Self Assessment tax bill by debit or credit card, Direct Debit, budget payment plan, through your bank, at the Post Office or by post.

It’s your responsibility to make sure payments reach HMRC by the deadline if the payment is late.

You’ll need your Self Assessment Reference number, also known as a Unique Taxpayer Reference number (UTR). You can get this on the payslip or your HMRC online account.

Direct Debit

You can set up a Direct Debit through your HMRC online account. Allow 5 working days to process a Direct Debit the first time you set one up. After that, it’s 3 working days.

Your Direct Debit will appear on your bank statement as “HMRC NDDS”. You can make changes to the Direct Debit through your HMRC online account.

Making flexible payments

You can make flexible payments if you’re on a ‘budget payment plan’. It lets you:

  • decide how much to pay each week or month
  • stop paying for up to 6 months

Set up a budget payment plan through your HMRC online account - you must be up-to-date with your payments and pay by Direct Debit.

Online by debit or credit card

You can pay online. You need your UTR. Allow 3 working days for payment to reach HMRC.

You must use a card issued by a UK card issuer - not American Express or Diners Club.

There’s a 1.4% fee if you pay by credit card.

Online or telephone banking, Bacs or CHAPS

Check your bank’s transaction limits and processing times.

Your payslip tells you which HMRC account to use. If you’re not sure, use HMRC Cumbernauld. You must use your UTR as the payment reference.

Your bank or building society

Use your payslip to pay at your own bank by cash or cheque made payable to ‘HM Revenue & Customs only’ followed by your UTR. Allow 3 working days for payments to reach HMRC.

Post Office

You must use your payslip. You can pay by debit card, cash or cheque made payable to ‘Post Office Ltd’. Allow 3 working days for payment to reach HMRC.

Post

Include your payslip and cheque made payable to ‘HM Revenue & Customs only’ followed by your UTR. Allow 3 working days for your payment to reach HMRC.

HM Revenue & Customs
Bradford
BD98 1YY

Don’t fold your payslip or cheque or fasten them together. If you want a receipt, include a note asking for one. You can only send a postdated cheque if you have HMRC’s permission.

If you don’t have a payslip

You don’t need a payslip to pay online or by Direct Debit.

If you’re posting a cheque, you can print a replacement payslip. - it won’t be accepted by banks, building societies or the Post Office.

Part 3: What custody is like for young people

Time in custody is spent:

  • in lessons
  • learning skills to get a job or to return to education
  • taking part in programmes to improve behaviour
  • participating in sport, fitness, and other activities

There are strict rules about what young people can and can’t do, and they may have to go through alcohol or drug counselling.

Types of secure centre

There are 3 types of custody for young people:

  • young offender institutions
  • secure training centres
  • secure children’s homes

Young offender institutions

Run by Prison Service and private companies
Age 15 to 21-year-olds. Under 18s held in different buildings from over 18s
Size From 60 to 400. Mainly big places, split into ‘wings’ of 30-60 people
Education and skills 25 hours a week

Secure training centres

Run by Private companies
Age Up to 17
Size Between 50 and 80. Split into units of 5-8 people
Education and skills 30 hours a week, following a school day timetable

Secure children’s homes

Run by Local councils
Age 10 to 14-year-olds
Size Between 8 and 40
Education and skills 30 hours a week, following a school day timetable

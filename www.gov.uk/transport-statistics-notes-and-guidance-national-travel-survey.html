This section provides technical information, notes and guidance and pre-release access relating to the National Travel Survey

Technical information

The National Travel Survey presents information on personal travel in Great Britain. The survey is primarily designed to track long-term development of trends in travel, although short-term changes can also be detected.

NTS data is collected via two main sources: face-to-face interviews with people in their homes and a seven-day travel diary, allowing travel patterns to be linked with individual characteristics. The NTS covers travel by people in all age groups, including children.

Future developments

The DfT ran a public consultation, on the future design of the National Travel Survey from June to September 2011. The consultation sought users’ views on the design of the NTS from 2013 onwards. The consultation document put forward a number of proposals to develop the NTS while reducing data collection costs.

A report containing a summary of responses to the consultation and details of the decisions the Department have made regarding the design of the survey from 2013 was published on 19 January 2012 and is available for download:

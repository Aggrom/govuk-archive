Part 4: Reports and supervision

The court order may say that the deputy needs to make regular reports to the Office of the Public Guardian. This is to make sure they are acting in the personâ€™s best interest.

A deputy should keep a record of any decisions they make. This includes:

  • making a major investment
  • changing the care a person is getting
  • deciding where someone should live
  • any meetings with a doctor
  • receipts and bank statements

All deputies are asked to make a report once a year using the deputyship report form.

You are responsible for paying a supervision fee to the Office of the Public Guardian if you are appointed as a deputy.

The fee will depend on the level of supervision you require. If you are eligible for an exemption or remission you will not have to pay the full fee.

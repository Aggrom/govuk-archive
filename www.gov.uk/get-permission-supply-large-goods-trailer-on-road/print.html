Permission to supply a large goods trailer for use on the road, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You should apply to the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) for permission if you supply large goods trailers for use on the road.

This applies to:

  • large goods trailers or semi-trailers of any weight
  • trailers (other than converter dollies) that have an unladen weight of more than 1,020kg
  • any trailers to which the Goods Vehicle (Plating and Testing) Regulations apply

This is currently a voluntary arrangement but will become mandatory for certain categories of large goods trailers from 29 October 2012.

Part 2: Who needs permission

Any trailer supplier can apply for permission on a voluntary basis.

From 29 October 2012, you must get permission if you are a ‘final supplier’ of:

  • new trailers manufactured in single or multiple stages
  • special purpose trailers

It will be an offence to supply these trailers without getting permission first.

Final suppliers are:

  • trailer manufacturers
  • trailer dealers
  • importers of new trailers

If you’re a dealer or manufacturer, you only have to get permission if it hasn’t already been applied for.

If you’re an operator using large goods trailers on the road, you don’t need to get permission - from 29 October, it should already have been given. You can check this by calling the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) helpline:

VOSA helpline
enquiries@vosa.gov.uk
0300 123 9000

Part 3: How to apply

Apply using form TES1. If the manufacture of the trailer was completed after 29 July 2012, you need to support your application with evidence of vehicle approval. If the manufacture was completed by 29 July 2012, you don’t need this.

The Vehicle and Operator Standards Agency (VOSA) accepts the following evidence of vehicle approval:

  • a European Community Whole Vehicle Type Approval (ECWVTA) Certificate of Conformity (CoC)
  • a UK National Small Series Type Approval (NSSTA) CoC
  • a UK Individual Vehicle Approval (IVA) certificate
  • an IVA certificate from another EU country (subject to acceptance by the UK approval authority)

More details are in the guidance notes.

After you apply

Once VOSA has received your application and confirmed that you meet the requirements you’ll get a ‘letter of consent to supply’.

If applicable, you’ll also get ministry plates, provided your approval documents give enough technical information.

If your application is rejected, VOSA will send you a letter explaining the reasons why.

Small commercial vessel registrations, surveys and inspections, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you own a small commercial boat, you must get it surveyed to make sure that it complies with UK safety laws.

Any boat that’s not a pleasure vessel is considered to be a commercial boat.

Before going to sea, it must be tested on its:

  • safety
  • construction
  • control systems
  • onboard machinery
  • electrical equipment

Small commercial vessels can be registered online.

Part 2: Register a small commercial vessel

A small ship is classified as being less than 24 metres long.

You can register small ships online using the Small Ships Register (Part III) On-Line Registration Service.

Contact the UK Ship Register (UKSR) for more information on registering small commercial vessels.

Part 3: Small commercial vessel surveys

Your boat will be surveyed to one of the following codes of practice:

  • the safety of small commercial motor vessels
  • the safety of small commercial sailing vessels
  • the safety of small workboats and pilot boats
  • the safety of small vessels in commercial use for sport or pleasure operating from a nominated departure point
  • small vessels in commercial use for sport or pleasure, workboats and pilot boats - alternative construction standards

To download these guides and to arrange a survey, see operational standards for small vessels.

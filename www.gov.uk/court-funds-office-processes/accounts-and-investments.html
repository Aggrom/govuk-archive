Part 2: Accounts and investments

The Court Funds Office can hold money in an account or invest some of the money on the stock market.

Accounts

Money held for a child or someone who is unable to make their own decisions is held in a â€˜special accountâ€™.

Money being held until a civil case is settled is held in a â€˜basic accountâ€™.

The special and basic accounts pay different rates of interest:

  • special account: 0.5%
  • basic account: 0.3%

Tax will have to be paid if the interest on the account is more than the personâ€™s tax allowance.

Only a deputy appointed by the Court of Protection or a litigation friend can access a Court Funds Office account for someone else.

Money will be held in a Court Funds Office account for a child until they are 18, unless a court order says otherwise. The Court Funds Office will write to the child 1 month before their 18th birthday and explain how they can get their money.

Statements

The Court Funds Office sends out annual statements for special accounts at the end of April.

Another statement is issued in October for all accounts that:

  • are being held for Court of Protection clients

Investments

The Court Funds Office can make stock market investments in a range of companies using the Equity Tracker Index Fund (ETIF).

They will invest money if all of the following apply:

  • the money is being held on behalf of a child or someone unable to make decisions for themselves
  • the amount of money being held is Â£10,000 or more
  • the money is likely to be held for 5 or more years

The amount of money invested is based on instructions from the Court of Protection, a deputy or an investment manager appointed by a deputy.

If any investments are sold for more than their original value, tax may also be payable. This may also happen if the difference in value is more than the personâ€™s Capital Gains Tax allowance.

You can check the value of the ETIF on the Financial Times website.

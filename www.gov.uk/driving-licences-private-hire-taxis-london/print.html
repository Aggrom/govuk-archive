Driving licences for private hire or taxis in London, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

To become a taxi or private hire driver in London you’ll need to apply to Transport for London (TfL) for the correct driving licence.

There are 2 types of driving licenses for taxi drivers:

  • the all London licence (known as a ‘green badge’) which allows you to work throughout London
  • the suburban licence (known as a ‘yellow badge’) which restricts you to working in the suburban areas of London

Private hire drivers will need to apply for a private hire driving licence.

Before applying for your driver’s licence, you’ll need to apply for an enhanced Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check.

Part 2: Apply for a taxi licence

To apply for a taxi licence in London you must be:

  • at least 21 years old
  • a UK or European Union (EU) citizen, or have the right the stay and work in the UK
  • the holder of a full Great Britain (GB), Northern Ireland (NI) or European Economic Area (EEA) driving licence

You’ll also need to:

  • have a Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check which costs £36
  • pass ‘the knowledge’ test
  • pass the Driving standards Agency (DSA) taxi driving assessment
  • have a medical examination with your GP

You can get a taxi driver application form from Transport for London (TfL).

Part 3: Renewing your taxi driver licence

Transport for London (TfL) will send you a renewal form before your current driving licence expires. You can also get one from the TfL office or use the Post Office check and send service.

If you don’t renew your licence before it expires you won’t be able to work as a licensed taxi driver.

Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check

Before returning your renewal you’ll need to apply for an enhanced CRB check.

What you need to send with your renewal

When returning your renewal form to TfL you’ll need to include:

  • 2 passport-sized photographs
  • your current DVLA licence (for photocard licences the paper copy must also be attached)
  • your current taxi driver’s licence (the copy and original)
  • payment of £249
  • your CRB reference number

Postal address

Please return your renewal form, supporting documents and payment to TfL.

Part 4: Apply for a private hire vehicle drivers licence

If you want to work as a private hire vehicle (PHV) driver in London, you’ll need to hold a driver licence issued by Transport for London (TfL).

Applying for your PHV drivers licence

To apply for your PHV drivers licence you’ll need to:

  • be at least 21 years of age
  • have held a full Great Britain (GB), Northern Ireland (NI) or European Economic Area (EEA) driving licence for at least 3 years
  • have the right to live and work in the UK
  • have an enhanced Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check which will cost £36
  • have a medical examination with your GP
  • sit a topographical (route finding) skills test

You can apply for your PHV driver licence from TfL.

The current fee is £298 for 3 years.

Part 5: Renewing your private hire licence

Transport for London (TfL) will send you a renewal form before your current driving licence expires. You can also get one from the TfL office or use the Post Office check and send service.

You need to send your renewal form to TfL 6 weeks before your licence expires

If you don’t renew your licence before it expires you won’t be able to work as a private hire driver

What you need to send with your renewal

When returning your renewal form to TfL you’ll need to include:

  • 1 recent, colour passport-sized photograph
  • clear photocopies of both sides of your current DVLA licence (for photocard licences the paper copy must also be attached)
  • a completed enhanced Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check
  • a completed medical form, if required
  • payment of £306 (to cover the licence application, CRB Disclosure application and grant of licence fees)

Postal address

Please return your renewal form, supporting documents and payment to TfL.

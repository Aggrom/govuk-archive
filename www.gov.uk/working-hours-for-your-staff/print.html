Working hours for your staff, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Staff aged 18 or over must not work more than 48 hours per week on average, unless you have a written opt-out agreement.

You must:

  • calculate and keep records of your staff’s working hours
  • arrange opting-out agreements
  • make sure your staff take rest breaks
  • check for and apply any special exemptions - eg for staff who work a long way from home or chose their own hours

Special rules apply when employing people under 18.

Part 2: Calculating working hours

You must calculate the number of hours worked by your staff over a 17-week period by adding up the time worked during that time.

In your calculations you must include:

  • work-related training
  • travel by your staff as part for their day-to-day work, eg travelling to visit customers
  • working lunches

You can, with the agreement of your staff, work out their average hours over longer periods of time, eg up to 52 weeks.

Working time does not include commuting between their home and place of work, lunch breaks, evening classes or day-release courses.

Part 3: Opting-out of the 48-hour working time limit

If you want to give your staff the choice to work longer than the 48-hour per week limit, you must get them to sign a written opt-out agreement.

Your staff can cancel this opt-out arrangement as long as they give you at least 7 days’ notice.

Mobile staff who can not opt out

You must not allow the following staff to opt out of the 48-hour weekly working time limit:

  • staff in the road transport industry, eg delivery drivers
  • staff who work on ships or boats
  • airline staff
  • other staff who travel in and operate vehicles covered by EU rules on drivers’ hours, eg bus conductors
  • security guards on a vehicle carrying high-value goods

Part 4: Special working hours rules

You do not have to monitor the working hours of staff who:

  • decide their own hours of work
  • work a long way from home
  • have to travel to different places for work

Special working time arrangements

You can work out the average working hours over a 26-week period (instead of 17 weeks) for staff who:

  • work in security or surveillance
  • work in an environment that operates 24-hour staffing
  • work during particularly busy periods
  • are involved in work-related emergencies

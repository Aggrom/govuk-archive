Benefits & credits: Plug-in car and van grants

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You can get a grant towards the cost of each new electric (plug-in) car or van you buy, if it meets certain conditions. Grants are available for:

  • 25% off the cost of a car, up to a maximum of £5,000
  • 20% off the cost of a van up to a maximum of £8,000

Part 2: What you'll get

The term ‘plug-in’ applies to any vehicle which can be charged by electricity.

The grant covers:

  • 25% off the cost of a car, up to a maximum of £5,000
  • 20% off the cost of a van up to a maximum of £8,000

The ‘cost’ is the full purchase price you pay for the basic vehicle - including number plates, vehicle excise duty and VAT. It doesn’t include any optional extras, eg delivery charges or first registration fee.

Part 3: Eligibility

Your vehicle is likely to be eligible if it is one of the following:

  • electric vehicles (EVs) – these run completely on batteries and are plugged into the mains to be recharged
  • plug-in hybrid electric vehicles (PHEVs) – these use a petrol or diesel engine combined with a battery that plugs into the mains
  • hydrogen fuel cell vehicles and other technologies

Cars eligible for the grant

  • Chevrolet Volt
  • Citroen CZero
  • Mia Electric Mia
  • Mitsubishi iMiEV
  • Nissan Leaf
  • Peugeot iON
  • Renault Fluence
  • Smart fortwo electric drive
  • Toyota Prius Plug-in
  • Vauxhall Ampera

Vans eligible for the grant

  • Azure Dynamics Transit Connect Electric
  • Daimler Mercedes-Benz Vito E-Cell
  • Faam ECOMILE
  • Faam JOLLY 2000
  • Mia electric Mia U
  • Renault Kangoo
  • Smith Electric Smith Edison

Part 4: How to claim

The amount of your grant will be automatically deducted from the price of your car or van when you buy it. The dealership will complete the paperwork, so there are no application forms for you to fill in, but you may be asked to complete a short questionnaire.

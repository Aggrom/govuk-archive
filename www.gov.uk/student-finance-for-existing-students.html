Quick answer Student finance if you started before 1 September 2012

If you started university or college before 1 September 2012 this page applies to you. Different rules apply for anyone starting after this date.

Full-time students can apply for a Tuition Fee Loan, Maintenance Loan and Maintenance Grant.

Part-time students can apply for Tuition Fee Grant and Course Grant.

EU full-time students can apply for a Tuition Fee Loan. EU part-time students can apply for a Tuition Fee Grant and Course Grant.

Apply for student finance

You have to apply every year. You need your passport details the first time you apply. Don’t send your UK passport.

Only EU students have to send their passport or identity card.

You can apply up to 9 months after the start of your course.

About 6 weeks after your online application you’ll get a declaration form in the post. Sign this and send it back.

Tuition Fee Loans

You can apply for Tuition Fee Loans if you’re a full-time UK or EU student.

The loan is paid directly to your university or college you have to pay the loan back.

Part-time students can’t apply for Tuition Fee Loans.

Full-time student Tuition Fee Loan per year
Full-time Up to £3,465
Full-time at a private university or college Up to £3,465

Fee Grant

You can apply if you are a part-time UK or EU student.

The grant depends on your household income (your parents’ or partner’s, plus your own). The grant is paid directly to your university or college.

You must study at a rate of at least 50% equivalent to a full-time course (check with your university or college).

Full-time students can’t apply.

Part-time student household income Fee Grant
Up to £16,845 £820 to £1,230
£16,846 to £25,420 £770 to £1,180
Over £25,420 No grant

Maintenance Loan for living costs

You must be a full-time UK student.

You have to pay the loan back. The loan depends on your household income (parents’ or partner’s income plus yours) and where you live. It will be paid into your bank account at the start of term.

You can’t apply if you’re a part-time or an EU student

Full-time student Maintenance Loan per year
Living at home Up to £3,838
Living away from home, outside London Up to £4,950
Living away from home, in London Up to £6,928

Maintenance Grant for living costs

You must be a full-time UK student.

You don’t have to pay the grant back - the grant depends on your household income (parents’ or partner’s income plus yours) and when you start your course.

The grant is paid into your bank account at the start of term.

You can’t apply if you’re a part-time or an EU student.

Full-time student – household income Maintenance Grant per year
£25,000 or less £2,984
£25,001- £50,695 £50 to £2,983
Over £50,695 No grant

Course Grant for living costs

You can apply if you’re a part-time UK or EU students.

The grant depends on your household income (your parents’ or partner’s plus yours).

The grant is paid directly into your account at the start of term.

Full-time students can’t apply.

Part-time student household income Course Grant per year
up to £26,029 £265
£26,030 to £28,065 £50 to £264
Over £28,065 No grant

Extra help

You might also be able to get:

  • scholarships and bursaries from your college or university, eg if you’re on a low income
  • extra help for students with children: a Childcare Grant or a Parents’ Learning Allowance
  • Disabled Students’ Allowances
  • NHS bursaries for medical students
  • Social work bursaries
  • Access to Learning Fund if you’re on a low income

Repayments

Your payments are linked to your income. You only make repayments when your income is over £15,795 a year.

Each month you pay back 9% of any income over £15,795.

Example

You earn £18,000. This is £2,205 over the £15,795 threshold.

You repay 9% of £2,205 = £198 per year, or £16 per month

The Student Loans Company has detailed information on repayments.

Work experience at the Foreign and Commonwealth Office

Opportunities for work placements and internships at the FCO London office.

Contents

  3. See more like this

The Foreign and Commonwealth Office (FCO) aims to recruit a talented and diverse workforce which reflects the society we serve. Our recruitment policies are therefore designed to encourage applications from the widest range of backgrounds.

By offering work experience opportunities, we hope to encourage young people to consider the FCO as a future employer, as well as offering them beneficial work experience. Given our aim above, we are focusing our work experience opportunities on individuals from backgrounds that are currently under-represented in our workforce; and on those who bring skills which we require to achieve our objectives. These currently include, in particular, hard languages and economics.

The FCO Future Talent Scheme

This is a scheme run by the FCO for students in their penultimate year of university. The scheme is split into 2 strands.

  1. Firstly we are targeting female undergraduates of any background studying any degree at any UK university. This strand is aimed at increasing our future pool of female applicants to the fast stream, as they are currently under-represented.

  2. The second strand targets undergraduates of any background at any UK university who are studying Arabic, Mandarin, Japanese, Farsi, Dari, Pashtu, Russian or economics. This strand is aimed at attracting future applicants to our fast stream who have important skills for the FCO.

We will be taking 10 students from each strand for a 4-week placement during the summer (August to September). Students will be paid during their placement. They will get training in areas such as interview skills, and will be assigned to teams within departments across the FCO with a line manager responsible for their induction and development.

The FCO Future Talent Scheme for 2012 is now over. Information about future FCO work experience internship schemes will appear here.

Economic Sandwich Students Placement Scheme

Every year, the FCO offers a 1-year placement opportunity to 4 economics undergraduates. The successful candidates will work in our Economics Unit, the Commercial and Economic Diplomacy Department, or the EU Directorate. This is an excellent opportunity for students to gain valuable work experience, using their economic skills to support the FCO’s work across a range of foreign policy issues. Students will be based at the FCO’s Headquarters in Whitehall.

What will I be doing?

Students will be involved in a range of activities across economic, policy and corporate work. These are likely to include:

  • providing briefings for ministers or ambassadors
  • working on political lobbying and supporting political processes
  • compiling economic reports, for example, on international development, trade and EU issues
  • providing research support for economists, including statistical analysis
  • maintaining and editing the FCO’s economics website
  • contributing to foreign policy papers requiring economic input or analysis
  • taking responsibility for an area of corporate activity, for example, as a first aider or fire marshal

Have I got what you’re looking for?

We are looking for economics students with an interest in current and foreign affairs. Students will need to have a very good knowledge of economics and a good awareness of economic issues in a foreign policy context. The successful candidates will have good communication skills (written and oral), be able to deliver results and have strong inter-personal skills.

When do placements take place?

Placements start in the summer (June/July) and last for 12 months. Precise start dates will be negotiated with successful candidates.

Will I get paid?

Yes. Successful candidates will be paid £19,245 plus a London Location Allowance of £3,520. Placement students will also qualify (after 3 months’ employment) for an advance of salary to meet the cost of travel fares. The FCO offers a loan to students who are interested in purchasing a bike for home-to-office travel.

What are the eligibility criteria?

To be eligible you must:

  • be an undergraduate between your penultimate and final year of study at the time of the placement
  • have 3 A-level passes at grades A to C (or equivalent)
  • be on a degree course where at least half of the course is economics-based
  • be available to start work in June/July 2013 for 12 months
  • be a British citizen and have been resident in the UK for at least 2 of the previous 10 years, at least 1 year of which must have been a consecutive 12-month period

Security clearance

Successful candidates must undergo security clearance before being formally appointed. All applicants should be aware that a lack of sufficient background information could prevent an applicant from being granted clearance.

How do I apply?

Applications can be made through the GES Sandwich Student Placement.

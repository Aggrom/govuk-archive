Introduction

HIVES are information centres for forces personnel and their families and here you can find a list of them in northern England and Edinburgh, Scotland.

Edinburgh HIVE

Serving Redford, Dreghorn, Longstone, Craigiehall and Glencorse. Also Stirling and Glasgow.

Edinburgh Dreghorn HIVE is situated on the southside of Edinburgh within Dreghorn Barracks. The resident battalion is the 1st Battalion The Royal Regiment of Scotland, The Royal Scots Borderers. Nearby, Redford Barracks houses the 3rd Battalion The Rifles. Others include 150 RMP, 52 Brigade at Edinburgh Castle/Redford Barracks and many other attached personnel including TA Units.

Local amenities are excellent while a wider range of shopping and entertainment facilities are easily accessed from the nearby Edinburgh City bypass. There is a frequent bus service with buses serving many parts of Edinburgh including the city centre.

Edinburgh itself is a beautiful city with many tourist attractions including the castle and Holyrood Palace.

The community centre in Dreghorn Barracks hosts ‘Mums and Tots’, youth clubs, coffee mornings and lots more. The Army Learning Centre has a Satellite Access Point which soldiers and their dependents can use for learning or internet access.In Dreghorn Barracks there is a swimming pool and gym which is open to all soldiers and their dependents. The gym, which has two squash courts, can be used for football, circuit training, badminton and various other sports. A weights room and multi-gym can also be found there.

27 AEC Centre and the Army Learning Centre are located at Redford Barracks. There is also a gym with the same facilities as Dreghorn and an all weather football pitch.

Catterick HIVE

Serving Catterick Garrison and Alanbrooke Barracks, Topcliffe.

Catterick Garrison is situated on the edge of the exquisite Yorkshire Dales and close to the delights of the North Yorkshire Moors. The sea is only an hour’s drive away, as is the beautiful Lake District. Also within an hour you can reach the bustling city of Newcastle, the Metro Centre at Gateshead and the historic cities of York and Leeds. Closer to Catterick are the market towns of Darlington, Richmond and Northallerton. Catterick Garrison itself also has lot to offer with further development plans that include a new sports and leisure centre.

The garrison is well-supported with a variety of facilities within the Army Welfare Service community complex including the HIVE, free Internet Facilities (in HIVE), the NSPCC Almond Tree Project, Mother and Toddlers sessions and children’s activities hosted by Community Support. Outside the Army Welfare Service (AWS) complex are other facilities including two well-used Community Centres hosting regular events - the White Rose Club, which houses a ten pin bowling alley, and the Dalesman Community Centre, which adjoins Soldier Sam’s adventure play centre. There is also a thrift shop, library, leisure centre with three swimming pools and other sporting and recreational facilities including an 18 hole golf course.

Chilwell HIVE

Serving Chilwell, Old Dalby, Woodhouse Eves (Loughborough) and Melton Mowbray.

Chetwynd Barracks, Chilwell is situated approximately 7 miles from the City of Nottingham and 3 miles from J25 on M1. The towns of Long Eaton and Beeston are approx 2 miles away in different directions. Both of these towns have good local shops and all the usual banks and building societies.

Chetwynd Barracks is 10 minutes from an excellent bus service which goes either to Nottingham via Beeston or Derby via Long Eaton. Nottingham East Midlands Airport is approximately 20 minutes drive from Chetwynd Barracks.

The 20-Twenty Club and Colours Restaurant provide the main focus of catering and leisure within the barracks. The 20-Twenty Club also has a cash machine inside the building (charge payable). There is a gymnasium, and an Army Learning Centre/Library that has internet access.

All service families accommodation (SFA) at Chilwell are located inside the wire. However, please note that SFA may be allocated at Old Dalby (approx 20 miles from Chilwell), Woodhouse Eves (approx 16 miles from Chilwell), or Melton Mowbray (approx 28 miles from Chilwell).

Chilwell accommodation standing orders are available to ArmyNet. Navigate from the home site to: Units & Orgs / Divisions & Brigades / 5 Div / 49 Bde / Chilwell Station - Accommodation Guide.

Harrogate HIVE

Serving Hildebrand Barracks, Uniacke Barracks, Army Foundation College and Claro Barracks, Ripon.

The Army Foundation College (AFC), only 2 miles from Harrogate, is on the edge of the Yorkshire Dales, a delightful destination at the heart of England’s largest county. Harrogate itself is both cosmopolitan and elegant and has a great mixture of wonderful shopping, wining and dining combined with a glamorous heritage as a famous spa town. Explore and enjoy grand historic houses and awe-inspiring castles with their fascinating stories, Turkish Baths and monumental abbey ruins to name a few.

Uniacke (AFC) and Hildebrand Barracks (Families) both have their own HIVEs where you can find information on all the unit facilities. These include a gymnasium (open to all), swimming pool (open to all), Learn Direct centre, pre-school/crèche, community centre, Mums and Toddlers, youth centre (Guides and Rainbows), NAAFI shop, café, barbers and cash machines.

Leconfield HIVE

Serving Leconfield, Beverley, Driffield and Hull.

DST Normandy Barracks is in the village of Leconfield. The market town of Beverley, 2 miles away, has an attractive blend of the past and present ranging from music festivals to ghost walks, Georgian terraces to historic pubs, medieval churches and bustling markets. The horizon is dominated by the majestic Minster. A particular attraction of Beverley is the many independent shops set around the pretty cobbled square. There are leisure pursuits to suit all tastes.

Normandy Barracks has a gymnasium and recreational complex including cardiovascular and weights room, two large sports halls, squash and tennis courts and a sauna. These facilities are open to all. There is a crèche and parent and toddler group, guides, brownies and other community activities held within the Community Centre. Additional facilities include the Hub (snack bar), unisex hairdresser and Londis shop.

Ouston HIVE

Serving Albemarle Barracks

Albemarle Barracks is the home of 39 Regiment Royal Artillery which is located in the heart of Hadrian’s Wall country with Newcastle city centre to the east and Hexham to the west. Although it is in quite a remote location, local amenities are not far away. Newcastle is renowned for its many theatres, museums and concert venues as well as its numerous bars, restaurants and fantastic nightlife. Newcastle is a thriving city but is surrounded by lush countryside, stunning scenery and outstanding history, with Hadrian’s Wall, Durham and Northumberland on the doorstep.

The unit gymnasium is open to all and has very up-to-date equipment with a cardio area, squash courts, weights room and boxing training area, a brand new trim trail and several tennis courts.

There is also a youth centre run by the community development worker for the Tyne & Tees area who is based at Albemarle Barracks. There are 2 youth clubs (under 9’s and over 9’s) as well as Easter and summer activities program.

York HIVE

Serving York Garrison, Strensall and Dishforth.

York Garrison consists of 2 stations, York-Fulford and Strensall Station.

York Station is based on Imphal Barracks accommodating HQ York Garrison, 15 (NE) Brigade HQ, 2 Signal Regiment, Defence vetting agency and many small administrating units. Imphal Barracks is very accessible and within easy reach of the A64 just off the A19, one mile from the city centre. To the rear of the barracks is situated the excellent community centre called The Low Moor Centre, families accommodation, the gymnasium and outdoor all-weather sports complex.

Strensall Station is based on Queen Elizabeth Barracks in Strensall, home to:

  • HQ 2 Med Bde
  • 34 Field Hospital
  • HQ Strensall Training Centre
  • 9 Cadet Training Team
  • Army Medical Services (AMS) FTC
  • Kings Division Recruiting Team
  • Army Youth Team
  • The Garrison Dental Centre
  • other smaller units.

Queen Elizabeth Barracks, Strensall is situated just off the York bypass A1237/ A64 approximately 7 miles from York city centre. Just outside of the wire is families accommodation on small estates and a community centre. Hurst Hall Community Centre can be found on Border Road in Strensall. The Community Centre provides a wide range of activities and clubs for children and families, including HIVE and Stepping Stones Pre School. Please call in on arrival and see what we can offer you.

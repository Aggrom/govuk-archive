Part 3: Eligibility

Eligibility

Eligibility depends on:

  • your nationality or residency status
  • your course
  • your university or college

EU students

To get a Tuition Fee Loan you must be an EU national, or the family member of an EU national

To qualify for additional help you must:

  • be an EU national, or the family member of an EU national
  • have lived in the UK for 3 years or more before your course starts
  • be living in the UK on the first day of the academic year in which your course starts

Migrant workers

Migrant workers from the EEA or Switzerland and children of Turkish workers may also qualify for a Tuition Fee Loan and additional help. For more detail download the guide ‘How you are assessed and paid’ (PDF, 300KB).

Your course

This should be one of the following:

  • a first degree, eg, a Bachelor of Arts, Science or Education (BA, BSc or BEd)
  • a Foundation Degree
  • a Certificate of Higher Education
  • a Diploma of Higher Education (DipHE)
  • a Higher National Certificate (HNC)
  • a Higher National Diploma (HND)
  • Initial Teacher Training
  • further training of youth and community workers

Generally you’ll only get student finance if you’re doing your first higher education qualification. However, you may still get it if, for example:

  • you’ve changed course
  • you’ve withdrawn from your course but decide to start again
  • you’re ‘topping up’ a higher education qualification – for example, you’ve finished a foundation degree and now want to do an honours degree

Part-time courses

Part-time courses need to be least 25% of an equivalent full-time course (for example 4 years part-time instead of 1 year full-time). This is known as ‘course intensity’. You can check course intensity with your university or college.

Your university or college

This should be at a university, college or other institution that offers a qualifying course.

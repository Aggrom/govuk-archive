European Fisheries Fund overview

An overview of the European Fisheries Fund (EFF) and how it provides financial assistance to help fishermen and vessel managers.

What is the European Fisheries Fund?

The government has set aside approximately £38 million from the EFF between 2007 and 2013 to help the fisheries industry adapt to changing requirements under the Common Fisheries Policy. This funding is broken down as follows:

  • Axis 1: Measures for the adaptation of the European Community fishing fleet
  • Axis 2: Measures for investments in aquaculture and processing and marketing of fishery and aquaculture products
  • Axis 3: Measures of common interest
  • Axis 4: Measures of the sustainable development of fisheries areas

The Axis 4 programme is delivered through fisheries local action groups (FLAGs) who represent different area. These FLAGs have been set up in six areas in England, covering around 20 per cent of the English coastline. Read more about Axis 4 on the Marine Management Organisation (MMO) website.

Who can apply?

You, as the applicant, must sign the application form, even if you had help completing it as you will be responsible for making sure that all of the terms and conditions of the grant are met.

The different funding streams are available to different people or sectors. For example, Axis 1 is available to vessel owners in the private sector, but Axis 2 aquaculture projects are available to micro, small or medium sized in the public or private sector.

When can you apply?

You can apply at any time until 31 December 2013, which is the end of the programme. If your application is for a large amount of money or is more complicated, your form and business case may have to be assessed by a selection panel. The panels are usually held 3 times a year.

The MMO may have to close the programme earlier if the allocated funds have been used up.

What things may be eligible for a grant?

The kinds of projects that are eligible for a grant vary depending on the axis.

  • Axis 1 is aimed at improvements to on board safety, working conditions, hygience, product quality, selectivity of fishing gear, energy efficiency and engine replacement
  • Axis 2 is aimed at investments in aquaculture and processing, and for marketing of fishery and aquaculture products so may include buildings, equipment, fees, infrastructure and vehicles
  • Axis 3 is aimed at common interests so may include advertising and promotion, equipment, products not intended for human consumption and VAT

Grants are not awarded for projects that will increase fishing efforts.

Any work that starts before a decision has been made on an application is automatically not eligible for a grant.

How much grant will I get?

The EFF programme is competitive, and only projects that most effectively meet its aims will be awarded a grant.

You will receive a percentage of the total eligible cost of your project if you are successful. The amount you receive depends on your type or project, and your location. The different axes have different requirements both in terms of minimum and maximum grants you can apply for, and the percentages available.

  • Axis 1 has a £500 minimum and £60,000 maximum (except engine replacements where the maximum is £100,000), and the maximum percentage you will receive wil be between 20 and 60 per cent
  • Axis 2 has a £400 minimum and three maximums depending on the type of project, and the maximum percentage you will receive will be between 80 and 100 per cent, 20 and 40 per cent, and 20 and 40 per cent
  • Axis 3 has 5 different kinds of projects with their own minimums and maximums, and different maximum percentages depending on the applicant (public or private), and location (Cornwall and the Isles of Scilly or the rest of England)

How to apply for funding

You should submit the relevant application form to the MMO. You should use the application form that matches the appropriate axis of the EFF.

Your application must include a detailed business case that shows that your project:

  • has quantified outputs and clear and attainable targets
  • will provide an additional and sustainable benefit to the fishing industry
  • has a justified need for grant
  • will provide good value for money

You should also include:

  • a full description of your project
  • why the work is needed
  • the benefits of the project
  • financial, environmental and other benefits
  • a cost-benefit analysis
  • an analysis of quotes
  • the results you expect from the project
  • how you will monitor the project
  • how you will manage risks to delivery

Smaller projects just require a simple statement of costs and benefits with the appropriate number of quotes along with copies of your company accounts will be sufficient for the projects involving:

  • improvements to working conditions
  • safety equipment additional to that required by Maritime and Coastguard Agency rules
  • facilities to maximise fish quality where the costs are less than £1,250
  • the adoption of sustainable catching methods where the costs are less than £1,250 (in this case, we will also need the details of your current and future fishing position)

You should make sure that you describe all the costs and benefits of your project without double counting.

For advice on applying to the EFF, you should contact the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2694 or 0191 376 2676 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk

What is in the offer letter?

The offer letter sets out details of the conditions of the grant and any agreed performance targets and outputs for the project. You must comply with the conditions along with details of eligible expenditure to allow us to pay your grant claims.

There are some general conditions that apply to all grants, as well as conditions specific to your project. Read the general conditions of EFF grant for projects in England on the MMO website (PDF 93KB).

You should read your offer letter carefully, sign one copy and return it to the MMO within 4 weeks before you start your project.

Your application will be treated as commercially confidential throughout the assessment stage. Once approved, the MMO will make some details public – the name of the applicant, the amount approved, the nature of the project. By accepting the offer you are agreeing that this information can be made public.

You must inform the MMO in writing if you plan to:

  • modify your project
  • change the funding package
  • change the contractors and/or suppliers specified in the offer letter
  • change the timetable of the project
  • sell any item of infrastructure or stop or relocate any activity that the project is dependent on

Your letter to the MMO should provide justification for any changes.

How to claim funding from the EFF

If you are eligible for funding under the EFF, you can claim your grant by submitting a claim form by the date specified in your offer letter to the MMO at the following address:

Business Relations Team
Marine Management Organisation
Lancaster House
Hampshire Court
Newcastle upon Tyne
NE4 7YH

Download the EFF claim form and guidance from the MMO website.

You should normally submit a single full and final claim by the date specified in your offer letter. You may be allowed to submit one or more interim claims but the final claim must still be submitted by the date specified in the offer letter.

The MMO aims to pay the grant within 8 weeks once all the necessary information to consider your claim is received. The MMO accept no liability in respect of loss attributable to any delay in the payment of claims or to any suspension, reduction or cancellation of grant.

The payment will be made electronically into your bank account.

Completing the claim form

You or someone acting on your behalf should complete the EFF claim form and provide supporting documentation.

When filling in the claim form:

  • the name of the claimant should be the person who originally applied for the grant – if different, a covering note of explanation should be attached
  • the project number can be found on the offer letter
  • the title of the project should be a brief description of the project, as set out in the application form
  • the approval date is the date of the offer letter
  • the start and completion dates should relate to the project and not the claim – you should enter the expected date of completion of the project for interim claims
  • the total grant aid available as set out in the offer letter and details of any grant already received, the amount you are currently claiming and any remaining funding to be claimed
  • claims made and remaining should include details of payments still to be made – all figures must normally exclude VAT unless you are not registered
  • the work carried out table must show the breakdown of work as set out in the offer letter – the amounts shown in the table should be exactly as quoted on the invoice
  • changes in cost should show the differences between expected and actual costs – you should give brief reasons for any differences greater than 10 per cent
  • progress report should show what the project has achieved compared to the targets set out in the offer letter: you should give brief reasons where targets have not been achieved
  • you, or a person authorised to sign on your behalf, must sign the form

For advice on completing the EFF claim form, you can call the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 376 2694 or 0191 376 2676 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Supporting documentation for EFF claims

All claims must be accompanied by a progress report. If the progress report for your project is not satisfactory, the MMO may not be able to pay your claim.

You must submit the originals of all invoices and other sales dockets in support of each item listed in Section 2(c)1 of the claim form – the MMO will retain all original documents. If you cannot, for a legitimate reason, provide originals, you must provide copies which are certified by the supplier as true copies of the originals.

The MMO requires a BACs remittance or bank statement showing payment of each of the invoices in order to verify payment of the items.

The MMO also ask you to number the invoices in the order they are listed. You will be required to demonstrate that services or goods are costed at market rates and that documentation has a probative value similar to receipts that will satisfy the scheme’s auditors for payments in kind.

Some expenditure incurred in non-capital projects may not involve invoices. In such cases, you must provide appropriate documentation to confirm that the expenditure was incurred and defrayed, such as rail tickets for attendance at trade fairs. For claims in respect of structural work, a statement from an independent architect or surveyor on letter headed paper is required, confirming that the work has been carried out in accordance with the terms of the original application. Where the work has not been carried out in accordance with such terms, give reasons for the differences and refer to dates on which modifications were authorised by the MMO.

For further advice, you should contact the MMO Business Relations Team on 0191 376 2694 or 0191 376 2676 or email them at eff.projects@marinemanagement.org.uk.

Managing a EFF project

If you manage a project that is supported by the EFF, you will have to abide by these requirements.

Inspections

You must allow inspection of your project by officials authorised by the MMO, together with any person accompanying them (which may, from time to time, include an official of the European Commission). You must also provide them with access to relevant documents, and give them reasonable assistance.

All records held by you or the MMO may have to be made available for inspection by the Commission and the National Audit Office.

Progress reports

You will be asked to provide updates on the progress of your project every 6 months. You will also be required to submit a progress report on your business and performance of the project on an annual basis for 3 years from the date of the final payment of the grant.

Retaining records

The MMO will retain original copies of all documents, invoices and other evidence of payment for reference by authorities such as the Commission or European Court of Auditors. You must also keep documentary evidence of expenditure for 6 years from the date of final payment of grant.

Documentary evidence includes:

  • order forms and delivery notes for plant and equipment
  • bills of quantity
  • tenders
  • estimates and quotations
  • architects’ certificates
  • statements and invoices
  • relevant books of account
  • drawings
  • plans and technical specifications

Changing or cancelling your project

If the approved project needs to be modified, you should get the MMO’s agreement before the modification takes place. If you do not, you may lose your entitlement to the grant award.

Modifications include changes in the supplier’s name, status of the beneficiary, change of location and takeover of the company as well as changes in the design and purpose of the project.

If for any reason you decide not to proceed with the project, you should notify the MMO as soon as possible that you are giving up the grant. You should send a written notification to the MMO at this address:

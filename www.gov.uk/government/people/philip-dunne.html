Biography

In September 2012 Philip Dunne was appointed Minister for Defence Equipment, Support and Technology with responsibility for defence procurement and defence exports. He is the Conservative MP for Ludlow, Shropshire.

Education

Philip studied politics, philosophy and economics at Oxford University.

Political career

After the 2010 election, Philip was appointed Assistant Government Whip, a role he had carried out in opposition. He has been a member of the work and pensions select committee, the public accounts committee and the Treasury select committee.

Career outside politics

Philip spent 20 years in business and investment banking. This included chairmanship of a Venture Capital Trust which invests in entrepreneurial new businesses.

Personal life

He is married with 2 daughters and 2 sons.

Parliamentary Under Secretary of State for Defence Equipment, Support and Technology

The minister is responsible for:

  • delivering the MOD’s equipment and support plan and the MOD’s relationship with industry
  • reform of the Defence Equipment and Support Organisation through the Materiel Strategy
  • defence exports
  • defence science and technology

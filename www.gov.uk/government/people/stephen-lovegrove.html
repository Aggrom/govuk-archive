Biography

Stephen joined the Shareholder Executive in April 2004 and was appointed Chief Executive in June 2007.

Shareholder Executive comprises four Units: Portfolio Unit (comprising c.20 Government owned companies); Corporate Finance Practice (which oversees the interventions and investments Government makes in private sector companies); Royal Mail & Post Office (which works to safeguard the future of both companies) and Information Economy (which progresses the UK’s Cyber Security Strategy and shapes the business environment to help grow the online economy).

Key transactions concluded by the Shareholder Executive include the sale of HMG’s stake in British Energy to EDF for £4.4bn, the placing of HMG’s remaining shares in QinetiQ, the provision of £340m in financing for Airbus and HMG’s sale of the Tote to Betfred.

Separate from his ShEx duties, Stephen also sits on the board of The London Organising Committee of the Olympic Games and Paralympic Games and is a Trustee of the Charities Aid Foundation.

Prior to the Shareholder Executive, he spent ten years at Deutsche Bank, where he was the head of the European media team working on a wide range of capital raisings, acquisitions and divestments in all the sub-sectors of the media industry. Before the City, Stephen spent five years as a strategic consultant.

Chief Executive, Shareholder Executive

The Chief Executive, Shareholder Executive sits on the BIS Departmental Board and Executive Committee.

He or she chairs the BIS Operations Committee and the cross-Whitehall Central Spectrum Release Group, heads the Civil Service Corporate Finance Profession, is an observer member of Cabinet Office Sub Committee PEX(A).

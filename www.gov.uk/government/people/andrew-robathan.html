Biography

Andrew Robathan was appointed Minister of State for the Armed Forces in September 2012. He is the Conservative MP for south Leicestershire.

Education

Andrew was educated at Merchant Taylors’ School in Northwood and studied modern history at Oriel College, Oxford. He later attended the Army Staff College in Camberley.

Political career

He was previously Minister for Defence Personnel, Welfare and Veterans. He has been an MP since 1992.

Career outside politics

Andrew served around the world in the Coldstream Guards from 1974 to 1989. He later worked for BP from 1991 to 1992.

Andrew then volunteered to rejoin the army in 1991, and served as Chief of Staff of the Prisoner of War Guard Force in Saudi Arabia, Iraq and Kuwait. He worked for BP from 1991 to 92.

Personal life

Andrew is married and has 2 children.

Minister of State for the Armed Forces

The minister is responsible for the armed forces. Responsibilities include:

  • operations and operational policy, including operational legal issues
  • force generation, including readiness, recuperation, key enablers, deployed operational logistic delivery and operational training exercises

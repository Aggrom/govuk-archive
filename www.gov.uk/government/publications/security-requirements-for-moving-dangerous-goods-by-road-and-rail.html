Detail

Introduction

Businesses involved in the carriage of dangerous goods by road and rail for national and international journeys are required to take security measures to safeguard against acts of terrorism in accordance with chapter 1.10 of the European Agreement concerning the International Carriage of Dangerous Goods by Road (ADR) and of the Appendix C of the Convention concerning International Carriage by Rail, Regulations concerning the International Carriage of Dangerous Goods by Rail (RID).

The requirements

Regulation 5 of the Carriage of Dangerous Goods and Use of Transportable Pressure Equipment Regulations 2009 (and as amended) apply the security provisions of ADR and RID for national journeys. Regulations 7 and 8 also contain additional security requirements for Great Britain. These regulations are subject to agreed derogations as set out in the Carriage of Dangerous Goods: Approved Derogations and Transitional Provisions.

The security requirements are split between two levels. A general level is applicable to all dangerous goods and requires that they shall only be offered to people, companies or organisations that have been appropriately identified, temporary storage sites shall be properly secured, drivers shall carry a means of photographic identification and security training awareness shall be provided. A higher level applies for high consequence dangerous goods and requires that a security plan is adopted, implemented and complied with. High consequence dangerous goods are defined as those which have the potential for misuse in a terrorist incident and which may, as a result, produce serious consequences such as mass casualties or mass destruction.

The requirements do not apply to the carriage of civil nuclear material as defined by the Nuclear Industries Security Regulations 2003 (NISR). The transport of such material and non nuclear radioactive material is regulated by the Office for Nuclear Regulation in accordance with NISR.

Road and rail guidance documents

The Department for Transport (DfT) published guidance when the security requirements originally came into force in July 2005 to help you determine which measures you should apply in order to comply with the security requirements. This guidance is currently being reviewed and updated and will appear here in the near future. If in the mean time you have any enquiries regarding the security requirements please contact the department using the contact details below.

Supplementary guidance

Carriage by road and rail

  • For advice on security training content, see our ‘Guidance for the design and delivery of security awareness training for the carriage of dangerous goods by rail & road’ publication
  • For information on personnel security see the CPNI Personnel security resource list
  • The UK National Counter Terrorism Security Office (NaCTSO) promotes an awareness raising scheme which encourages businesses and individuals to report suspicious activities and transactions involving chemicals, fertiliser and equipment of security concern
  • For more information on the security of Class 1 Explosives: HSE Security requirements for manufacture and storage of explosives
  • You should consider the planning system when putting in new physical security measures - the planning portal website includes a planning guide for small businesses that will help you determine whether planning permission is required
  • Compliance with security requirements should be considered in conjunction with developing your business continuity plan
  • Physical site security guidance from the Centre for the protection of National Infrastructure

Carriage by road only

  • DfT has produced a security training film for drivers of vehicles carrying dangerous goods; managers and employers of those drivers; and depot staff having responsibilities for communications with those drivers whilst on the road - please use our ‘Application form’ to apply for a copy
  • Please also use our ‘Security film notes for trainers’ which support the dangerous goods road security training film
  • The Serious Organised Crime Agency and UK Border Agency have produced a Lorry Crime Prevention Booklet with a focus on preventing illegal immigration across the Channel but which reinforces DfT’s transport security messages

Enforcement of the requirements

The Department for Transport and the Office for Nuclear Regulation enforce the security requirements of ADR and RID in Great Britain. For enquiries on legislation concerning Northern Ireland please contact the Health and Safety Executive Northern Ireland.

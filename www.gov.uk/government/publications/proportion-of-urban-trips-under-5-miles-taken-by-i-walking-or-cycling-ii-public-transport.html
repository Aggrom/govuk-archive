  • In 2011, 39% of urban trips under 5 miles in England were taken by walking or cycling and 9% were taken by public transport.

Proportion of urban trips under 5 miles taken by (i) walking or cycling; (ii) public transport: England, 2010 and 2011

Year Walking or cycling Public transport All modes
2010R 37% 10% 100%
2011 39% 9% 100%

For urban trips under 5 miles in England:

  • 39% were taken by walking or cycling in 2011 compared with 37% in 2010.
  • 9% were taken by public transport in 2011, compared with 10% in 2010.
  • London was the region with the highest proportion of walking or cycling and public transport trips, 43% and 22% respectively.
  • The region with the lowest proportion of walking or cycling trips was the West Midlands (32%).

  • The South West had the lowest proportion of public transport trips (4%).

    • Proportion of urban trips under 5 miles by main mode: England, 2007 to 2011

Background information

The proportions of walking or cycling and public transport trips are calculated using the number of trips under 5 miles as reported in the National Travel Survey by residents living in urban areas (settlements of over 3,000 population). Data are available by region and county.

Latest data

  • Provisional data show that 81.9% of journeys made on Highways Agency managed roads between December 2011 and November 2012 were ‘on time’. This is 0.4 percentage points lower than the previous rolling year, ending in October 2012.
  • During November 2012, provisional data show that 76.6% of journeys on Highways Agency’s motorway and ‘A’ road network were ‘on time’. This is 4.7 percentage points lower than the equivalent figure for November 2011.
  • The annual reliability measure consistently increased up to March 2012, but has fallen in the last eight months, and is now at a similar level to that observed in mid-2011. 2012 was the second wettest year since records began with substantial rainfall during the final three quarters of the year. Significant amounts of rainfall compared to 2011 will have led to slower speeds on the network, slower journeys and thus a fall in reliability.

Background information

This indicator monitors the reliability of journeys made on Highways Agency’s motorway and A road network.

The indicator is measured by the percentage of ‘journeys’ on these roads that are ‘on time’. For this measure:

  • a ‘journey’ represents travel between adjacent junctions on the network
  • an ‘on time journey’ is defined as one which is completed within a set reference time, drawn from historic data on that particular section of road
  • reference journey times are updated for the April data each year - further information on the impact of updating reference times can be found in section 3 of the latest statistical release

At present, information about the reliability of journeys on individual routes, or between particular junctions on the network, are not sufficiently robust to be presented as official statistics. This is due to the range of different sources used to calculate journey times across the network.

The Department has been working with the Highways Agency to address these issues of comparability. As a result we will be making a number of methodological changes to improve the quality of the reliability measure from the publication of the January 2013 statistics (in March). More detail on these changes will be provided in updated technical guidance.

Journey time reliability data for individual road sections is currently published by the Highways Agency through the data.gov.uk webpage.

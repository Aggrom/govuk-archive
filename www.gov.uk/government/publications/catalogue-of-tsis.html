Transverse TSIs (including downloads)

So called ‘transverse’ TSIs potentially apply to all subsystems of both the High Speed and conventional rail systems. Contracting Entities will need to consider their applicability to each and every project. Transverse TSIs apply to both high speed and conventional networks.

  • Safety in railway tunnels (SRT)
  • Accessibility for persons with reduced mobility (PRM)

These TSIs came into force in the UK on 1 July 2008.

The existing domestic accessibility regimes (the Rail Vehicle Accessibility Regulations (RVAR) and the Train and Station Services for Disabled Passengers - A Code of Practice) have been amended to reflect the incoming PRM TSI. To avoid dual regulation of heavy rail vehicles by domestic and European accessibility regimes, the Rail Vehicle Accessibility (Interoperable Rail System) Regulations 2008 came into force on 7 July. These regulations remove those trains used on the interoperable rail system from the scope of RVAR although there remains a requirement for train operators to maintain their vehicles to the standards to which they were built, ie RVAR. They also set an ‘end date’, of 1 January 2020, by which time all heavy rail vehicles must be accessible.

RVAR continues to apply to light rail (metro, underground and tram) services such as the Docklands Light Railway and London Underground.

Please contact the Interoperability team in DfT via interoperability@dft.gsi.gov.uk.

High Speed TSIs (including downloads)

The first High Speed TSIs became effective on 30 November 2002. A process of review and revision has been under way for some time.

Revised High Speed TSIs are now emerging from the revision process, being notified to Member States and published in the Official Journal. Until such time as the OJ versions are available, we will, as quickly as possible, include them below.

CoCoSig TSIs

Please note that, although the CoCoSig TSI itself has not been revised (please see the 2002 list below for a link to the original HS CCS TSI), there have been three modifications to Annex A, as follows:

  • High Speed command control and signalling TSI second modification to amended Annex A (2007/386) - (PDF, 44KB)
  • High Speed command control and signalling TSI modification to amended Annex A (2007/153) - (PDF, 44KB)
  • High Speed command control and signalling TSI second modification to amended Annex A (2008/386/EC) - (PDF)

Any contracting entity to whose project a revised High Speed TSI may apply, whether that contract is in the course of performance or in the preparatory stages, should consult with their NoBo and if necessary with the Competent Authority (DfT in Great Britain, the Department for Regional Development Northern Ireland for Northern Ireland) to clarify how this will affect their project, and should consider what derogations they may need to seek.

Please contact the Interoperability team in DfT via interoperability@dft.gsi.gov.uk.

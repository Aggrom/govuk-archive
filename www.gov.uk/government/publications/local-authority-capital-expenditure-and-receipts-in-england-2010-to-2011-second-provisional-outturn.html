Detail

The latest national statistics on the 2nd provisional outturn figures of local authority capital expenditure and receipts in the financial year April 2010 to March 2011 was released on 8 September 2011 under arrangements approved by the UK Statistics Authority.

The latest statistics release includes data from 2006-07 to 2010-11 and updates those statistics previously released on 28 June 2011.

  • Capital expenditure by local authorities in England rose to £23.1 billion in 2010-11, a year-on-year increase of 8 per cent, due mainly to the Greater London Authority’s expenditure via TfL. If the GLA’s expenditure were excluded from the England totals, capital expenditure was virtually unchanged from 2009-10.
  • Capital expenditure has risen by nearly 42 per cent in the last four years, 28 per cent in real terms.
  • Capital receipts were unchanged in 2010-11, at £1.5 billion.

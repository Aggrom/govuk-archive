The Armed Forces Covenant sets out the relationship between the Nation, the State and the Armed Forces. It recognises that the whole nation has a moral obligation to members of the Armed Forces and their families, and it establishes how they should expect to be treated. Below are just a few examples of how the Covenant is making a difference to the lives of Service children and young people:

Further Education And University Scholarships For Bereaved Service Children. The MOD administers a scheme to provide university and further education scholarships for the children of Servicemen and Women who have been killed on active duty since 1990. The aim of this scheme is to give the children of those who have died in the service of their country a head start in life by enabling them to obtain a university degree or further education training.The scheme is open to children whose parent died, on or after 1 Jan 1990, whilst serving in HM Armed Forces, where the death was deemed to be attributable to their service. The Community Covenant Grant Scheme which helps fund local projects that bring together the Armed Forces community and the civilian communities. Successful projects include children in Gloucestershire getting funding for places on ‘Go Ape’ children’s assault courses, supported by the 1st Battalion The Rifles; a play area for the Little Rascals Creche in the Vale of Glamorgan and funding to replace the Scout and Guide Headquarters in Bedale, North Yorks, with a new building that can be used by community groups; The Service Pupil Premium. Every school in England that has a Service child identified on their school roll during the yearly January school census can receive 250 pounds per child per year as part of the Pupil Premium scheme. The £3M Support Fund. In 2011 the Government introduced a new fund of 3 million pounds every year until 2014 to support UK state schools in catering for Service and Reservist children. For further information please use the link on the right hand side. Schools are allowed exceptionally and on a case-by-case basis to exceed their maximum admissions numbers to make a place available for a Service child, through the Schools Admissions Code. The link on the right hand side of this page accesses further information. Free Early Education Places for 2 year olds In May 2012 the Government confirmed that two-year-olds in England who live in households which meet the eligibility criteria also used for free school meals will be entitled to a free early education place from Sept 2013. In England, children are eligible to receive free school meals if their parent(s) are in receipt of any of the following benefits:

Income Support; Income-based Job Seekers’ Allowance; Income-related Employment and Support Allowance; Support under Part VI of the Immigration and Asylum Act 1999; The Guaranteed element of State Pension Credit; Child Tax Credit, provided they are not also entitled to Working Tax Credit and have an annual gross income of no more than £16,190, as assessed by Her Majesty’s Revenue and Customs.

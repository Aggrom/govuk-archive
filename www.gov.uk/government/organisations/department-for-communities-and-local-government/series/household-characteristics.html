In this series

This series covers trends in:

  • number of households by tenure
  • length of residence by tenure
  • economic status by tenure
  • household type by tenure
  • current tenure by previous tenure for households that have moved in the past 12 months
  • overcrowding by region
  • average household size by accommodation type.

Data tables in this section are mainly derived from the Survey of English Housing (SEH), a continuous household survey based on interviews with an annual sample of 20,000 private households, or the Labour Force Survey (LFS), also a continuous household survey but it is based on a much larger sample of households than the SEH.

In 2008 the Survey of English Housing was merged with the English House Condition Survey to form the new English Housing Survey. Updates for individual tables previously based on SEH or LFS data are therefore not available here.

The SEH was designed to provide Communities and Local Government with information on the housing arrangements and preferences of private households, thereby providing answers to a range of information needs that arise in developing and monitoring housing policies. The SEH provides key housing data on tenure (owner occupiers, social renters and private renters) and the attitudes of households to their area. The survey collects both factual and attitudinal information.

It should be noted that these tables represent a small fraction of the range of tables available from the SEH, the LFS and other household surveys. The full range of available data tables from each of the household surveys can be found on their respective websites.

The main report of the SEH survey entitled Housing in England is published annually. Other supplementary reports are published as part of the Housing Statistics Summary Series (see Housing Statistics Annuals and Occasional topic summaries). Some of the live tables available here are also included in the Housing Statistics Annual.

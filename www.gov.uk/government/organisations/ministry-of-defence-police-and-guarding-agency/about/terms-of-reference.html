Terms of reference

Accountability

In its policing role MDP is entirely independent of political or departmental control in the maintenance and operation of the criminal law. However, the Defence Secretary may set priorities for policing, determine and provide resources and appoint the management of the agency.

Chief Constable of the MOD Police is Steve Love who was appointed in June 2005 and is a former Deputy Chief Constable of Humberside Police and Assistant Chief Constable at Thames Valley Police.

MOD Police Committee

The independently chaired MOD Police Committee overseas police powers exercised by the MDP. It is not a police authority in the standard sense because it does not allocate financial and other resource, which is provided by the MOD. Notwithstanding, the Committee aims to mirror police authority best practice where possible and appropriate.

Freedom of Information

MDPGA has its own Freedom of Information team who deal with FOI requests relating to MOD Police and the MOD Guard Service. The MDPGA publication scheme forms part of the wider Ministry of Defence scheme.

How the MOD Police investigates crime that involve defence In addition to supplying armed policing to protect sensitive MOD sites, the MDP has a Criminal Investigation Department which investigates crime that impacts significantly against defence capability.

This can include theft or damage to key military assets; criminal threats to the physical security of the MOD estate such as damage to security fences, barriers and control gates; or crime on service family accommodation estates - particularly during overseas deployment of service personnel.

Crimes investigated by other police forces

The local police force will take the lead on more serious offences including deaths on military establishments, Official Secrets Act inquiries, and terrorism investigations - where the Metropolitan Police take the national lead. MDP are often called on to provide defence policing expertise.

The relationship between MDP and Home Office and Scottish Government police forces is governed by agreed protocols.

Major Incident Units

MDP has two major incident units incorporating specialist computer systems to support major investigations undertaken on MOD sites, ranging from serious and complex fraud and large-scale theft to serious sexual offences and work-related deaths.

Fraud Squad

The MDP Fraud Squad investigates serious fraud and corruption offences against the MOD. The squad works closely with the MOD’s Defence Fraud Analysis Unit and also provides fraud awareness and prevention advice across the MOD. On major cases, the squad works closely with the Serious Fraud Office.

Fraud awareness

Any fraud that diverts resources away from the frontline defence of the UK is damaging, potentially dangerous (eg covert delivery of substandard equipment) and, if undetected, costly. The end result of undetected fraud is a weakening of the UK’s defence capability and a threat to our servicemen and women.

The MDP Fraud Squad supports the MOD procurement officers who oversee the thousands of contractors large and small who supply goods and services to defence. Allegations are always treated seriously and if appropriate investigated by MDP CID officers.

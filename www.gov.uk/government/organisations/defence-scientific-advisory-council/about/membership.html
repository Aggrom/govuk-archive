Defence Scientific
Advisory Council

Membership

Find out who sits on the Defence Scientific Advisory Council (DSAC).

Chairman DSAC

Professor Ian Poll, OBE FREng FCGI FAIAA FRAeS

Ian Poll is Professor of Aerospace Engineering at Cranfield University and Technical Director of Cranfield Aerospace Ltd.

He has particular expertise in fluid mechanics, thermodynamics, air vehicle performance and design and the impact of aviation on the environment.

Deputy Chairman

Dr Andy Low OBE FREng FIET FInstP

Dr Low is a senior industrialist with extensive experience in the research and development of advanced systems for defence.

He is particularly interested in the application of modern system engineering skills to complex programmes and in the management of R&T within a highly constrained cost/risk/performance regime.

Other independent council members

Mr John Ames, FIET

Mr Ames is Director of Kenilworth Carr Consulting Ltd.

Until recently he was a senior BT Executive with 30 years experience in the Communications industry. He is a Chartered Engineer and Fellow of the FIET (formally IEE). His current roles include: technology futures consultant for BT and Associate to Futurizon, a future technology, market and strategy consulting company.

Professor Peter G Blain CBE

Professor Blain is Director of the Medical Toxicology Centre at the University of Newcastle. He is chairman of the Advisory Group on Military Medicine (MOD) and the Expert Group on the Medical Management of Casualties from Terrorism (DH).

His major research projects are in neurotoxicology, diagnostics biomarkers, CBRN medical countermeasures and emergency response interventions.

Professor Helga Drummond

Professor Drummond is the Professor of Decision Sciences at the University of Liverpool’s Management School.

Her research interests are in social and psychological aspects of decision making and phenomenological sociology including IT, systems operation and risk management.

Professor Peter Fryer FIChemE FREng

Peter Fryer is the Head of the School of Chemical Engineering at the University of Birmingham.

His research interests lie along the interface between engineering and biology, and have included projects on microbial food safety, hygienic design, manufacturing of chocolate and the engineering modelling of eating and digestion.

Professor Doug Greenhalgh, FIS

Professor Greenhalgh is a Pro Vice Chancellor and Executive Dean of Engineering and Built Environment at Glasgow Caledonian University.

His current research concerns the sensitive detection and measurement of atmospheric or gas-phase nanoparticles and has over 130 publications in the fields of chemistry, physics, optics and engineering.

Professor Peter Grindrod, CBE, FIMA

Professor Grindrod is Professor of Mathematics at the University of Reading and Co-Director of the Center for the Mathematics of Human Behaviour.

His recent research is in the field of evolving graphs and their applications to social media, digital marketing, and peer to peer communications.

Professor Peter Johnson, FBCS

Professor Johnson is Professor of Computing Science at the University of Bath.

His research expertise lies in the cross-section of Psychology and Computer Science, concerned with human interaction with and in systems.

Dr Mike Markin, OBE, FRAeS

Now retired, Dr Markin was Director General of Research and Technology at MOD HQ and a founder member of the Joint Capabilities Board responsible for specifying the Defence Future Equipment Programme.

Originally specialising as an optical physicist, much of his career was based at the Royal Aircraft Establishment Farnborough researching the full range of electro-optical sensors and countermeasures to them, and including wider responsibilities, for example for test and experimental flying, a broad range of guided weapon projects, and the initial audit of the Eurofighter programme.

Professor Ursula Martin CBE, FIET

Professor Ursula Martin is Director of the impactQM project at Queen Mary University of London, and a Professor of Computer Science in the School of Electronic Engineering and Computer Science.

Her research interests concern the application of computer science techniques to engineering design.

Professor John McDermid, OBE, FREng

Professor John McDermid is Head of Department of Computer Science at the University of York.

He works with the professional bodies, including the Royal Academy of Engineering, seeking to improve standards in software and systems engineering.

Professor Gareth Padfield, BSc, PhD, CEng, FRAeS, FREng

Gareth Padfield formally retired in November 2011 and now holds an Emeritus Professorship at Liverpool and runs a consultancy business Flight Stability and Control.

Professor Douglas Paul, FinstP, FRSE

Professor Paul is the Professor of Semiconductor Devices and Director of the James Watt Nanofabrication Centre in the School of Engineering at the University of Glasgow.

His main research interests lie in nanoelectronics, nanofabrication, autonomous sensing, millimetre-wave and terahertz technology, energy harvesting, thermoelectrics and Peltier coolers.

Mrs Judith Rawle, FORS

Judith Rawle is the Head of Capability at CORDA, a decision support consulting business wholly owned by BAE Systems.

Mrs Rawle has more than 25 years’ experience of Operational Analysis and business modelling across the defence domain. She is particularly interested in analysis to support policy and strategy development; counter terrorism; and acquisition.

Professor Sarah Spurgeon FREng, FIET, FInstMC, FIMA, CEng, CMath

Professor Spurgeon is the Professor of Control Engineering and Head of the School of Engineering and Digital Arts at the University of Kent.

Her research interests are in the area of systems modelling and analysis, robust control and estimation in which areas she has published over 270 refereed research papers.

Dr Martyn Thomas, CBE, FREng, FIET, FBCS

Dr Martyn Thomas is an independent consultant software engineer with particular expertise in safety-related and other critical computer systems.

Professor Simon Wessely, FMedSci

Professor Simon Wessely is the Head and Chair of the Department of Psychological Medicine and also Vice Dean for Academic Psychiatry at the Institute of Psychiatry, King’s College London.

His current research interests include epidemiological studies on the health consequences of service with the UK Armed forces in the 1991 Gulf War and the conflicts in Iraq and Afghanistan.

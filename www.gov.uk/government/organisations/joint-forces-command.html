We work toward making military operations successful by making sure joint capabilities - like medical services, training and education, intelligence, and cyber-operations - are efficiently managed and supported. We also communicate actual experience in operational theatres so that it can be reflected in top-level decision making.

Responsibilities

We are responsible for making sure that:

  • investment in joint capabilities makes sense
  • lessons learned from operations are applied in a more structured way

Priorities

In 2012 to 2013 our priorities will be:

  • ensuring UK forces are as prepared as possible in operational theatres by communicating risk and past experience better
  • prioritising joint enabling capabilities when it comes to deciding on resources, and making sure actual demand is met
  • saving money by managing and investing in capabilities more efficiently
  • developing defence and joint capabilities even further after the Joint Forces Command reaches reaches its full role in April 2013

Who we are

The Commander of Joint Forces Command is Air Chief Marshal Sir Stuart Peach. Our HQ is in Northwood and the command is represented at sites across the UK.

We are made up of the following organisations:

  • Permanent Joint Headquarters (PJHQ)
  • The Permanent Joint Operating Bases (PJOBs) (Gibraltar, Cyprus, British Indian Ocean Territory and South Atlantic Islands)
  • Joint Force Headquarters (JFHQ)
  • Joint Force Logistics Component (JFLogC)
  • Joint Counter-Terrorist Training and Advisory Team (JCTTAT)
  • Directorate of Special Forces (DSF)
  • Defence Academy (Def Ac)
  • Development Concepts and Doctrine Centre (DCDC)
  • Defence Intelligence (DI)
  • Surgeon General (SG)
  • Joint Arms Control Implementation Group (JACIG)
  • Defence Centre of Training Support (DCTS)
  • Defence Cyber Operations Group (DCOG)

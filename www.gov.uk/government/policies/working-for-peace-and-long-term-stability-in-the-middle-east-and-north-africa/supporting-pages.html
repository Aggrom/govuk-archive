The crisis in Syria

The Syrian uprising began in March 2011 with national protests against the ruling Ba’ath Party calling for the resignation of President Assad and demanding legitimate rights. Over 30,000 people have been killed since March 2011 and more are dying each week, mostly at the hands of the Assad regime.

We are supporting the UN-Arab League Special Representative Brahimi in his efforts to implement the Geneva communique, which sets out a process of political transition that was agreed by the permanent 5 members of the Security Council and the Arab League in June.

We continue to believe that there is a need for a Chapter VII Resolution that will let the Security Council take such action by air, sea, or land forces as may be necessary to reach a peaceful settlement and will continue our efforts to that end. However, in the absence of Russian and Chinese agreement to a resolution, we are intensifying our work in 4 areas: * supporting the Syrian people and political opposition * increasing pressure on the Syrian regime, including through EU sanctions and seeking accountability through the UN’s Commission of Inquiry * preparing for a political transition * helping mitigate the humanitarian and regional effects of the crisis

On 20 September 2012 the Foreign Secretary set out to Parliament that the UK had decided to recognise the National Coalition of Syrian Revolutionary and Opposition Forces as the sole legitimate representative of the Syrian people. This imposes responsibilities on the government, and we will continue to press them to uphold their commitments.

The UK is also increasing its practical support for the Syrian Opposition. This adds to the training of citizen journalists, human rights advocates, doctors and Syrian activists that we have already provided, and the generators, communications equipment and water purification kits for unarmed opposition groups and civil society. Alongside that increased political and practical support, the UK is pressing the EU to increase its support to civil society in Syria.

The government is responding to serious human rights concerns in Syria. We have played an important role in UN Human Rights Council discussions on the escalating human rights violations and deteriorating humanitarian situation, and in resolutions adopted by the Council on 1 and 23 March 2012 condemning these human rights violations. On 28 November the United Nations General Assembly also adopted an Arab-led resolution on the human rights situation in Syria condemning the widespread and systematic human rights violations being committed by the Syrian regime and its militia.

Improving governance and economies

The government renewed Britain’s relationship with the Overseas Territories in June 2012 with the publication of its white paper, The Overseas Territories: security, success and sustainability.

The paper covers 6 main areas:

Defence, security and safety

Our priorities are to work with territory governments to address security threats such as organised crime and illegal migration, including through strengthened border security and the development of National Security Councils.

Successful economies

We are working with territory governments to:

  • build economic strength through improved financial management and diversification
  • help territories in receipt of budgetary aid to achieve sustainable growth and financial independence from the UK
  • increase trade and investment between the UK and the territories
  • protect trade rights and preferences

Environment

We are working with territory governments to manage natural resources sustainably. We are addressing the challenges of climate change by putting environmental considerations in all government policies and decision-making.

We will:

  • oversee exemplary environmental management of the uninhabited Territories
  • ensure compliance with the requirements of relevant multilateral environmental agreements
  • strengthen co-operation with the non-governmental and scientific communities

Better government

We are working with territory governments to:

  • monitor and report progress on good governance, public financial management and economic planning
  • continue developing democratic institutions that serve and take account of the interests of all the people in the territories
  • take necessary action to safeguard fundamental rights and freedoms and beat discrimination
  • ensure effective justice systems
  • strengthen public service, including through increased secondments between the UK and the Territories
  • strengthen assurance that public spending provides value for money
  • act whenever there is evidence of corruption or maladministration, work to bring in anti-bribery legislation and to sign up to the relevant international conventions

Communities

We will continue to provide development assistance to territories in need. We are working with territory governments to:

  • improve education systems and encourage and enable students to study in the UK
  • build sustainable healthcare systems and prepare for health emergencies in line with international obligations
  • encourage employment and ensure the most vulnerable members of society are protected
  • build partnerships between the Territories and UK local government
  • celebrate the cultural heritage of the Territories and support their participation in international sporting events.

Productive links with the wider world

We are working with territory governments to:

  • develop the territories’ special relationship with the EU, particularly in trade, educational and cultural links
  • ensure EU support is continued, processes are simplified and assistance is used effectively
  • successfully negotiate the successor to the Overseas Association Decision, the EU’s economic development plan for overseas countries and territories
  • strengthen engagement with the Commonwealth, particularly programmes for small states
  • encourage co-operation with regional partners in the Caribbean
  • develop with the South Atlantic Territories a long-term strategy to improve the region’s economy

Thameslink programme

The Thameslink programme comprises the upgrading of infrastructure, the deployment of new rolling stock and the introduction of a new Thameslink franchise to achieve 24 trains per hour through London’s core, almost doubling current capacity.

Through the Department for Transport’s implementation of this £6 billion programme, rail passengers will benefit from more frequent and less crowded trains on one of the south-east’s busiest rail routes. The trains will also be lighter, more reliable and more energy efficient.

New trains procurement

Siemens plc and Cross London Trains – a consortium comprising Siemens Project Ventures GmbH, Innisfree Ltd and 3i Infrastructure plc – have been appointed as the preferred bidder to build, own, finance and maintain the new trains. Two new maintenance depots for the new trains will be built at Three Bridges near Crawley and at Hornsey in the London Borough of Haringey. Siemens have stated that up to 2,000 new jobs will be created in the UK on this project.

New infrastructure

The £4.5 billion infrastructure investment will allow longer trains to run with new, fast and frequent London connections to towns and cities both north and south of the capital. Significant investment has been made in both Blackfriars and Farringdon stations, which were fully operational in time for the Olympics, and longer 12 carriage trains are now in operation.

Looking ahead

Enabling works at London Bridge Station are already underway, in preparation for the main station redevelopment due to start in the New Year. This redevelopment will be complete by January 2018 and ready for 24 trains per hour by June 2018.

Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development guidelines

The OECD guidelines for multinational enterprises are a voluntary code for businesses that operate internationally.

The principles and standards cover a wide area and include:

  • human rights
  • employment
  • industrial relations
  • reducing bribery
  • competition
  • taxation

For full details, have a look at the section on the guidelines on the OECD website.

Who’s signed up?

43 governments have agreed to follow the guidelines – 34 OECD members (including the UK) and 9 non-members. They’re from all regions of the world, and control 85% of foreign direct investment between them.

Each government taking part must provide a National Contact Point (NCP) to:

  • inform businesses, trade unions and NGOs about the guidelines
  • look at complaints that businesses have not acted consistently with the guidelines

How does it work?

The guidelines are voluntary, but we expect UK multinationals to follow them.

If someone (such as an NGO) has evidence that a UK multinational is breaking any of the guidelines – for example by treating local workers or communities badly – they can report them to the UK NCP.

Although the NCP looks at all the complaints we receive, we expect people who complain to us to have a close interest in the case. They should also be able to give us detailed information about it.

Complaints that we take on go to mediation first. If this doesn’t work, we’ll investigate the case and make a public statement about whether the company broke the guidelines.

Contacting the NCP

The UK NCP is based at BIS and is partly funded by the Department of International Development (DFID).

Please have a look at the UK NCP procedures and read BIS’s procedures on how to make a complaint before you get in touch.

You can email the NCP at: uk.ncp@bis.gsi.gov.uk

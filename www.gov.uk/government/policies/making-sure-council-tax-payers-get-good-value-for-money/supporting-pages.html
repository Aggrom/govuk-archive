Supporting detail:

Council Tax freeze

Government provided £652.2 million in 2011 to 2012 and £675 million in 2012 to 2013 to help councils, police and fire authorities to avoid increasing their Council Tax charges. Any authority that agreed not to increase its Council Tax was eligible to receive a grant from the government equivalent to a 2.5% increase in Council Tax.

As a result, the average Band D Council Tax in England did not rise in 2011 to 2012; 85% of local councils (359 out of 421) did not increase Band D Council Tax charges. The average Band D Council Tax charge rose by 0.3% in 2012 to 2013.

On 8 October 2012 the Chancellor of the Exchequer announced an extra £450 million to help freeze Council Tax bills in England for a further year. This means that taxpayers living in an average Band D home in England could save up to £72. A freeze in Council Tax in 2013 to 2014 would represent a cut of around 2% that year and a 9% reduction in real terms from 201 to 2013.

The £450 million will be made available through a new grant scheme to local authorities who decide to freeze or reduce their Council Tax in 2013. If they do, councils, police and fire authorities will receive £225 million of funding in both financial years 2013 to 2014 and 2014 to 2015, equivalent to raising their 2012 to 2013 Council Tax by 1%.

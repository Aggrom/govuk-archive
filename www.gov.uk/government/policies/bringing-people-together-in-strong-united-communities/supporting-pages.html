Projects to support strong communities

Government is taking action when it can make a genuine difference. Action is usually most effective when it is led by the people it most concerns. But in a few cases government can, and should, promote targeted activities which can make a real difference, acting in partnership with businesses, voluntary organisations and communities.

DCLG does not run a national programme to deliver integration across the country, but is supporting over 30 projects which set direction and pioneer innovative ideas in order to encourage leadership and mobilise action at a local level.

These projects build on the widespread celebration of national identity seen during the Queen’s Diamond Jubilee and the London Olympic Games and form part of a wider campaign promoting the importance of community participation, through projects such as:

  • The Big Lunch - in May 2012 we announced £185,000 of funding for 2 years to help 20 communities each year organise their own street parties and events
  • national Inter Faith Week, held every November by the Inter Faith Network, to bring people of different faiths together to serve their communities and learn more about each other
  • the National Citizen Service programme, which brings young people together from a wide range of backgrounds to develop their skills, contribute to their communities and promote integration – we are committed to having 90,000 young people in the scheme in 2014
  • Young Advisors, which helps young people who’ve been through the National Citizen Service programme to continue social action in their local areas
  • giving £10 million to Youth United to recruit and train up to 2,700 new adult volunteers to run 400 new groups of their member organisations in communities across the country
  • Near Neighbours – we’re providing £5 million over 3 years (2011 to 2014) to the Church Urban Fund to get people of different faiths working together on projects to improve their neighbourhood
  • A Year Of Service - a series of volunteering days in 2012 to promote different aspects of service like helping the homeless or caring for the elderly
  • giving young people a chance to work together and learn about responsibility and self-discipline, though a £10 million grant over 2 and a half years to support the Scouts Association, Girlguiding UK, Army Cadets, Volunteer Police Cadets and St John Ambulance
  • enabling people to realise their potential to get on in life, by supporting English language learning in local communities

More information about possible funding for projects can be found at the Government Funding Portal.

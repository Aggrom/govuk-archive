Helping UK business overcome trade barriers

British companies can sometimes have trouble operating outside the UK. This can be because of lack of information or misunderstandings about rules.

It can also be because another country imposes an unfair increase in duty or import levy on UK business. Measures like this are known as trade barriers.

What is a trade barrier?

There are several different types of trade barrier. They include tariffs and non-tariff barriers.

A tariff is the amount of import duty charged on a particular type of goods. Non-tariff trade barriers can include trade regulations, labelling rules and unfair government subsidies intended to favour local producers.

What can I do when I come up against a trade barrier?

We need to know about any trade barriers as soon as possible. You can tell us via UK Trade and Investment (UKTI) or the Trade Policy Unit (TPU)

We can negotiate about trade barriers directly with the country concerned, or we can deal with them through the EU. We may do both.

As an example of how things can work, we negotiated through the EU in 2012 when the Colombian authorities redefined what they meant by alcoholic drinks. This new definition could have affected sales of Scotch whisky in Colombia.The EU took the matter up with Colombia, who changed their definition as a result. This made it easier for Scotch whisky to sell in the region.

The European Commission’s market access strategy

The Commission’s market access strategy deals with trade barriers outside the EU. An alliance of European Commission delegates, officials from the embassies of the various countries, trade associations and business will lobby the country they’re based in to dismantle local trade barriers. Negotiations also take place at the Commission’s offices in Brussels.

The Commission has a number of ways to deal with trade barriers.

The Market Access Database (MAD)

This is an online database. It provides a wide range of information for exporters, including information on trade barriers and import duties. It can be searched by product, region or country. It allows businesses to report barriers they’ve come up against directly to the Commission.

The Trade Barriers Regulation (TBR)

This regulation allows the Commission to investigate complaints about violations of international trade rules.

Negotiations for World Trade Organization (WTO) membership

The Commission uses individual countries’ negotiations for membership of the WTO to push for greater freedom in world trade.

Responsibilities

The minister is responsible for:

  • rail strategy (including overall fares policy)
  • London - including Crossrail
  • HS2
  • international and European engagement
  • aviation – including Air Accident Branch

Current role holder: Simon Burns MP

Simon Burns was appointed Minister of State for Transport in September 2012. He is the Conservative MP for Chelmsford.

Education

He was educated at Christ the King School in Ghana and Stamford School, Lincolnshire before going on to study modern history at Worcester College, Oxford.

Political career

Highlights include: * MP for Chelmsford, 1987 to present * Shadow Health Minister, 2004 to 2005 * Opposition Whip, 2005 to 2010 * Minister of State for Health, 2010 to 2012 * Minister of State for Transport September, 2012 to present

Career outside politics

Before becoming an MP, Simon worked in manufacturing, consultancy, property and construction, and oil and gas, both in the UK and abroad.

Personal life

Simon has a daughter and a son.

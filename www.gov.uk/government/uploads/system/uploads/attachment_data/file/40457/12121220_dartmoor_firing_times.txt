Warning signals, red flags by day and red lamps by night, 
indicate that live firing is taking place within the range boundaries, 
which are marked by red and white poles.  When warning signals are 
displayed do not cross the range boundary.  If the warning signals 
are not hoisted by 0900 (Apr-Sep) or 1000 (Oct - Mar) no live firing 
will take place that day and any bangs you hear will be blank 
cartridges or fireworks.  DAY firing is between sunrise and sunset.  
NIGHT firing is between sunset and sunrise.  Flags are lowered as soon 
as live firing ceases.

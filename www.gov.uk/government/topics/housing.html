Increasing protection for homeowners updated 14 November 2012 18:40 News article
Offering suitable housing to homeless people published 14 November 2012 18:04 Consultation
English indices of deprivation published 14 November 2012 12:38 Consultation

The government is helping local councils and developers work with local communities to plan and build better places to live for everyone. This includes building affordable housing, improving the quality of rented housing, helping more people to buy a home, and providing housing support for vulnerable people.

Policies

  • Providing housing support for older and vulnerable people

    We are committed to providing housing advice and housing support services to those who need it most.

  • Increasing the number of available homes

    We're helping to get Britain building again, through schemes like the NewBuy Guarantee, New Homes Bonus and loan guarantees for developers.

  • Improving the rented housing sector

    We're improving the quality and quantity of social homes for rent and encouraging investment in the private rented sector.

  • Helping people to buy a home

    We're helping people who want to own their own home through schemes like Right to Buy, FirstBuy and NewBuy, and encouraging self-builders.

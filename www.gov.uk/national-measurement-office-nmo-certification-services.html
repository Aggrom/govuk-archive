Introduction

The National Measurement Office (NMO) is responsible for managing and developing the UK’s National Measurement System network.

This guide provides an overview of the NMO’s Certification Services to industry and businesses, including product certification (UK National Approval under the Weights & Measures Act 1985 and European Commission type examination under the Non-automatic Weighing Instruments and Measuring Instruments Directives), calibration of mass, length and volume standards, and quality management system certification.

Services to industry

The NMO offers professional services that include:

  • calibration
  • product certification (type approval)
  • Quality Management Systems (QMS) certification

Calibration

If your business needs to ensure that its use of mass, length and volume is correct and accurate, the NMO Calibration Team offers equipment calibration services that are UK Accreditation Service accredited to ISO 17025. If your business needs to accurately measure mass, length or volume, the NMO offers a service to calibrate your measures to demonstrate your traceability to national standards in their purpose-built laboratory.

Mass

The NMO mass calibration service provides UK Accreditation Service (UKAS) accredited in accordance with ISO 17025, over a range of mass from 1 milligram to 1,000 kilograms. The NMO can calibrate to a high degree of accuracy that is better than E1 for 1 milligram to 50 kilograms, E2 (one part per two million) or better than up to 500 kilograms and F2 (three parts per million) up to 1,000 kilograms.

Length

NMO can calibrate length up to 50 metres using the NMO’s tape bench, which is the longest in the UK. Note that longer length calibrations can be made with lengths that are multiples of 50 metres.

All length calibrations are UKAS-accredited using a laser-based interferometer. Accuracy of calibration is to 10+0.6L micrometres. Rigid line standards up to 2.3 metres long can be calibrated with uncertainties of 1+1L micrometres.

Volume

The need for the accurate measurement of volume is required in a number of industries, including petro-chemical and pharmaceutical sectors. NMO offers a UKAS-accredited calibration service for volume that has a range of 1 millilitre to 20 litres for glassware and 1 litre to 500 litres for metal measures. They also offer an on-site service for larger volume vessels - however, this is not UKAS-accredited.

How often should instruments be calibrated?

The recalibration period of any item depends on its stability and can be affected by usage, how the item is kept, the construction of the item, age etc. The NMO recommends an initial recalibration period of one year.

As a general rule, any item which has not been calibrated for over five years is normally considered to be out of calibration. Items which are used for trade purposes have their recalibration period laid down in the Weights and Measures Act 1985.

National Measurement Office service forms

The National Weights and Measures Laboratory (NWML) is part of the National Measurement Office (NMO) and provides services to manufacturers, installers and repairers of weighing and measuring equipment and to any business in which the weighing and measuring elements are vital.

This is to ensure that legal requirements and benchmarks are standard throughout industries that package, weigh or provide goods in specified quantities.

Please note that the forms on this site are free to complete, but costs will be quoted by NMO in response to submitting the forms below.

Application for Pre-Assessment - make an application for pre-compliance testing of equipment (prior to making an application for product certification)

Application for Calibration - request calibration of mass, length or volume standards to demonstrate traceability to national and international standards

Product Certification - access a range of type approval services for new designs of weighing and measuring instruments enabling access to the UK, European and global markets

Quality Management System Certification - request an assessment of a quality management system against the requirements of ISO9001 and/or EC Directives associated with ‘self-verification’

For any enquiries relating to these services, please contact the NMO Enquiry Line on 020 8943 7272 or email info@nmo.gov.uk.

Product certification

NMO offers a range of type approval services that your business can use to gain access to the global and European markets for measuring and weighing instruments. This includes European Commission (EC) type examination under the Non-Automatic Weighing Instrument (NAWI) Directive 2009/23/EC and the Measuring Instruments Directive (MID) 2004/22/EC, issuing International Organization of Legal Metrology Certificates of Conformity, and UK National Approval under section 12 of the Weights and Measures Act 1985.

The NMO is a Notified Body under the Non-Automatic Weighing Instruments (NAWI) Directive and Measuring Instruments Directive (MID). This means that NMO can issue European Commission (EC) type examination certificates for a wide range of weighing and measuring instruments.

In addition, the NMO is also the issuing authority in the UK for a number of International Organisation of Legal Metrology (OIML) certificates of conformity. OIML certificates and the associated test reports are generally accepted by countries outside Europe as a basis for granting national type approval.

NMO also issues UK National Type Approval Certificates for certain instrument types under section 12 of the Weights & Measures Act 1985.

Using the NMO for your product certification has a number of advantages that include:

  • a pre-assessment service that enables your business to submit products that meet minimum standards
  • world-class expertise
  • certification that is recognised globally
  • fast and efficient turnaround once your product has been submitted for certification

NAWIs

A NAWI EC Type Approval Certificate is used to satisfy the legal requirements your products must meet in accordance with the NAWI Directive. This Directive was implemented into UK law by the NAWI Regulations 2000.

You can apply for NAWI product certification using the online industry service forms from the NMO.

MID

The MID came into force in October 2006. The aim of the MID is to create a single European market in measuring instruments, which would benefit both manufacturers and consumers.

MID covers a diverse range of instruments, including automatic weighing instruments, fuel dispensers, material measures, and utility meters. Under the MID you can obtain an approved certificate that is valid across the European Union.

You can apply for MID product certification using the online industry service forms from the NMO.

Pre-assessment services

If you are unsure whether your products will pass the testing procedure, NWML offers a pre-assessment service whereby you can discover whether your product would pass certification if it was formally submitted.

You can apply for a product certification pre-assessment using the online industry service forms from the NMO.

You can also view current MID, NAWI, UK National and European Economic Community (EEC) certificates issued by NMO on the NMO website.

QMS certification

NMO provides the following services for QMS certification:

  • ISO 9001 certification - find details of the NMO’s auditing service on the NMO website
  • declaration of type conformity under the NAWI Directive
  • declaration of conformity based on quality assurance under the MID
  • ‘self-verification’ under section 11A of the Weights and Measures Act 1985

As the NMO is a centre for excellence in legal metrology, your business can be confident that the partnership it develops with the NMO will be professional and productive. All NMO certification services auditors are experienced in reviewing management systems to the ISO 9001:2008 standard. With a team of highly skilled auditors, the NMO can help your business achieve and maintain a high level of systems certification and management.

The current requirements for QMS certification are outlined below.

ISO 9001:2008

This standard is used as a template to compare against your organisation’s own management system. An independent body will come into your business and check that you have systems in place that are in accordance with the standard. Read about ISO 9001:2008 and QMS certification on the NMO website.

The NAWI Directive

This European Directive permits manufacturers to be certified to allow them to undertake ‘self-verification’ of their EC type approved instruments. The Directive describes this activity as ‘EC Declaration of Type Conformity’.

For your business to be granted permission to self-verify, you must have your activities assessed by a notified body. The NMO is a notified body in the UK.

MID

The MID covers a range of instrument types such as automatic weighing instruments, fuel dispensers, material measures and utility meters. The MID provides a range of conformity assessment routes, some of which are based on certification of the manufacturer quality system.

The NMO’s QMS Certification Services were designated a Notified Body under the MID in 2006.

UK Deregulation Order 1999

As an amendment to the Weights and Measures Act 1985, the UK Deregulation Order enables approved manufacturers, installers and repairers to carry out their own initial verification on specific measuring and weighing equipment.

One of the fundamental requirements for approval is to operate a QMS based on ISO 9001.

To obtain approval, your business must make an application to the Secretary of State for Weights and Measures. The Secretary of State will recognise the certification of your QMS if it has been conducted by a Certification Body approved for weights and measures. NMO is one of the few approved Certification Bodies for this role.

You can find a list of NMO-certified organisations on the NMO website.

Apply for NMO QMS certification using the online industry service forms from the NMO.

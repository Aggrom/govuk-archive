Quick answer Register for Self Assessment

Once you’ve registered for Self Assessment with HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC), you’ll get a Unique Taxpayer Reference (UTR) number and you can then send your tax return to HMRC.

Before you register

You’ll need your:

  • National Insurance number
  • personal contact details - eg address
  • business details - eg business address, date you started self-employment or joined a partnership

If you’re registering a new business partnership, you must be the ‘nominated partner’ (the partner responsible for sending in the partnership tax return).

Deadline

Register by 5 October following the end of the tax year you need to send a tax return for. This way, you’ll have enough time to complete registration before the tax return and any tax is due. If you register late, you may have to pay a penalty.

Example
Register by 5 October 2012 to send a 2011 to 2012 tax return.

How to register

How you register depends on your circumstances.

New sole traders and business partnerships can register for Self Assessment and get an online account (known as a ‘government gateway’ account) at the same time. This lets you send your tax return online.

Your circumstance Your reason for registering How to register
New sole trader You’ve not been registered before Sole trader - register for Self Assessment
Existing sole trader You’ve been registered before and need to re-register - eg you’re starting self-employment again Existing sole trader - register for Self Assessment
Partnerships You’re the nominated partner and you need to register both yourself and the business partnership Partnership - register for Self Assessment
Partner You’re joining a business partnership Partner - register for Self Assessment
Partner non-individual You’re registering a partner that’s joining a business partnership and they’re not an individual - eg a company or trust Partner non-individual - register for Self Assessment by downloading form SA402 (PDF, 95KB)
Any other reason You’re not a sole trader, partner, nominated partner or partnership Register for Self Assessment by downloading form SA1 (PDF, 65KB)

How to get an online account

If you want to send a tax return online you’ll need an HMRC online account (also known as a ‘Government Gateway’ account).

New sole traders and business partnerships automatically get an account when they register for Self Assessment. If you’re not one of these groups, you have to sign up for an online account after you’ve registered for Self Assessment.

Introduction

Working Together (WT) is a partnership between HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) and the six main agent representative bodies which look for ways to improve HMRC’s operations for the benefit of tax agents, their clients and HMRC.

This guide explains what WT does at national and local level. It also includes a link to the WT publications.

Working Together nationally

The Working Together Steering Group (WTSG) provides the strategic direction for WT and is responsible for the local WT network. The National WT Team supports local groups by providing regular communications and briefings on operational matters - as well as helping to resolve problems through the issues resolution process. 

The WTSG is made up of senior representatives from HMRC and the six main professional bodies:

  • Chartered Institute of Taxation
  • Institute of Chartered Accountants of Scotland
  • Institute of Chartered Accountants in England and Wales
  • Association of Taxation Technicians
  • Association of Accounting Technicians
  • Association of Chartered Certified Accountants

The WTSG meets three times a year and enables a free exchange of views on operational matters that are important both to HMRC and tax agents.

Key aims

The key aims of the WTSG are to:

  • focus on systemic issues where HMRC procedures and service affect tax agents and their clients
  • deal with new issues of national importance as and when they arise
  • agree key priorities and check on their progress
  • receive information on reviews or projects that are being carried out by HMRC
  • make sure that agents’ views are passed to consultation groups
  • identify opportunities to work together to improve HMRC’s service to tax agents and their clients

WT locally

WT includes a network of local groups across the UK which is an essential part of the partnership.

Key objectives of the local WT groups are to:

  • improve two-way communications between HMRC and tax agents
  • identify and clarify issues which can be escalated to the National WT team in real time
  • help HMRC focus on the best way to help tax agents and their clients
  • be open and exchange views on how the tax system works

Many of the agents in the local groups will represent one of the main representative bodies. But some will attend as part of the wider local agent community, or as a representative of other agent organisations. Agents who attend the meetings are asked to bring discussion items from their colleagues with them, and feed back to them after each meeting.

Find more information and contact details for your local WT group in our guide Local WT meetings - getting involved.

Expenses and benefits: tool allowances, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer providing a tool allowance to your employees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

What’s included

A tool allowance is a payment you give to an employee to buy tools that are only used for work.

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to report anything to HMRC or pay tax and National Insurance if you provide a tool allowance for an employee earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year.

Part 3: What to report and pay

If the tool allowances aren’t exempt, you may have to report them to HMRC and deduct tax and National Insurance on the costs.

You can apply for a dispensation to cover tool allowances. This means you won’t have to include them in your end-of year reports.

If you don’t have a dispensation, for employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year, or directors, report on form P11D, section N.

If work tools are available for employees to use privately

The allowance you provide counts as earnings. Add the amount to the employee’s other earnings, and deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance through payroll.

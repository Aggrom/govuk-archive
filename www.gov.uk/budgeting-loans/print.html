Benefits & credits: Budgeting Loans

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get a loan to help pay for essential things like rent, furniture, clothes or hire purchase debts. This is known as a ‘Budgeting Loan.’ The smallest amount you can borrow is £100.

Budgeting Loans are interest-free so you only pay back what you borrow. You normally have to repay the loan within 104 weeks.

You can apply for a loan if you’ve been getting income-related benefits for at least 26 weeks.

Budgeting Loans won’t affect your other benefits.

Part 2: What you'll get

How much you can get depends on if you:

  • are single
  • have children
  • can pay the loan back
  • have any savings over £1,000 (or £2,000 if you or your partner are over 60)
  • already owe money to the Social Fund

The total amount of all Social Fund debt that you can borrow with a Crisis Loan or Budgeting Loan cannot be more than £1,500.

How you’re paid

Loans are usually paid into your account, eg a bank account.

Part 3: Eligibility

To qualify for a Budgeting Loan you or your partner must have been getting one of these benefits for at least 26 weeks:

  • Income Support
  • income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance
  • Pension Credit

What can a loan pay for

Budgeting Loans can pay for:

  • furniture or household equipment
  • clothing or footwear
  • rent
  • removal expenses when you move home
  • travelling expenses
  • things to help you look for or start work
  • improving, maintaining or securing your home
  • maternity or funeral expenses
  • repaying hire purchase or other debts you took out to pay for any of the above

Part 4: How to claim

Send a claim form to your local Jobcentre Plus.

Download ‘SF500 - Budgeting Loan claim form - England, Wales or Scotland’ (PDF, 135KB)

Download ‘SF500 - Budgeting Loan claim form - Northern Ireland’ (PDF, 325KB)

If you have any questions about your claim, contact your local Jobcentre Plus.

Reviewing a decision on your application

If you’re unhappy with the decision on your Budgeting Loan application you’ve got the right to ask for a review. You must write to Jobcentre Plus within 28 days of the decision, explaining why you think it was wrong.

If, after the review, you still think the decision was wrong you can ask for a further review by a Social Fund Inspector. This review should take no longer than 12 days. You can download a leaflet and claim form to ask for an independent review.

Iran List: WMD End-Use control

Types of goods and destinations which may be of concern, and previous licensing decisions on Iranian organisations

Overview

The Weapons of Mass Destruction (WMD) End-Use guidance provides general information about the types of goods and destinations which could potentially be of WMD concern. However, you will often not know that an end-user, or other party to an export transaction, may be involved in a WMD programme.

Given the current concerns about Iran in particular, the Export Control Organisation (ECO) feel it would be useful to provide more specific information about end-users. Therefore, the ECO are making a list of Iranian entities publicly available for exporters.

This guide will benefit those wanting to export to Iran and gives details of previous licensing decisions made in relation to Iranian organisations or individuals.

The purpose of the Iran List

The Iran List is intended to help you judge which exports might potentially be of concern on End-Use grounds. This is based on previous licensing decisions. This information may be factored into your business planning and help you to make informed decisions on whether to contact the Export Control Organisation (ECO). Find contact details for the ECO on the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) website.

The List is amended on a monthly basis and should be checked regularly if you are planning business with Iran.

The Iran List (amended monthly)

You can download the latest Iran List from the BIS website (PDF, 581KB).

Please note the following important points in relation to the Iran List (we use the term ‘entity’ to mean companies, individuals and government bodies in Iran).

  1. The entities included on the list are mainly based on the last three years’ experience of refusing licence applications under the WMD End-Use Control. Others are included because of publicly available information which indicates their involvement in WMD programmes.
  2. Inclusion of an entity on the list does not necessarily indicate that an export licence would be refused. However, if an entity does not appear on the list, it does not necessarily mean that there are no end-user concerns with that entity. It could mean that we have not been asked to consider a licence application with them as the end-user.
  3. Checking against the list is not a substitute for a rating by the ECO, which will be based on the goods to be exported in a particular case and the latest available information on your Iranian customer.
  4. If an entity appears on the list, it may be cause for concern, whatever their involvement in an export transaction may be. This includes end-users, consignees and third parties. If you are considering a transaction which involves a company or individual on the list in any capacity, it would be wise to first submit a request to the End-User Advice Service. For more information, see the guide on strategic exports: when to request an export licence. You can make an end-user advice service request using SPIRE.
  5. The inclusion of an entity on the list does not constitute being ‘informed’ that a licence is required under the WMD End-Use control and, therefore, does not indicate that a licence must always be applied for under the WMD End-Use control. However, it may lead you to suspect that there are WMD End-Use concerns with the entity.
  6. Publication of this list in no way invalidates any existing licences that specifically permit an export to an entity on the list.

Electrical waste: producer and supplier responsibilities , a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

There are rules on how to deal with most waste electrical or electronic equipment (WEEE). These rules are known as the WEEE regulations and will apply to your business if you:

  • sell electrical and electronic equipment
  • generate WEEE
  • produce electrical or electronic equipment
  • manufacture or rebrand electrical or electronic equipment
  • import or export electrical and electronic equipment within the EU

These rules apply to most electrical and electronic equipment, there are very few exemptions.

Part 2: Suppliers

You’re a supplier (or ‘distributor’) if you sell products, even if you sell them via distance selling (eg, by telephone, internet).

Collection schemes

Customers can return their waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE) to you, this is known as ‘takeback’. There are 3 ways to provide a takeback service:

  • offer an in-store service - eg, customers can come to your shop and give you their WEEE
  • join the Distributor Takeback Scheme - you can then tell customers where their nearest WEEE recycling facility is
  • set up your own takeback scheme - this must be free and accessible

If the customer is buying a similar item from you, they can also return their old item to you. Contact the producer to dispose of this WEEE.

Fees

You can charge businesses for taking back their WEEE. You can’t charge members of the public. You can charge to collect WEEE (eg, from a house) but the fee can only cover the actual cost of collection.

Provide information

You must give customers free written information on:

  • how they can safely dispose of their WEEE
  • the benefits of the takeback scheme
  • the environmental impacts of electrical / electronic products
  • why they must separate WEEE from other waste
  • explain the WEEE labelling on the product

You can do this using posters or a website.

Keep records

You must keep records about:

  • how much WEEE you collect - these must be kept for 4 years
  • how you tell customers about WEEE

Part 3: Producers

You’re a producer if you manufacture, import, export or rebrand electrical or electronic equipment.

Recycling schemes

You’re responsible for the collection, treatment, recovery and disposal of your market share of waste electrical and electronic equipment (WEEE). You can meet these responsibilities in 2 ways:

  • join a Producer Compliance Scheme
  • set up your own scheme

Keep records

You must keep records showing how much electrical and electronic equipment you produce and sell within the EU. Keep these records for 4 years.

Manufacturing responsibilities

If you manufacture electrical or electronic equipment there are extra WEEE rules you must follow including:

  • labelling products - eg, use the crossed-out wheelie bin symbol
  • marking your products - eg, use your producer identification mark
  • producing products that are easy to repair and recycle

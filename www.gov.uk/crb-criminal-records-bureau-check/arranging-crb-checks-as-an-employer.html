Part 4: Arranging CRB checks as an employer

Employers should only arrange a Criminal Records Bureau (CRB) check on a successful job applicant. They can withdraw a job offer if the results show anything that would make the applicant unsuitable.

Employers who need to do fewer than 100 checks a year can use an umbrella body.

Employers in Scotland or Northern Ireland

Employers in Scotland can get a criminal records check from Disclosure Scotland, while employers in Northern Ireland should go through Access NI.

Overseas applicants

If the applicant is from overseas, the employer can ask their foreign embassy if it can get a similar check done in their country.

Export licensing of Man-Portable Air Defence Systems

The UK applies strict national strategic export controls administered by the Export Control Organisation on exporting shoulder-launched surface-to-air missiles.

Starting to Export

Overview

The Export Control Organisation (ECO) is responsible for legislating, assessing and issuing export licences for a wide variety of controlled goods. One particular licensable category of controlled goods are Man-Portable Air Defence Systems (MANPADs).

MANPADs are shoulder-launched surface-to-air missiles which are a specific category of small arms and light weapons. Since they are relatively cheap and easy to acquire MANPADs are recognised internationally as posing a unique terrorist and proliferation threat - especially to civil aviation, peace-keeping, crisis-management and anti-terrorist operations.

In view of the threats posed countries that have signed up to the Wassenaar Arrangement - including the UK - apply strict national controls on the export of MANPADs.

Export licensing of Man-Portable Air Defence Systems

The ECO licenses the export of MANPADs on the basis of various factors. These include:

  • the consolidated criteria which outline the eight conditions against which all export licences issued by the ECO are judged on a case-by-case basis - for more information, see our guide on assessment of export licence applications
  • related policy statements, including those on incorporation and components - see policy on incorporated goods
  • arms embargoes in place on specific countries - see current arms embargoes and other restrictions
  • the ‘Elements for Export Controls of MANPADs’ agreed by the Wassenaar Arrangement countries

The ECO, in association with the Ministry of Defence and the Foreign & Commonwealth Office, has produced further guidance on export licensing of MANPADs for officials and industry.

This includes:

  • definition of MANPADs
  • assessment of export licence applications for MANPADs
  • handling of licence applications for components and spare parts
  • export for display, exhibition or demonstration
  • proviso to be included with all F680 applications

You can download guidance on exporting MANPADs from the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills (BIS) website (PDF, 102K).

Applying for an export licence or F680

MANPADs are subject to stricter controls on the trafficking and brokering of military goods. They fall within Category B of the trade controls listed within the Export Control Order 2008. This categorisation includes goods for which there is legitimate trade, but which are identified because of heightened concerns. The controls for Category B goods are the same as those for Category A with some exemptions.

This guide should be read in conjunction with our guides on MOD F680 applications. Further information is also available about types of export licences and strategic exports: when to request an export licence.

You can apply for an export licence using SPIRE.

The Wassenaar Arrangement

The Wassenaar Arrangement is a group of countries that includes the UK and collectively maintains agreed control lists of restricted goods comprising both military and dual-use items. The Wassenaar lists form the basis for the UK’s national export control lists - in particular, the UK Military List.

For more information about the Wassenaar Arrangement you can read about international non-proliferation and arms control regimes.

Benefits & credits: War Disablement Pension

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you have been injured or disabled as a result of service in HM Armed Forces before 6 April 2005, you may be able to claim War Disablement Pension.

Part 2: What you'll get

The amount you’re paid depends on the degree of your disability.

You’ll need to complete a claim form before the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency checks your service records. The degree of your disability will then be decided by Veterans Agency medical advisers.

Your assessment is worked out as a percentage.

If the percentage is one of the following:

  • more than 20% - you’ll get a pension
  • less than 20% - you’ll usually get a lump sum (or gratuity)

The gratuity amount depends on the extent of your disablement and how long you’re likely to be disabled. If your condition is hearing loss assessed at less than 20%, you won’t get a gratuity.

In addition to the basic War Disablement Pension (WDP) an additional allowance may be payable dependent on your age. Other allowances can also be claimed for care or mobility or if you are permanently unable to carry on with your regular occupation.

Whether you are entitled to an additional allowance depends on the rate of WDP you have been awarded and other criteria, such as your level of care or mobility needs, or whether you have lost earnings.

For a full list of rates download the leaflet ‘Rates of War Pensions and allowances 2011-2012’ from the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency website.

Download ‘Rates of War Pensions and allowances 2011-2012’ (PDF, 249KB)

How War Disablement Pension is paid

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg your bank account.

Part 3: Eligibility

You may be able to get War Disablement Pension if you were injured or disabled through serving in HM Armed Forces, including the:

  • Ulster Defence Regiment (now known as the Royal Irish Regiment)
  • Home Guard
  • nursing and auxiliary services

You can claim if you were injured or disabled because of conditions during a war or from being a prisoner of war, while serving as:

  • a civil defence volunteer (CDV)
  • part of the Merchant Navy (MN)
  • naval auxiliary
  • coastguard

You can also claim if during the Second World War, you were:

  • a civilian injured or disabled as a result of enemy action
  • a member of the Polish Forces under British Command (or you were in the Polish Resettlement Forces) and were injured or disabled in service

Part 5: Further information

Effect on other benefits

The amount of War Disablement Pension you’re entitled to can be affected if you’re receiving one of the following benefits:

  • Employment and Support Allowance
  • Incapacity Benefit
  • Disability Living Allowance
  • Income Support

For more information about which benefits may be affected contact your local Jobcentre Plus office.

How to appeal

If you disagree with a decision about your claim you can appeal to the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency.

Before you appeal it’s a good idea to:

  • ask the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency for more information about how the decision was reached
  • ask for the decision to be reconsidered if there are some facts the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency may not have known when they made their decision

If you’re still unhappy with the outcome contact the Service Personnel and Veterans Agency to let them know that you want to appeal.

Free help with appeals

You can get free help with an appeal from the:

  • Royal British Legion
  • Royal British Legion Scotland
  • Royal Air Forces Association

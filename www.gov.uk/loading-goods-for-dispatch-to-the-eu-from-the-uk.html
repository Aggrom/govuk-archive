Starting to export

Introduction

This guide is aimed at those responsible for receiving, presenting and loading goods for export from the UK.

The information in this guide is to assist you in submitting accurate arrival and departure messages. It tells you about your part in the export chain and how your timely actions to complete export procedures help assure the business and allow UK exporters to zero rate their goods for VAT, obtain Common Agriculture Policy (CAP) refunds and complete other export formalities.

Actions points loaders should be aware of and complete

HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) have set out basic information that they feel should be provided by exporters and freight agents to enable loaders to accurately record goods presented for export at UK ports and airports. For more information, see our guide on freight forwarding - managing risk.

HMRC have reminded exporters to supply accurate and timely information to their freight agents and, consequently, those paying agents to correctly advise port loaders.

Following the declaration being made, HMRC recommend you are informed (by the agent) of the official Customs reference for shipping goods. References such as the actual Declaration Unique Consignment Number (DUCR) used or official entry reference number would create a good auditable record. What Customs need to be able to do is trace shipments through a trader’s books and also Customs Handling of Import Export Freight (CHIEF) records. HMRC believe accurate CHIEF messaging and use of official references are key to achieving their ultimate goal.

Whilst exports is now a largely paperless system, HMRC have recommended which New Export System (NES) details agents ought supply to enhance the export system, especially when sending goods forward to Transit Sheds, Designated Export Places, Inland Clearance Depots and ports.

This will hopefully improve any transposition of the Master Unique Consignment Number (MUCR) or the DUCR made by you, the NES approved loader. Many arrivals are notified through use of inventory-booking references so these are also worth confirming with your agents in order to successfully link them to the correct CHIEF record.

Loader actions to be completed

Most exports are delivered to inventory linked, electronic port systems or ports with approved loaders, who in turn have electronic links into HMRC central computer system, CHIEF.

Where you are responsible for inputting the electronic arrival message, this completes the act of legal presentation of the goods for export.

For those ports with no electronic links to CHIEF, any goods for export must be presented independently to Customs who will access CHIEF on the trader’s behalf.

A check-list to help Customs Approved Loaders avoid Customs Civil Penalties

Below is a check-list which will help Customs Approved Loaders in avoiding Customs Civil Penalties.

Electronic arrival messages at inventory or web-linked locations

For successful arrival messages at the port or airport you should supply:

  • a timely and accurate arrival message to CHIEF with the following details
  • the CHIEF reference (entry processing unit, entry number and date) or Unique Consignment Number - MUCR or DUCR
  • information on the location of goods
  • the correctly input DUCR reference number, inventory reference or MUCR - any mis-keying may result in Customs holds

Electronic departure messages at inventory or web-linked locations

If a departure message is required for the shipments, all of the following six items must be entered. These are:

  • DUCR/MUCR
  • location of Goods
  • name of vessel
  • the actual date of export
  • flag code of vessel
  • mode of transport

For arrivals and departures at manual locations

Where ports or export locations are not controlled through electronic means, goods for export must be presented to Customs.

Loaders should supply Customs with a completed C1601 form, along with a completed C130EX form.

Find form C1601 on the HMRC website.

Find form C130EX on the HMRC website.

Customs will perform the actions similar to the loader found at inventory ports or Customs approved web loaders. Customs input the details mentioned in the paragraphs above. Once cleared for shipment, the C130EX will allow goods to be loaded and shipped. Once shipped, the C130EX should be stamped by the loader and be presented again to Customs to complete the required departure message.

Goods must not be shipped, or be loaded to be shipped, without ‘permission to progress’ having been granted from Customs.

What a loader should do for an invalid reference number

If the reference number covering export goods is entered to CHIEF and it does not accept the details, a new entry should be considered so as to allow the export to take place.

Any duplication must be brought to the attention of HMRC as soon as possible.

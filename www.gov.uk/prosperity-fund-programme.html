Strengthening UK relationships in Asia, Latin America and Africa to support UK prosperity and security

Overview

The Foreign & Commonwealth Office launched the Prosperity Fund in April 2011 to tackle climate change, strengthen energy security and promote an open global economy in key emerging economies. In its first year, the fund supported 248 projects across a network of 14 countries and regions at a cost of Â£19.4 million.

The Fund is part of the Strategic Programme fund, which promotes action on global issues in areas of strategic importance to the UK. Current areas of focus include the Arab Partnership, commercial diplomacy, counter proliferation, counter terrorism, human rights, prosperity, bilateral programmes and the Overseas Territories.

Target countries

Countries with dedicated programmes are: Brazil, China, Colombia, India, Indonesia, Japan, Mexico, Russia, South Africa, South Korea and Turkey.

We also operate regional funds in the Gulf, Latin America and South East Asia to widen our global reach, and work with the International Energy Agency (IEA) and the Organisation for Economic Co-operation and Development (OECD) through our delegation in Paris.

2013/14 Programme

It is likely that funds will be available for 2013 to 14 and 2014 to 15, although the exact amount of money allocated to each region will not be known until nearer the time. As your first point of contact, please contact your nearest British Embassy or High Commission.

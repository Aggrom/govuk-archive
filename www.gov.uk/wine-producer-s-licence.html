Licence Wine producer's licence

It is not currently possible to apply online for this licence. Please contact your local authority.

Overview

If you plan to produce wine or made-wine for sale, you must first obtain the following from HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC):

  • a wine producer’s licence
  • approval of your premises and plant

Wine is defined as any drink produced by fermentation of fresh grapes or the juice (must) of fresh grapes.

Made-wine is any other drink containing alcohol - apart from beer or cider - that is made by fermentation rather than distillation or any other process (including drinks like mead and ‘alcopops’).

You must also have a licence to produce sparkling wine if it exceeds 5.5 per cent in strength, unless you intend to do so in an excise warehouse - a place approved by HMRC for the holding of goods on which excise duty is suspended.

How to apply

Download ‘Application for a licence to carry on an excise trade (form L5)’ (PDF, 49KB)

A separate licence is needed for each set of premises at which your wine or made-wine is for sale.

As part of your application, you must make entry of the premises and plant you intend to use. This involves creating a plan describing and showing the position of each room, place and vessel that you use in the production process.

Applications should be sent to the HMRC National Registration Unit.


National Registration Unit
Portcullis House
21 India Street
Glasgow
G2 4PZ

No fee is payable and the licence will last until you stop trading.

If you have any queries about obtaining a licence or approval, contact:

HMRC Excise Helpline
Telephone: 0845 010 9000.

If you are operating in an excise warehouse, you don’t need to make entry, but you will need authorisation from HMRC to deal in duty-suspended goods.

You must keep the licence on your premises and produce it on request for inspection by HMRC officers.

Fines and penalties

If you produce wine or made-wine without a licence or premises approval, you may be fined. The wine and all vessels, utensils and materials used in the production process may also be forfeited.

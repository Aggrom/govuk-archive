How the National Measurement System, for scientific and legal metrology, helps to keep trade, industry, academia and government competitive.

Introduction

The UK’s National Measurement System (NMS) is a group of measurement laboratories which deliver standards of measurement for use in trade, industry, academia and government. It is run by a directorate within the National Measurement Office (NMO).

The NMS is concerned with all aspects of scientific and legal metrology. The NMO itself has responsibility for policy and delivery of the Legal Metrology infrastructure, whereas scientific metrology is carried out by the other National Measurement Institutes (NMIs).

The NMS aims to:

  • ensure all products comply with national and international measurement standards
  • stimulate product development in the instrument sector
  • increase productivity through better process and quality control

This guide will explain the role of the NMS in business and how it helps your business to be competitive. It also points you towards further information on NMS funding for the development of science and technology.

The National Measurement System

The NMS is the UK’s national infrastructure of measurement laboratories, which delivers world-class measurement science and technology. It provides traceable and increasingly accurate standards of measurement for use in trade, industry, academia and government. It invests around £60 million every year in improving measurement in the UK.

The NMS helps businesses to make accurate and traceable measurements by:

  • maintaining the measurement infrastructure
  • representing the UK’s position internationally
  • enabling safe and fair competition
  • providing support for innovation

Measurement also plays an important role in:

  • consumer protection - known as legal metrology
  • forensic science
  • environmental controls
  • food safety regulation
  • technical standards which enable barrier-free trade
  • safe medical treatment
  • law and order
  • leisure

The NMS is made up of four NMIs which hold the primary measuring standards, reference materials and equipment used for measuring.

The National Measurement System and competitiveness

The UK is world class in measurement science, engineering and technology. To remain competitive, the UK needs to have high standards for consumer safety and be competitive in both European and international markets.

The NMS aims to standardise and improve certification, calibration, testing and inspection across many different sectors. Mutual recognition in Europe and internationally means that businesses do not have to carry out multiple assessments of their suppliers. This system increases cross-border trade and export opportunities.

The NMS encourages competitiveness in UK businesses by:

  • contributing to science, engineering and technology
  • contributing to health and safety - eg by creating standards for the safety of medical equipment
  • supporting manufacturing and industry - eg by allowing new processes and products to be tested with accurate measurements
  • decreasing manufacturing waste - this aims to reduce prices for the same high quality or product and therefore increase profit margins
  • opening trade to Europe and internationally by ensuring a level playing field
  • encouraging development of resources and workforce skills

Using the National Measurement System in business

The NMS plays an important role in business. By enabling your business to make accurate and traceable measurements, you can increase the efficiency of processes and products, lower your costs and remain competitive.

The NMS provides several benefits to businesses, including:

  • opening up markets for cross-border trade within Europe and internationally
  • increasing opportunities for export
  • improving trade protection
  • ensuring safe and fair competition
  • improving the effectiveness of regulations - eg creating stronger products, reducing regulatory delays and reducing the need for penalties

The quality of products and services is also improved through the NMS due to:

  • a better quality control
  • suppliers being more likely to meet your specifications
  • strong design and development of product, plant and process equipment
  • public confidence in your products and services - consumers can make price and quantity comparisons more easily

Better measurement systems can also mean greater efficiency in your business due to:

  • lower scrap rates
  • fewer rejects
  • less rework
  • more efficient production processes
  • less energy consumption

National Measurement Institutes

The NMS funds a broad range of projects, including:

  • research into measurements and the development of new measurement techniques, procedures and instrumentation
  • maintenance of primary national measurement - such as high-accuracy non-routine calibration and testing services
  • supporting traceability in measurement through a pyramid of accreditation
  • supporting international activities - eg the management of international technical intercomparisons

These activities are mostly undertaken by the four NMIs, which are:

  • The NMO, an executive agency of the Department for Business, Innovation & Skills. It makes sure that all trade measurements are accurate, legal, and fair to buyer and seller.
  • LGC Ltd (the former Laboratory of the Government Chemist), an independent provider of analytical and diagnostic services. They offer chemical, biochemical and forensic analysis, genetic testing and screening, research, method validation, consultancy and analytical outsourcing. Find information on LGC Ltd research and scientific analysis on the LGC website.
  • The National Physical Laboratory (NPL), the UK’s national standards laboratory, an internationally respected and independent centre of excellence for measurement research and development. Read about the NPL’s research programmes on the NPL website.
  • TUV NEL Ltd, which provides technical services to industries including oil and gas, power, water, petrochemical, pharmaceutical, industrial, automotive and defence. It is the UK’s centre of excellence for flow measurement. Find out about the work and services of TUV NEL Ltd on the TUV NEL website.

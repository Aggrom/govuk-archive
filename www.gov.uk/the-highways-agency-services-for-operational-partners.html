The Highways Agency: services for operational partners

Online services and resources for statutory bodies, contractors, organisations and facilities who work with the Highways Agency.

Introduction

The Highways Agency provides a number of online services and resources for its operational partners. These range from procurement guidance and documentation to technical standards and traffic equipment data logging services.

Operational partners are those who work directly or indirectly on behalf of the Highways Agency. They can be categorised as:

  • statutory bodies - eg local transport authorities, and emergency and police services
  • Highways Agency contractors - such as the National Traffic Operations Centre, maintenance contractors and research and development contractors
  • organisations involved in managing traffic on England’s strategic road network - including airports, bridges, toll roads, bus stations and ports
  • facilities that generate large amounts of road traffic - such as sports and entertainment venues, and shopping centres

This guide outlines some of the main Highways Agency services for operational partners and points you towards more detailed information for each.

Procurement documentation and guidance

The Highways Agency procures goods and services from many different suppliers as part of its responsibility to maintain the strategic road network in England.

The agency buys goods and services in several main areas, such as:

  • consultancy
  • highways improvement
  • highways maintenance
  • traffic technology
  • auditing
  • vehicles and uniforms - eg for the Traffic Officer Service

Most Highways Agency procurement is done through framework contracts or indirectly through managing agents. To find out more see procurement and the Highways Agency.

Accreditation for Highways Agency suppliers

If you are tendering for a Highways Agency contract you may need to hold specific accreditations - eg quality management schemes or National Highways Sector Schemes (NHSSs) for certain types of work. For some high-value contracts potential suppliers may need to successfully pass a pre-qualification process. This might include completing a Capability Assessment Toolkit, which scores your business’ capabilities. Find out how to tender for Highways Agency projects.

Highways Agency supplier skills requirements

All Highways Agency suppliers must employ fully qualified and registered operatives with the relevant NHSS registration skills cards. NHSS schemes are quality management programmes - developed within the ISO 9001:2000 framework - for use within the highways industry. For more information see National Highway Sector Schemes - certification for contractors and subcontractors.

Registration and skills cards are issued both by NHSS certification bodies and the Construction Skills Certification Scheme. See skills card and registration requirements for Highways Agency suppliers.

Standards for Highways documents and online resources

The Highways Agency runs the Standards for Highways online document resource for contractors. You can use this website to download documents on design, construction and maintenance standards, and other useful documents such as:

  • Design Manual for Roads and Bridges
  • Manual of Contract Documents for Highway Works
  • Interim Advice Notes
  • Network Management Manual
  • Routine and Winter Service Code

For information on how to access these documents, see our guide on Standards for Highways online resources.

Traffic systems, signs and equipment documentation

The Highways Agency manages two online libraries of information about traffic systems, signing and equipment:

  • The Traffic Systems and Signing Plans Registry - registered users of this service can search for and download unpriced documents such as specifications and technical drawings. See Traffic Systems and Signing Plans Registry: how to access documents.
  • MCS215 - an online database of traffic control equipment with statutory approvals, available to registered users. See MCS215 - the register of type approved and self-certified traffic control equipment.

HALOGEN - the Highways Agency’s data logging environment

HALOGEN is the central source for HATMS (Highways Agency Traffic Management Systems) logged data.

The database logs real-time and historic data from roadside equipment across the strategic road network. Data sets include matrix and variable message sign settings, emergency roadside telephone and equipment faults, and equipment locations.

The Highways Agency encourages maintenance contractors such as technical managing agent contractors (TechMACs) and enhanced managing agent contractors (EMACs) to use HALOGEN. For example, contractors can use the logged data to detect roadside equipment failures within their areas of operation.

You’ll need to register to use HALOGEN. For more information see HALOGEN - the Highways Agency’s online data logging system and reporting tools and system interfaces for HALOGEN data.

Traffic camera still image and video services

The Highways Agency licenses still and video images from its network of roadside traffic cameras to organisations with interests in traffic management, eg contractors, events organisers and broadcast media.

Operational partners of the Highways Agency - such as those involved in highways construction or equipment maintenance - can use traffic camera images and video to plan and carry out projects. The services can be used to find out about traffic flow and speed, as well as delays and roadworks.

Several types of still image and video streaming services are available to suit the needs of different users. Some - such as HANET and Edge Client - are free web-based services that can be used by maintenance contractors. The Media Still and Streaming services are mainly used by broadcast media organisations for traffic information purposes and require a licence from the Highways Agency.

You can find detailed information about the various Highways Agency traffic camera services on the Highways Agency website

To help you decide which service is best suited to your needs, the Highways Agency has developed a decision making tool. See how to apply for a licence to use traffic camera images or video.

PartnerNET - the Highways Agency extranet for operational partners

If you are an operational partner of the Highways Agency you can register with PartnerNET - the agency’s extranet for contractors. The PartnerNET website includes public and discreet areas in which you can access supporting information for your projects.

PartnerNET is aimed at businesses and suppliers who:

  • are involved in network maintenance, motorway and bridge construction projects
  • deliver major road projects
  • maintain traffic control systems

PartnerNET provides a wealth of knowledge and advice. It gives you access to toolkits, safety alerts and details of training courses delivered by the Traffic Learning Centre. The website also carries news and features of interest to the wider highways maintenance community.

The secure area of PartnerNET allows contractors working on particular projects to create online communities so that they can work collaboratively with other organisations.

Highways Agency toolkits

The three main toolkits on PartnerNET are:

  • Managing Down Costs toolkit - this aims to make supply chain partners aware of ideas for reducing costs that can be applied at any stage of scheme development. Read about the Managing Down Costs toolkit on the PartnerNET website
  • Journey Time Reliability toolkit - this gives ideas to help address ‘causes of delay’ that make journey times unreliable. [http://www.highways.gov.uk/specialist-information/highways-agency-toolkits/the-journey-time-reliability-toolkit/)
  • Health and Safety toolkit - a collection of good practice, innovations and ideas that are currently being used on the Highways Agency network. Read about the Health and Safety toolkit on the PartnerNET website

Collaborative working

PartnerNET enables businesses to work collaboratively through various online communities. These include the:

  • highways maintenance community
  • major projects community
  • managed motorway delivery community

Registered users of the maintenance community can:

  • join communities according to their particular areas of interest
  • work collaboratively with others
  • access the PartnerNET document repository

You can find out about the Highways Agency maintenance community.

New guidance, reports and presentations

To help partners stay up to date, latest guidance, reports and presentations are published in the ‘Features’ section of the PartnerNET website.

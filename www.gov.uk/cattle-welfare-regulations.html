Introduction

Cattle farmers have to meet all relevant farm animal welfare requirements that apply to cattle and calves.

This guide outlines the welfare requirements for keeping cattle. It also gives guidance on satisfying Cross Compliance requirements. The guide refers to the Department for Environment, Food and Rural Affairs (Defra) code of recommendation for the welfare of cattle and will take you to specific guidance in each level. For general information on the legislation you can read the Defra welfare code introduction on the Agricultural Document Library (ADLib) website.

Welfare codes don’t lay down statutory requirements but farmers are legally obliged to ensure that all staff attending their cattle are familiar with - and have access to - the welfare code. The codes may also be used to back up legislative requirements, eg where a person is accused of a welfare offence, failure to comply with the provisions of the welfare code may be relied on by the prosecution to establish guilt.

Cattle welfare and Cross Compliance

The Animal Welfare Act 2006

This Act contains the general laws relating to animal welfare. It is an offence under the Act to cause any unnecessary suffering to any animal. The Act also contains a duty of care to animals - this means that anyone responsible for an animal must take reasonable steps to make sure that an animal’s needs are met. This legislation is based on the ‘five freedoms’ of the Farm Animal Welfare Council (FAWC). The Act says that an animal’s welfare needs include:

  • a suitable environment (how it is housed)
  • a suitable diet (what it eats and drinks)
  • the ability to exhibit normal behaviour patterns
  • any need it has to be housed with, or apart from, other animals
  • protection from pain, suffering, injury and disease

The Welfare of Farmed Animals (England) Regulations 2007

These regulations are made under the Animal Welfare Act 2006 and provide additional protection to farmed animals. Schedule 8 of these regulations covers the additional welfare requirements for the keeping of pigs and implements EU Council Directive 91/630/EEC (as amended).

Cross Compliance requirements

To qualify for full payment under the Single Payment Scheme (SPS) and other direct payments - eg the Environmental Stewardship schemes - you must meet all relevant Cross Compliance requirements. These requirements are split into two types:

  • Statutory Management Requirements (SMRs)
  • Requirements to keep your land in Good Agricultural and Environmental Condition (GAEC)

To meet Cross Compliance welfare standards for your cattle, you need to follow:

  • SMR 18 - welfare of farmed animals
  • SMR 16 - welfare of calves
  • SMR 11 - food and feed law
  • SMR 10 - restrictions on the use of substances having hormonal or thyrostatic action and beta-agonists in farm animals

SMR 18

SMR 18 sets out the minimum welfare requirements for all farmed animals. Key requirements include thoroughly inspecting livestock as often as necessary to avoid suffering, and ensuring that they are looked after by staff who have the correct skills.

SMR 18 also sets out the minimum standards of accommodation, feeding and watering of livestock, as well as the maintenance of livestock equipment. It also outlines harmful practices which you must not use when dealing with your animals.

This guide includes information on good practice in meeting SMR 18 as it applies to cattle.

Download SMR 18 from the Rural Payments Agency (RPA) website (PDF, 864KB).

SMR 16

SMR 16 lays out specific minimum requirements for the welfare of calves. It covers standards for accommodation, feeding and watering. For more information, see the page in this guide on pregnancy, calving and calf-rearing.

Download SMR 16 (welfare of calves guidance) from the RPA website (PDF, 660KB).

SMR 11

SMR 11 helps ensure that feed for food-producing animals is safe - so that ultimately our food is safe. For more information see this guide on [food safety: farmed meat, farm produce and animal feed].(http://whitehall-admin.production.alphagov.co.uk/government/admin/specialist-guides/4063)

Download SMR 11 (food and feed law guidance) from the RPA website (PDF, 737KB).

SMR 10

The aim of SMR 10 is to prevent residues from beta-agonists and substances with hormonal or thyrostatic effects from entering the human or animal food chain. Download SMR 10 from the RPA website (PDF, 527KB).

Your farm can be selected for a Cross Compliance inspection to check you are meeting the required standards for the above SMRs. These inspections can be carried out by both RPA and/or the Animal Health and Veterinary Laboratories Agency (AHVLA) - as their areas of responsibility cover different SMRs.

For more information about Cross Compliance see this guide on Cross Compliance: the basics.

Inspections by AHVLA for animal welfare and disease control purposes

The inspector will expect:

  • to view all animals in their normal rearing environment
  • you to demonstrate the operation of your alarm systems and to ensure back-up generators etc are working
  • to be able to examine specific animals on request
  • to take, where appropriate, any samples/carcasses/photographs etc as evidence to support both compliance and non-compliance

In addition, the inspector will:

  • inspect veterinary medicine and mortality records
  • check any other records that may support compliance with the requirements
  • enquire whether there has been a known or suspected outbreak case of a notifiable disease (as listed under the disease control requirements) on your premises during the year

Stockmanship

The stock-keeper has the most significant influence on the welfare of cattle. Stock-keepers should be competent in a wide range of animal health and welfare skills, including:

  • handling skills
  • ear tagging
  • preventing and treating certain basic or common causes of lameness
  • preventing and treating internal and external parasites
  • administrating medicines
  • providing appropriate care to sick and injured cattle
  • castration
  • disbudding
  • removing supernumerary (extra) teats
  • milking

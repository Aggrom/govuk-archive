Benefits & credits: Residential Support Scheme

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If you need to study away from home because your course isn’t available locally, you may get help with the cost of term-time accommodation.

You may qualify if your course is too far away for you to travel daily to and you’re studying for a Level 2 or Level 3 qualification (equivalent to GCSEs and A levels).

How much you get depends on your household income and whether you live and study in London.

Part 2: What you'll get

Depending on your household income you can get the following amounts each year, for a maximum of 3 years. The actual amount you’ll get depends on how much your accommodation costs.

Gross household income Studying outside London Studying in London
Up to £21,000 Up to £3,458 Up to £4,079
£21,001 to £25,704 Up to £2,305 Up to £2,685
£25,705 to £30,993 Up to £1,152 Up to £1,355
£30,994 or more No award No award

Part 3: Eligibility

You may get help from the Residential Support Scheme if:

  • you’re 16 to 18
  • a similar course isn’t available locally
  • you’re not studying at a college offering a residential bursary - see further information
  • you’re ‘ordinarily resident’ in England (this means you’re settled in the UK and have lived there for at least 3 years before your course starts)

Your course must be one of the following:

  • more than 15 miles from your home
  • more than a 2 hour return journey away

Your course must also:

  • be for your first Level 2 or Level 3 qualification (equivalent to GCSEs or A levels)
  • need at least 16 hours attendance a week
  • last for at least 10 weeks
  • be known as ‘learner-responsive funded provision’ (ask your college’s student adviser)

Part 4: How to claim

Ask your school or college for a Residential Support Scheme application pack.

Part 5: Further information

Help

If you need help with the application form or if you’re not satisfied with how your application was handled call the Learner Support Helpline.

Learner Support helpline
0800 121 8989

Residential bursary

You may be able to get a residential bursary towards the cost of accommodation from some colleges instead of the Residential Support Scheme.

Most of these colleges are ‘Specialist Residential Centres’ for subjects like agriculture, art and design or horticulture.

To apply for help contact the college directly.

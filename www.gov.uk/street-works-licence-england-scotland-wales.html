Licence Street works licence (England, Scotland and Wales)

It is not currently possible to apply online for this licence. Please contact your local authority.

Overview

If you wish to place apparatus on a street or road in England and Wales, you must be licensed by the local street authority.

In Scotland, you must obtain permission in writing - this can include by electronic means.

Licences are also required if you wish to inspect, adjust, maintain, repair, renew, move, alter or remove such apparatus, or break up a street, or sewer, drain or tunnel under a street, for the purpose of doing so.

However, if you need to carry out such actions for emergency works (eg works necessary to avoid or prevent danger) you must instead give notice as soon as reasonably practicable, and at the latest within two hours of the works commencing.

If you wish to carry out street works involving breaking up a street, or a sewer, drain or tunnel under a street, you must give at least 7 working daysâ€™ notice and ensure that you complete the works as soon as reasonably practicable.

A fee may be payable for a licence, and you must comply with any conditions that are attached to the licence.

Conditions

You must comply with any directions by the authority in regard to timing of works and placing of apparatus, and comply with any codes of practice to ensure that you:

  • minimise inconvenience to people using the street
  • protect the structure of the street

You must comply with prescribed safety measures, including ensuring that:

  • any part of the street which is broken up or obstructed is adequately guarded and lit
  • traffic signs are placed and operated as necessary for the guidance or direction of people using the street

Works must be supervised by a suitably qualified supervisor and there must always be a qualified trained operative on site.

You must record the location of all apparatus placed or moved in the street, stating the nature of the apparatus and whether it is currently in use. The apparatus must be satisfactorily maintained for the safety and convenience of people using the street and the structure of the street.

If you are carrying out works in a street which is in the vicinity of a railway, tramway, dock, harbour, pier or canal, you must comply with requirements by the transport authority as to the displaying of lights to avoid any risk of their being mistaken for, or a hindrance to, a signal light.

If you wish to carry out works affecting a public sewer you must consult the sewer authority (or in Scotland, Scottish Water) before giving notice.

Where your apparatus is to be affected by other major works you must consult with the local authority and take any necessary measures regarding the apparatus in order to secure efficient implementation of the works in question.

If your works are likely to affect another personâ€™s apparatus you must comply with any reasonable requirement to allow them to protect or access their apparatus.

You must reinstate the street as soon as practicable after the completion of your street works, and you must give notice to the local authority within ten working days of the reinstatement. In Scotland, this notice must be given by the working day following the completion.

If the reinstatement is interim, you must complete permanent reinstatement as soon as possible, and within six months from the completion of the interim reinstatement.

Permanent reinstatement must include reinstatement of features designed to assist people with a disability, and must comply with any requirements on materials to be used, standards of workmanship and performance standards.

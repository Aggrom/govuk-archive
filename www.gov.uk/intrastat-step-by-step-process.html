Intrastat: step-by-step guide

The process of submitting your declarations explained step-by-step, to help you become Intrastat compliant.

Introduction

Intrastat is the name given to the method of collecting information and producing statistics on the movement of goods, not services, between member states of the EU.

The data you send to HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC) is used to produce European trade statistics, which is an essential part of the UK balance of payments. It is therefore important that the information you submit is timely and accurate.

For the majority of businesses that trade with other EU countries, compliance with the Intrastat system will involve nothing more than completing box 8 and/or 9 on their VAT return. However, if you trade in goods within the EU that exceeds the annual Intrastat thresholds, you are legally required to submit monthly Intrastat Supplementary Declarations (SDs). The 2012 thresholds are £600,000 for Arrivals and £250,000 for Dispatches.

Intrastat step 1

You will need to submit Intrastat SDs for the arrivals or dispatches of goods within the EU, or both, if you’ve exceeded their annual threshold. The 2012 thresholds are £600,000 for Arrivals and £250,000 for Dispatches.

I’ve exceeded the threshold - what do I do now?

To prepare for this the following procedure is recommended:

First, from your trade with the EU you need to collect together all:

  • sales or purchase ledgers
  • invoices
  • any other commercial documentation relating to the sale/purchase of goods for the month of return

Then, check your invoices to see that they include the following information:

  • commodity code - this is an eight digit description of the goods
  • value of the goods
  • quantity - number of items
  • net mass - this is the weight of the goods including immediate packaging
  • country

You now have the information required to fill in Intrastat SDs.

Intrastat step 2

This step outlines basic information on completing each box on the Intrastat SD which you must complete monthly if your trade in goods within the EU exceeds the Intrastat threshold, whether for Arrivals or Dispatches, or both. It provides details of where you can go to get more information and advice.

The sales of goods to other EU member states are known as ‘dispatches’ and the purchases of goods from them are known as ‘arrivals’. There are separate electronic forms for arrivals and dispatches. The quickest and easiest way to submit your SDs is via the internet. See the guide on Intrastat: technology and technicalities.

SDs must reach HMRC by the 21st day of the month following the reference period to which they relate, eg January data to reach HMRC no later than the 21st of February. Submitting Intrastat data is a legal requirement and HMRC operates a penalty regime for failure to provide timely and accurate SDs.

If you have repeated similar consignments where the commodity codes, delivery terms, nature of transaction codes, and goods to/from are the same, then it would be less time consuming for you to aggregate this data onto one line by adding the values and net mass together.

Find further information in the section on Intrastat returns.

Intrastat step 3

One of the easiest ways of submitting your SD data to HMRC is via the internet, for which a username and password is required. Find out about registering online in step 6.

The application can be completed in one of two ways:

  • online form
  • offline option

The online form

This is an ideal option for submitting small amounts of data using 20 line Arrival and Dispatch forms.

Offline option

This is the preferred option for submitting larger amounts of data and uses a pre-prepared Excel spreadsheet.

Access the interactive internet application demonstration on the uktradeinfo website.

Intrastat step 4

If your EU trade is above the Intrastat threshold, whether for arrivals or dispatches, or both, you must:

  • submit timely and accurate Intrastat SDs
  • keep a copy of every SD you make or which is made on your behalf
  • keep copies of all papers and documents which have been used to compile SDs
  • keep records for six years - these can include computerised records in a legible format in line with VAT requirements
  • produce any of the above records to an officer of HMRC when asked
  • permit the officer to make copies, take extracts or remove them for a reasonable period
  • make records that are compiled by computer bureau available to HMRC, normally at the principal place of business
  • allow HMRC officers to enter your premises or those of anyone compiling Intrastat SDs, at any reasonable time in order to carry out checks.

Who is legally responsible for completing the Intrastat SDs?

Generally speaking, the person or company responsible for making the Intrastat SD in the UK is the one who concluded the contract giving rise to the movement of goods into or out of the country.

However, where a business which concluded such a contract is unable to provide the data required because of the way the data is traded, the business which actually transfers the goods across the frontier will be responsible for the SD.

If the person or business which concluded the contract is not resident in the UK the responsibility for completing the Intrastat SD rests with the person or company which either arranged for the physical dispatch of goods from the UK, or physical possession of goods arriving in the UK (such as a warehousekeeper).

In nearly all cases the legal entity responsible is the VAT registered business that is able to zero rate the supply to a business in another EU member state (for Dispatches) or is liable to account for VAT on acquisition of the goods (for Arrivals). Where a fiscal agent is responsible for VAT declarations they are also responsible for the Intrastat SD, subject to this principal’s trade being above the threshold.

Failure to comply with the above legal requirements can result in penalty action.

Intrastat step 5

If you fail to submit your Intrastat SD by the due date or send incomplete or inaccurate data to HMRC you may be committing a criminal offence.

Penalty procedure

Penalties can be incurred where SDs are late, missing, inaccurate or incomplete. They are seen as a last resort but are imposed for persistent offenders. HMRC can provide help on completing Intrastat forms for businesses experiencing difficulties.

Although the penalty regime is a criminal one and could result in proceedings in a Magistrates Court, HMRC normally prefer to ‘compound’ any proceedings. This involves offering an administrative fine in lieu of any court proceedings. However, this is only offered when, after receiving a Notice of Intended Prosecution, a business has brought its Intrastat completely up to date. If any SDs remain outstanding then court proceedings will be instigated.

Please note - payment of a compound penalty does not remove your legal obligation to submit the Intrastat SDs for the periods covered by the penalty.

Intrastat online instructions

The advantages of submitting your Intrastat Supplementary Declaration online are that:

  • it is quick and easy
  • you can fill in and send data as often as you like, when you like
  • you can view your past declarations online
  • you get instant feedback if your declaration has a problem and you can amend it straight away

Online instructions

The application is free to use and requires registration to obtain a username and password. These are the screens you will see when you access the system.

User Login screen

On the User Login screen, in lower case enter your username and password, click the “Login” button.

Main Menu screen

The main menu screen features several options. Click on “Arrival” or “Dispatch” for the online forms.

Submission Errors screen

The Submission Errors screen highlights incorrect data. Print off the screen for a list of all errors, press the “Cancel” button. Correct the errors on the form then resubmit.

Submission Status

This is a receipt of transmission. It has a unique submission reference number. It is advisable to record this number, which can be useful if for any reason the data does not reach your account.

View the Selected File screen

Details of your file will appear; use the scroll bars at the bottom to view the whole file.

User Options screen

The User Options screen gives you the option of changing your password. Click on “Intrastat Online Services” for the main menu.

Online Arrivals form

This form is for arrivals, while the dispatch form is yellow. Enter your data.

To enter your data, move across the form using either the mouse or the tab key. Some data is validated during completion (a pop-up box appears giving the correct alternative). During submission the Commodity Code, Net Mass and Supplementary Units are validated. Data errors will cause the Submission Error screen to appear. Error-free files will receive a Confirm Submission screen.

Confirm Submission screen

Where there are no errors a Confirm Submission screen displaying details of the submission will appear. To transmit the data click the “Confirm” button. To abort the transmission click the “Cancel” button.

Browse facility screen

Enter the period and number then click ‘View Submissions’. This will show the number of files submitted for that period. If more than one file has been submitted select the appropriate file in the Available Submission box. Click ‘View Arrival/Dispatches’.

Intrastat offline instructions

The application is free to use and requires registration to obtain a username and password. These are the screens you will see when you access the system.

The offline option involves submitting a CSV file. This is a standard text file where the fields are separated by a comma. A pre-prepared Excel spreadsheet or a technical manual with the CSV file format may be available from uktradeinfo.

User Login screen

On the User Login screen, in lower case enter your username and password, click the ,Login, button.

Main Menu screen

The Main Menu screen features several options. Click on ‘CSV Generator Tool’ for the pre-prepared Excel spreadsheet and ‘CSV File Upload’ to submit a CSV file.

Pre-prepared Excel spreadsheet

The Excel workbook consists of five worksheets - an introduction showing the format required for trader and agent, separate flowcharts for trader and agent, and separate pre-prepared Excel spreadsheets with examples for trader and agent. Save the file on your PC for future use.

When creating a CSV file replace the dummy data with your own, save the file as CSV format (a ‘Save As’ option in Excel). The file is uploaded through the CSV File Upload screen on the main menu.

Submission Error screen

The Submission Error screen highlights incorrect data. Print off the screen for a list of all errors, press the Cancel button then return to the CSV file to amend. Correct the errors and resubmit.

Submission Status

This is a receipt of transmission - it has a unique submission reference number. Record this number as it can be useful if for any reason the data does not reach your account.

View the Selected File screen

Details of your file will appear ten at a time - use the scroll bars at the bottom to view the whole file.

User Options screen

The User Options screen gives you the option of changing your password. Click on ‘Intrastat Online Services’ for the main menu.

CSV Generator screen

To create a CSV file click on ‘Download CSV Generator’. The file consists of help files and a pre-prepared worksheet. Save the file on your computer for future use.

CSV Upload File screen

To send the CSV file, click on ‘CSV File Upload’ on the main menu then enter your file name or browse for your file and press enter. During submission your file with validated errors will result in an error submission screen or an error free file will receive a confirm submission screen.

Confirm Submission screen

Where there are no errors, a confirm screen displaying details of the submission will appear. To transmit the data click the confirm button, to abort the transmission click the ‘cancel’ button.

Browse facility screen

Enter the period number and press ‘view submissions’. This will show the number of files submitted for that period. If more than one file has been submitted select the appropriate file in the ‘available submission’ box. Click ‘view Arrival/Dispatches’.

Accessibility policies

This page includes the the accessibility policy for GOV.UK and the organisation accessibility policy for the Government Digital Service.

GOV.UK Web Accessibility Policy October 2012

Government Digital Service (GDS) is committed to providing websites that are usable and accessible to everyone. GDS actively works to ensure that its websites are practical, helpful and available to people of all abilities.

GDS aims to conform to British Standard (BS) 8878 Web Accessibility Code of Practice from the British Standards Institution (BSI). In accordance with BS8878, this policy documents the accessibility steps that were taken throughout the lifecycle of the GOV.UK website.

Purpose of the website

GDS is developing a website that will bring together information, tools and resources relating to government services in a single place. The purpose of the website is to provide a way for UK citizens to find the information they need and complete the transactions they require.

User research and requirements

Target audience

The target audience of the website is any adult who wants information, tools or resources relating to government services in the UK.

Audience requirements

The proportion of older and disabled people using the website will be typical of the general population. General requirements for older and disabled people can be based on the following:

  • People who are able to experience the website without assistance or accommodation;
  • People who need to change the accessibility settings of their operating system or browser in order to experience the website;
  • People who require assistance or use an assistive technology in order to experience the website.

Audience preferences

Older and disabled people will use a range of devices, operating systems, browsers and assistive technologies to access the website. This may include both proprietary and open source technologies.

Audience relationship

The website will be open.

It will be possible to filter information based on location. People may enter a postcode to receive information relevant to their local area.

Audience goals

The following user goals have been identified for the GOV.UK website. Visitors to the site should be able to:

  • Search and find government information and services;
  • Browse by category and find government information and services;
  • Set their location and be shown services and information relevant to their location;
  • Find answers to complex questions using a simple decision tree style interface;
  • Find clear consistent start pages for a range of online government services;
  • Submit feedback on their experience of using the website;
  • Browse help pages to help them use the site;
  • Easily read content in a variety of page layouts.

Strategic choices

Audience experience

BS8878 defines three experience levels: technically accessible, useably accessible, and enjoyably accessible. The GOV.UK website will aim to deliver an experience that is enjoyably accessible by everyone.

Accessibility approach

The GOV.UK website will take an inclusive design approach. Regular testing will take place throughout the lifetime of the site. Testing will be a combination of technical assessment against recognised guidelines and standards, and evaluation with people representative of the website’s diverse audience.

The inclusive design approach will be supplemented by a degree of product adaptation (personalisation).

Supported platforms

The GOV.UK website will be based on best practice guidelines and standards. It is anticipated that this approach will facilitate accessibility on alternate platforms such as mobile or IPTV. No specific design, development or testing on alternate platforms is currently planned.

Supported technologies

The GOV.UK website will be supported by Internet Explorer 7+, as well as recent versions of Chrome, Opera, Firefox and Safari.

In addition the GOV.UK website should be supported by recent versions of Jaws, NVDA, VoiceOver, Window Eyes and Supernova screen readers, ZoomText, Magic, Supernova and basic operating system screen magnifiers, as well as speech recognition software including Dragon Naturally Speaking, and native operating system speech packages.

The beta GOV.UK website should be usable without a mouse, and with a range of alternative hardware devices such as sip/puff technology, eye tracking or analogue devices including head/mouth wands.

Production choices

Web procurement

The website and the tools used to manage it will be created by the GDS team. The only third party tools will be systems monitoring applications such as Nagios. Where the output from these tools is required by the editorial team, it will be presented in a custom accessible interface.

Web technologies

The website and the tools used to manage it will be built using the following technologies:

  • Ruby On Rails;
  • HTML 5;
  • CSS 2/3;
  • ARIA 1.0;
  • jQuery 1.7.x;
  • jQuery UI 1.8.x.

Web guidelines

The GOV.UK site will aim to meet Level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0, and conform to the relevant W3C specifications wherever possible.

Evaluation choices

Accessibility testing

As each new feature of the GOV.UK website is added or refined, it will be informally assessed against the target accessibility guidelines.

At regular points throughout the lifecycle of the GOV.UK website, it will be formally assessed against the target accessibility guidelines. It will also be formally evaluated with older, disabled and able people representative of the website’s diverse audience.

Communication choices

Accessibility statement

The GOV.UK website will not include an accessibility statement. The GDS team is not comfortable with a statement that draws a distinction between accessibility and any other aspect of best practice development.

Sustainability choices

Accessibility maintenance

The website has been developed from a public alpha. It is anticipated that the website will continue to evolve and improve in response to feedback and further testing. Therefore the steps and decisions outlined in this document will be continually applied and refined.

Government Digital Service Organisational Web Accessibility Policy October 2012

Government Digital Service (GDS) is committed to providing websites that are usable and accessible to everyone. GDS actively works to ensure that its websites are practical, helpful and available to people of all abilities.

GDS aims to conform to British Standard (BS) 8878 Web Accessibility Code of Practice from the British Standards Institution (BSI). In accordance with BS8878, this policy documents the organisational accessibility goals that GDS aims to achieve.

Policy responsibility

Joshua Marshall (joshua.marshall@digital.cabinet-office.gov.uk) is responsible for this policy and its implementation within GDS. Joshua will work with other people within GDS to ensure that the principles of this policy are upheld.

Each person within GDS is also responsible for upholding the principles of this policy within their particular areas of operation. Effectively the principles of this policy form “business as usual” within GDS.

Policy intention

The intention of this policy is to identify clear accessibility principles that all GDS websites should follow. For the purpose of this policy, the term “websites” includes:

  • External facing websites, microsites, and online applications;
  • Internal facing websites and online applications.

Policy principles

The principles of this policy are:

  • Engage with disabled people, understand their requirements, and incorporate those requirements into website scoping and planning;
  • Meet Level AA of the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0 as a minimum, and Level AAA wherever possible;
  • Conduct formal and informal accessibility evaluations against the Web Content Accessibility Guidelines 2.0 throughout development;
  • Put in place an ongoing post launch programme of accessibility evaluations and testing with people with disabilities.

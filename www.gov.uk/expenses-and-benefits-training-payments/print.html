Expenses and benefits: training payments, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

As an employer providing training expenses for your employees, you have certain tax, National Insurance and reporting obligations.

What’s included

  • the cost of training
  • related costs, eg books or travel expenses while training

Part 2: What's exempt

You don’t have to report anything to HMRC or pay tax and National Insurance if either of the following apply:

  • the employee is in work-related training
  • you arrange and pay for non-work-related training and the employee earns at a rate of less than £8,500 a year

Part 3: What to report and pay

If training expenses aren’t exempt, you may have to report them to HMRC and deduct and pay tax and National Insurance on them.

What you need to report and pay depends on who arranges and pays for the training.

The amount to report and pay is the cost of the training plus any related costs like books or travel expenses.

If you arrange and pay for an employee’s non-work-related training

For employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year, or directors:

  • report on form P11D, section M
  • pay Class 1A National Insurance on the cost to you

If you pay for non-work-related training arranged by the employee

Who gets the benefit What to do What to pay
Employees earning at a rate of less than £8,500 a year Report on form P9D, section A(2) Class 1 National Insurance through payroll
Employees earning at a rate of £8,500 or more a year Report on form P11D, section B Class 1 National Insurance through payroll
Directors Report on form P11D, section B Class 1 National Insurance through payroll

If you reimburse an employee who has arranged and paid for non-work-related training

The amount you reimburse them counts as earnings. You’ll need to:

  • add the amount to the employee’s other earnings
  • deduct and pay PAYE tax and Class 1 National Insurance through payroll

Quick answer Tell Us Once

Not what you're looking for? ↓

Tell Us Once lets you report a death to most of the government organisations you need to tell in one go - you can use it online, over the phone or face-to-face.

If you already have your Tell Us Once reference number, sign in to use the online Tell Us Once service.

How to register

  1. The registrar will tell you about Tell Us Once when you register the death.

  2. Find out whether Tell Us Once is available by looking up the register office in the area where the deceased lived.

  3. If you can and want to use Tell Us Once, tell the registrar.

You need to register the death before you can use Tell Us Once.

Use Tell Us Once online

The registrar will give you a Tell Us Once reference number - you’ll need this to sign in to Tell Us Once.

You’ll need the deceased person’s:

  • death certificate details
  • National Insurance number
  • date of birth
  • driving licence number
  • passport number
  • details of any benefits and services they were getting

You will also be asked about the deceased person’s next of kin, and the person or company dealing with their estate (their property, belongings and money). You will need:

  • their names, addresses and phone numbers
  • permission to provide their details

Use Tell Us Once by phone or face-to-face

If you don’t want to use Tell Us Once online, you can use it:

  • by phone - the registrar will give you the phone number
  • in person, by making an appointment through your local council

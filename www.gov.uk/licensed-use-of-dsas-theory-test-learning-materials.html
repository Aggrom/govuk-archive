Licensed use of DSA's theory test learning materials

The Driving Standards Agency (DSA) encourages the reuse of its official learning materials with a licence.

England, Scotland and Wales.

Introduction

DSA licenses the official DSA theory test revision banks and 16 simulated hazard perception test video clips for use by other publishers.

They can be published under a Crown copyright licence agreement (CCLA). This helps to make sure that learner drivers and riders have a choice of learning materials, as DSA publishes its own official learning materials.

Some Crown copyright materials are available via the open government licence (OGL) which allows you to reuse:

  • The Highway Code and traffic signs
  • the national driving or riding standards and the syllabus

For more information visit the UK government licensing framework website.

Copyright infringement

DSA actively encourages the licensed reuse of its material. However, it:

  • will protect it against any unlicensed use
  • constantly monitors the use of its material
  • pursues any case where material has been used without its permission
  • tries to remedy breaches amicably
  • will take any necessary legal action in order to protect Crown copyright

Infringements should be reported to DSA’s intellectual property department.

Email: crowncopyright@dsa.gsi.gov.uk

Print and electronic images and photography

DSA has an image library on Flickr.

This is free for use by press and news media so long as users acknowledge DSA Crown copyright.

Crown copyright licensees should contact DSA’s intellectual property team about reusing these images.

Email: crowncopyright@dsa.gsi.gov.uk

DSA does not allow the reuse of photographs and other images of identifiable individuals, particularly children and people from vulnerable groups, included in its printed publications or on its website. These images fall outside the scope of the OGL.

Competition Act 1998

DSA follows an open and transparent system of trading that aims to meet the terms of the Competition Act 1998.

Copyright licences

DSA licenses the official DSA theory test revision banks and 16 simulated hazard perception test video clips for use by other publishers by way of a CCLA.

Royalty based Crown copyright licence

This category of licence is required when an applicant wishes to reuse any DSA Crown copyright material within a product that is to be offered for sale on a commercial basis. This licence is valid for 3 years.

Royalty rates

The royalty rate payable is 13% of ‘net sales revenue’ for each electronic and paper-based product sold. ‘Net sales revenue’ means the income to the licensee after tax has been deducted.

This rate varies if the DSA copyright material used in the publication constitutes less than 50% of the entire product.

Certified royalty statements should be sent to DSA within 30 days of the end of each financial quarter.

DSA will issue an invoice within 30 days of receipt of the certified statement. Each payment of royalty will be due and payable within 30 days after the date of DSA’s invoice

Crown copyright licences available for education or research

The CCLA is available:

  • to educational establishments where pupils are not charged by the licensee for access to the material and there is no commercial gain involved
  • if you wish to use copyright data for a research project or coursework purposes

The student or researcher should deliver a copy of the final work to DSA within a reasonable time before any results are published, and must be able to confirm that the finished item / course work is not:

  • copied
  • sold
  • published electronically
  • passed off in any way as being available for sale

An acknowledgement regarding the use of Crown copyright material must be included in the product as follows: ‘Crown copyright material has been reproduced by permission of DSA which does not accept any responsibility for the accuracy of the reproduction’.

Crown copyright licences for translated material

Translation licences are available on application for anyone wishing to translate DSA Crown copyright material into languages other than English. Any type of product can be produced and royalties are payable on net sales revenue (see above under royalty rates).

Memorandum of Understanding (MoU)

A MoU is issued to other governments or governmental departments who use the official DSA theory test question banks. The MoU will generally be under the same terms and conditions as a CCLA.

Licensed materials

Theory test revision banks

All the theory test revision banks are available for reuse under the CCLA and can be purchased by licensees in electronic format.

The official theory test revision banks are:

  • car
  • motorcycle
  • large goods vehicle (LGV) and passenger carrying vehicle (PCV)
  • approved driving instructor (ADI) part 1

The individual revision banks (1 to 4 above) are available free in PDF format or to buy in Filemaker Pro on CD-Rom including GIF and high-resolution images. Price: £350 plus VAT per category. There are discounts available for more than one category:

  • 1 category - £350 plus VAT
  • 2 categories - £600 plus VAT
  • 3 categories - £800 plus VAT
  • 4 categories - £1,000 plus VAT

Licensees are typically given around 6 months notice of any changes, together with a publication embargo date if applicable. There can be exceptions to these arrangements, however all licensees are informed accordingly.

Hazard perception test simulated video clips

In addition to the theory test question banks, licensees can also purchase a maximum of 16 simulated hazard perception test video clips.

These licensed clips will not be used in the live test but are provided for licensees to include within their products.

A hazard perception test is based on measuring the speed of a candidate’s response to developing situations as a measure of their scanning techniques and anticipation skills. If a candidate frequently reviews a clip the validity of that measurement is dramatically reduced. Consequently, if candidates were given the opportunity to rehearse on live clips in learning products, when confronted with these clips within the test it would not be giving a true measure of the time the candidate would take to identify a developing hazard. Releasing live clips would therefore unacceptably compromise the objectives and validity of the test.

A licensee may purchase 8 or 16 clips, however in order to protect the integrity of the hazard perception part of the theory test only 10 clips can be used in a single product. Fees for the clips are as follows:

  • 8 clips - £150 plus VAT
  • 16 clips - £300 plus VAT

Paying royalty and licence fees

To pay by secure credit card telephone 0115 936 6123. You can use MasterCard, Visa, Delta and UK Electron cards only.

Failure to pay royalties can incur a 2% charge per year above the base lending rate. A nil statement should be submitted if no sales took place during that financial period.

Rules about product artwork

All product artwork, created under a CCLA must be approved by the Crown copyright manager before the publication stage.

It must contain a Crown copyright acknowledgment on the product in the following format:

‘Crown copyright material has been reproduced by permission of the Driving Standards Agency which does not accept any responsibility for the accuracy of the reproduction’

Please note the additional guidance in the list below, this list is not exhaustive or limited to the examples given. The product must:

  • in no way resemble a current DSA product
  • not display DSA’s logo
  • not in any way suggest that it is recommended, endorsed or approved by DSA
  • not mislead the public in any way as to its content

How theory test changes affect licensees

DSA has stopped publishing multiple-choice questions or answers used in theory tests.

The knowledge and understanding information now provided with your question bank explains all the things learner must consider in any given situation. It also offers an opportunity for you to add value to your product.

By understanding the context, they will be able to apply their knowledge and answer the question correctly. This will help to ensure that new drivers learn and understand the principles behind safe driving.

Presentation of the theory test questions and answers

On any page, the knowledge and understanding text (shown in blue) should appear with the question. The correct answer option must not be indicated with the question itself, it must be listed either:

  • at the end of the section
  • at the end of the book
  • elsewhere

The ‘knowledge and understanding’ text does not have to be printed in colour.

Clause 5.3 of your CCLA applies:

5.3. the licensee will use the licensed materials in accordance with the reasonable instructions of DSA and will observe any reasonable directions given by DSA as to the presentation and disposition of the licensed materials on products and packaging for products.

Electronic products

In mock tests or quizzes in electronic products, the correct answer option should not be indicated until after the user has indicated their choice. We recommend that the knowledge and understanding text should be used as feedback or a hint to the user.

Reuse of Public Sector Information

The Reuse of Public Sector Information Regulations 2005 (PSI) establishes a framework for making the reuse of information held by public bodies easier. DSA has already made a commitment to this principle and is accredited to the Information Fair Trader Scheme (IFTS) by the Office of Public Sector Information (OPSI).

The PSI Regulations are designed to help release the value held in public sector information and allow the reuse of these assets commercially.

Alternatively, write to:

Admiralty Arch

The Mall
London
SW1A 2WH

The meaning of reuse

The phrase ‘reuse’ simply means to use information in a way that was originally intended.

Along with the Freedom of Information Act 2000 (FOI), the PSI regulations provide the public with the right of access to information held by public authorities subject to certain legal exemptions.

Although these regulations give the right of access to publicly held information it does not give an automatic right to reuse this information.

When information is given in response to a request under legislation such as FOI it may only be reused with permission, otherwise this would breach copyright. These PSI regulations simply manage that reuse.

The PSI Regulations impose the following main obligations upon a public sector body:

  • to publish a list of its documents available for reuse - this list is called an ‘information asset register’ and could also be held within the publication scheme
  • to provide a clear statement on the arrangements for reuse of its information
  • to clearly explain any applicable charges for reuse and any licence terms and conditions
  • to process applications for reuse in a timely, open and transparent manner and through fair, consistent and non-discrimatory processes
  • to establish a quick and easily accessible complaints procedure regarding that reuse

Information Fair Trader Scheme

The government is committed to making information protected by Crown copyright available as widely as possible for reuse.

Crown copyright is vested in the Controller of Her Majesty’s Stationery Office (HMSO). It manages and regulates licensing for the reuse of all information produced by Crown bodies.

The controller has authorised government trading funds to license Crown copyright information that they produce on behalf of Her Majesty.

Under the HMSO Information Fair Trader Scheme, each chief executive commits to:

  • trade openly, transparently and fairly in Crown copyright information
  • ensure that any complaints are investigated thoroughly and equitably
  • DSA’s Chief Executive has confirmed DSA’s commitment to fair-trading.

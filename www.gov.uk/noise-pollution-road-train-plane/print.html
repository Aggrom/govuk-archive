Noise pollution from roads, trains or planes, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Vehicle noise limits

The table shows the maximum noise limits for on and off-road vehicles.

Vehicle type Noise limit (decibels - db(A))
Petrol vehicles on public road 74
Direct injection diesel vehicles on public road 75
Off-road vehicles 75
Off-road direct-injection diesel vehicles 76

It’s illegal to modify the exhaust system to make a vehicle noisier after it has been ‘type approved’ (checked it meets environmental and safety standards).

The police can also take action if your vehicle’s silencer doesn’t work or if you’re driving in a way that creates too much noise.

Part 2: Noise from roads

There’s no legal limit to road noise, although noise levels might be taken into account when new roads or houses and offices near roads are planned.

Planned new roads

Local highway authorities assess noise when planning a new road to see if it will be noisier at your property when the new road opens.

If noise from a new road exceeds certain limits at existing houses, you might be able to get a noise insulation grant for your home.

To apply, or if you’re worried about the noise from a planned road scheme, contact your highway authority – get their contact details from your local council.

Compulsory purchase and road noise

When a new road is built, it often involves the ‘compulsory purchase’ of land to make way for the road.

You might be able to get compensation if a compulsory purchase means you become affected by road noise - the following booklets explain the process.

Booklet 1 explains the overall process – read this first.

Booklet 5 explains noise reduction work you might be able to get.

Part 3: Railway noise

There are no legal limits to noise from existing railways.

If noise from a new railway affects your property you might be able to get a noise insulation grant. Ask your local council for more details.

If particular trains are causing you a problem, speak to the company running those trains.

Find information on train operators on the National Rail website or call Network Rail’s 24-hour helpline on 08457 114 141.

Part 4: Aircraft noise

Noise is regulated at all UK airports and there are different noise limits for day and night flights.

Other rules include:

  • air traffic should fly over the least-populated areas on take-off whenever practical

  • there should be restrictions on night flights (for example a partial ban on night flights or complete phase-out of the noisiest aircraft)

There are also grant schemes to install noise insulation in affected homes. Contact the airport that affects you to apply.

Download the Civil Aviation Authority (CAA) factsheet (PDF, 615KB) for more information on aircraft noise and emissions.

Complaining about aircraft noise

To complain about noise and low-flying aircraft:

  • get in touch with the airport
  • report it to the Civil Aviation Authority (CAA)

Military aircraft

Military aircraft are covered by different rules.

Complain about noise or low flying on the Ministry of Defence (MOD) website.

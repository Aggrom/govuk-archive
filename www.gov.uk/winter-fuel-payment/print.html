Benefits & credits: Winter Fuel Payment

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You could get between £100 and £300 to help pay your heating bills if you were born before 6 July 1951. The is known as a ‘Winter Fuel Payment’.

The money is paid between mid-November and December. It’s tax free and doesn’t have to be paid back.

You don’t have to re-apply for it if you got it last year and your circumstances haven’t changed.

Any money you get won’t affect your Cold Weather Payment or Warm Home Discount.

Part 2: What you'll get

How much you get depends on your circumstances. You usually get less if you live with other people who also qualify.

Circumstance Born before 6 July 1951 Aged 80 or over before 24 September 2012
You qualify £200 £300
You live with someone under 80 who also qualifies £100 £200
You live with someone 80 or over who also qualifies £100 £150
You qualify but live in a care home and don’t get certain benefits* £100 £150
  • Benefits: Pension Credit, income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance, income-related Employment and Support Allowance

You’ll get a letter telling you how much you’ll get and an estimated payment date.

How you’re paid

Payments are paid between mid-November and December.

All benefits, pensions and allowances are paid into an account, eg a bank account.

Part 3: Eligibility

Usually, you’ll qualify for Winter Fuel Payment if:

  • you were born on or before 5 July 1951
  • you normally live in UK between 17 and 23 September 2012

You still get the payment if you qualify but move to another European Economic Area (EEA) country or Switzerland.

Exceptions

You don’t qualify if during 17 to 23 September 2012 you:

  • were in prison
  • were in hospital getting free treatment for more than 52 weeks
  • needed permission to enter the UK and didn’t qualify for help from the Department for Work and Pensions
  • lived in a care home, independent hospital or a Polish Resettlement Home (for the previous 12 weeks or more) and got Pension Credit, income-based Jobseeker’s Allowance or income-related Employment and Support Allowance

Change of circumstance

Report any change of circumstance as these can affect how much you get. For example, if the qualifying person moves out, dies or goes into care.

Contact the office paying your fuel payment (their details are on any letters they sent you). If you’ve not received a payment before, contact the helpline.

Part 4: How to claim

You only need to claim once - if you claimed last year you should get it automatically as long as your circumstances haven’t change.

To apply, send a claim form to the Department for Work and Pensions by 31 March 2013.

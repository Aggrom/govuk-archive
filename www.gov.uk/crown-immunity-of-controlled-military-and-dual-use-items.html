Crown immunity of controlled military and dual-use Items

Exemptions and export waivers for goods owned by the Crown that are otherwise controlled military or dual-use goods.

Overview

Crown (or sovereign) immunity is an exemption from criminal or civil prosecution where it is judged in law that a state or sovereign cannot commit a legal wrong.

Exemptions or export waivers for otherwise controlled military or dual-use goods are applicable where goods are owned by the Crown.

This guidance provides a brief overview about crown immunity exemptions for Crown-owned military or dual-use goods. It also covers information about licensing requirements for goods not covered by crown immunity but which are being exported or transferred to UK forces, diplomatic missions, embassies or consular posts overseas or for use in UK government defence contracts.

Crown immunity exemptions

Th UK Crown is not bound by the provisions of the Export Control Act 2002 for goods specified by Schedule 2 of the Export Control Order 2008 (ie the UK Military List which forms part of the UK Strategic Export Control Lists).

Official letter granting crown immunity

Where the Crown owns the controlled military goods, technology or software being exported, or has similar rights over disposal, then an exporter may carry out the export without a licence. This ’crown immunity’ exemption is subject to the issuing of an appropriate letter from Arms Control and Counter Proliferation (ACP) in the Ministry of Defence (MOD).

When crown immunity does not apply

If crown immunity is not available (ie if the goods are owned privately), then a licence will be required if the goods are subject to control by the Export Control Organisation. Goods are generally controlled either because they are listed on the UK Strategic Export Control Lists or as a result of End-Use Controls. For more information see the guide on strategic exports: when to request an export licence. 

You can apply for an export licence using SPIRE.

Open General Licensing options for less restricted controlled exports

It is important to highlight that the ECO publishes a number of more flexible Open General Export Licences (OGELs) which are designed to licence exports that are still controlled but designated of less risk. This includes specifically drafted OGELs relevant to export to UK forces or overseas consular posts and embassies.

If crown immunity is not applicable to your goods, but you can meet the terms and conditions specified then you might be able to export under authority of these licences. These include:

  • Open General Export Licence (Military and Dual-Use Goods: UK forces deployed in embargoed destinations) which permits subject to conditions, the export of transfer of most military goods, including software and technology to UK armed forces or MOD notified civilian contigents operating in certain embargoed destinations and to warships, naval auxiliaries or Government
  • Open General Export Licence (Military and Dual-Use Goods: UK forces deployed in non-embargoed destinations) provides similar coverage but a wider list of destinations that are not embargoed
  • Open General Export Licence (Exports of non-lethal military and Dual-Use Goods: To Diplomatic Missions or Consular Posts) covers the export of a limited range of non-lethal military and Dual-Use Goods, subject to certain conditions and restrictions, to any UK diplomatic mission or consular post overseas where it is intended for the exclusive use of UK government personnel
  • Open General Export Licence (Exports or transfers in support of UK Government Defence Contracts) permits, subject to conditions, the export of military goods, provision of technical assistance, and transfer of software and technology to a range of destinations if the export or transfer is in support of UK government defence contracts. You should note that this licence requires prior MOD F680 approval. For more information see the guide on MOD F680 applications

For more information and to download a copy of these licences, you can access the guide on Military Goods Open General Export Licences. 

Before exporting under authority of an OGEL, you need to ensure you can meet all the designated terms and conditions (including that you are entitled to export to a permitted destination and that the listed goods are specifically permitted by the licence). 
 
To help you in determining if an OGEL is appropriate for your export control needs, you can use OGEL Checker, a database which enables you to enter both the ’rating’ code of a specific controlled item and a destination country in order to determine a potential list of available OGELs. You can then work through the question responses to get an indication of whether a particular OGEL will provide the appropriate licence coverage. If not all the relevant OGEL terms and conditions can be met then the exporter will need to apply for a Standard Individual Export Licence.
 
All OGEL holders also need to register before use via SPIRE. Apply for an export licence using SPIRE.

OGEL holders are subject to compliance audits conducted by the ECO. For more information see the guide on compliance and enforcement of export controls.

To be aware of any recent licence amendments you should subscribe to the ECO’s Notices to Exporters.

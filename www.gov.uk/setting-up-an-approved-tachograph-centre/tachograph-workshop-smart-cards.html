Part 4: Tachograph workshop smart cards

A tachograph workshop card is used to calibrate digital tachographs. To be eligible for a workshop card, you must:

  • be a nominated technician working at an Approved Tachograph Centre (ATC) with digital status
  • hold a digital training certificate

Tachograph workshop cards are issued free of charge. They must be renewed every year on 31 March.

The cards are issued to a nominated technician, not to a centre - this means that only the individual who has the card can use it.

Working with tachograph workshop cards

Workshop cards can hold:

  • details of 88 calibrations
  • a small amount of events and faults data

Cards should be used as a driver card during any road tests related to tachograph work. This means that calibration details, periodic inspection details and any subsequent road test can be recorded together and downloaded to give a complete record of each vehicleâ€™s activities.

A workshop card must not be used instead of a company card. If you have a card used by your calibration centre, this must only be used in accordance with the tachograph centreâ€™s duties and not as a substitute company card.

All insertions of a card are recorded on the vehicle unit and the card.

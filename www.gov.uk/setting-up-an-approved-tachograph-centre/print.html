Setting up an Approved Tachograph Centre, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: How to apply

If you want to set up an Approved Tachograph Centre (ATC), you will need to read the ATC manual and fill in form GV07.

Download ‘ATC manual’ (PDF, 2.1MB)

Download ‘Form GV207’ (PDF, 60KB)

Send your application to the address on the form.

You can also contact the Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA) for the form and manual.

VOSA
Telephone: 0300 123 9000
Monday to Friday, 7:30am to 6pm

Part 2: Costs and equipment

It costs:

  • £361 to register as an Approved Tachograph Centre (ATC)
  • £148 to renew your registration each year

You can register to work on analogue tachographs, digital tachographs, or both.

Equipment

The ATC manual tells you what equipment you’ll need to buy. It also has a list of approved suppliers.

Download ‘ATC manual’ (PDF, 2.1MB)

Part 3: Who can work for you

You can only employ ‘nominated technicians’ to carry out work at an Approved Tachograph Centre (ATC). They must be skilled technicians with experience working on tachographs.

If they need to road test vehicles, they must also have a driving licence for the relevant categories of vehicles they’re testing.

They’ll also need to hold a ‘certificate of competence’ for each class of tachograph (digital, analogue or both) that they want to work on.

They’ll get this only after they’ve successfully completed a Vehicle and Operator Services Agency (VOSA)-approved training course. You’ll find details in the ATC manual.

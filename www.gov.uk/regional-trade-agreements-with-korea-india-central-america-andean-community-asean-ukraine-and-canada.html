Introduction

Regional Trade Agreements (RTAs) are preferential trading arrangements between countries or groups of countries. They are designed to encourage open and competitive markets for businesses in the participating countries.

As a member of the EU, the UK participates in EU RTAs, which are negotiated by the European Commission, with other countries or trading groups.

This guide is one of two guides that explains the different kinds of RTA, and gives information about the RTAs which the UK participates in. For more information, please see the guide on the RTA with Chile, Mexico, South Africa, Europe, Gulf States, Mediterranean, Mercosur and the Caribbean.

Regional Trade Agreements and the World Trade Organization

The World Trade Organization (WTO) has rules under which groups of countries can agree to trade together on preferential terms. This is known as a Regional Trade Agreement (RTA). There are different types of RTA, including:

  • free trade areas - where preferential rates for goods or services are exchanged, but individual countries keep their own tariff rates
  • customs unions - where members have both preferential rates for goods or services, and the same external tariff rates
  • asymmetrical agreements - where preferences are not exactly the same on each side - for example, one country may be given more time to put certain preferences in place

There are almost 200 RTAs, which the WTO monitors to ensure that they comply with WTO rules.

EU-South Korea Free Trade Agreement signed and in force on a provisional basis as of 1 July 2011

The EU-South Korea FTA is set to come into force on a provisional basis on 1 July 2011. As South Korea is the EU’s eighth largest trading partner and the EU is the largest investor in Korea, this FTA should provide substantial benefits for both the EU and South Korean economies. For example, it is expected to lead to €1.6 billion of tariff reductions per year.

Unusually, this new reciprocal agreement provides only for origin/invoice declarations to be issued as proof of preferential origin - ie exporters in the EU and South Korea will not be able to issue EUR1 Movement Certificates as proof of origin.

This means that if you, as a UK exporter, wish to export goods to South Korea under preference in consignments with a value above €6,000, you must become an approved exporter. This is because you will be required to insert the approval number in the body of the origin/invoice declaration, which the South Korean importer will need to support any claim to preference.

If you are already approved to issue invoice declarations for goods you export under another of the EU’s preferential trade agreements, you can apply to have that approval extended to South Korea. To do so, quoting your current approval number, you should contact HM Revenue & Customs (HMRC).

EU-India Free Trade Agreement under negotiation

Negotiations for an FTA between the EU and India began in June 2007, but progress was slow because of the elections in India in May 2009. Now that these are over, negotiations are expected to proceed more rapidly.

The EU is India’s largest trading partner and the FTA should lead to more economic integration and improved market access for EU and Indian companies, with benefits to consumers.

The agreement under negotiation is broad-based, covering:

  • technical barriers to trade
  • competition
  • customs
  • sanitary and phytosanitary regulations
  • non-agricultural market access
  • intellectual property rights
  • government procurement
  • investment
  • service

EU-Central America Free Trade Agreement under negotiation

At the EU-Latin America Caribbean Ministerial Summit in 2004, the EU agreed to begin negotiations for an FTA between the EU and Central American countries (Costa Rica, El Salvador, Guatemala, Honduras, Nicaragua and Panama). The FTA negotiations began in June 2007 and are well advanced.

Historically, the vast majority of Central America’s trade is with the USA and South America. Recently the region has been trying to widen its exports into Europe, which are dominated by agricultural products.

Economic integration between the countries of the Central America region is important in attracting investment. It will also help local businesses to develop strength in their regional markets, so that they can compete internationally.

The agreement under negotiation is broad-based, covering:

  • technical barriers to trade
  • competition
  • customs
  • sanitary and phytosanitary regulations
  • non-agricultural market access
  • intellectual property rights
  • government procurement
  • investment
  • service

EU-Andean Community Free Trade Agreement under negotiation

The EU agreed to start negotiations on an FTA with the Andean Community at the Latin America Caribbean Ministerial Summit held in 2004. The Andean Community consisted of Bolivia, Ecuador, Colombia, Peru and Venezuela, but Venezuela withdrew in 2006 and joined the Mercosur countries.

Negotiations with the Andean Community began in April 2007. Bolivia withdrew in September 2008, and negotiations were restarted with the remaining countries Ecuador, Colombia and Peru. The EU is the biggest foreign investor in the Andean Community, and the second largest trading partner after the US.

The trade part of the negotiations will lead to progressive and mutual liberalisation, through the development of an ambitious, comprehensive and balanced FTA that complies fully with the rules of the World Trade Organization.

The agreement under negotiation is broad-based, covering:

  • technical barriers to trade
  • competition
  • customs
  • sanitary and phytosanitary regulations
  • non-agricultural market access
  • intellectual property rights
  • government procurement
  • investment
  • service

EU-Association of South East Asian Nations Free Trade Agreement under negotiation

Negotiations began in May 2007 on an FTA between the EU and the Association of South East Asian Nations (ASEAN). ASEAN members are the Philippines, Indonesia, Malaysia, Singapore, Thailand, Brunei, Laos, Vietnam, Burma and Cambodia.

Progress on the FTA has been very slow because ASEAN has found it difficult to agree a common negotiating position. In addition, the UK does not wish Burma to benefit from the FTA.

The ASEAN region is very competitive, and offers important markets with which the EU wants to develop closer trade relations.

The agreement under negotiation is broad-based, covering:

  • technical barriers to trade
  • competition
  • customs
  • sanitary and phytosanitary regulations
  • non-agricultural market access
  • intellectual property rights
  • government procurement
  • investment
  • service

EU-Ukraine Free Trade Agreement under negotiation

The EU is Ukraine’s largest trading partner as well as its largest market, and negotiations began in February 2008 for an EU-Ukraine FTA. The EU believes that closer economic integration can be a key factor in economic growth for the Ukraine.

An EU-Ukraine FTA is part of Europe’s wider policy of creating a stable and prosperous European neighbourhood through closer economic ties.

The agreement under negotiation is broad-based, covering:

  • technical barriers to trade
  • competition
  • customs
  • sanitary and phytosanitary regulations
  • non-agricultural market access
  • intellectual property rights
  • government procurement
  • investment
  • service

EU-Canada Economic Integration Agreement under negotiation

Canada is one of the EU’s closest partners and the EU is Canada’s second largest economic partner. Negotiations for an EU-Canada agreement were launched at the EU-Canada Summit in May 2009. This agreement is expected to cover issues such as liberalisation of trade in goods and services, and better market access. The UK hopes that the agreement will include a very ambitious chapter on sustainable development.

The agreement under negotiation is broad-based, covering:

  • technical barriers to trade
  • competition
  • customs
  • sanitary and phytosanitary regulations
  • non-agricultural market access
  • intellectual property rights
  • government procurement
  • investment
  • service

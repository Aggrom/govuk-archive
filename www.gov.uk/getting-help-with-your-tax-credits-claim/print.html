Getting someone to help with your tax credits claim, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Authorisation

You must be authorised to talk to the Tax Credit Office about someone else’s tax credits. If you’re not, every time you call they must confirm their identity during the call.

Get authorised

It usually takes 2 days to authorise someone. You must:

  • send form TC689 to the Tax Credit Office
  • you, your partner (if you make a joint application for tax credits) and the person you’re representing must sign the form

The authorisation lasts 12 months unless you put a different end date on the form. Usually, you won’t get a letter confirming your authorisation, it can take a few days to set up.

More than one person can be authorised but each one must have sent a Form TC689.

Tax credits will not be paid into your account unless you’re the appointee.

Cancel an authorisation

An authorisation can be cancelled by writing to the Tax Credit Office.

Part 2: Appointees

An appointee is someone who has the legal right to act for a person who can’t manage their own affairs. For example, because they’re disabled or mentally ill.

You’re not an appointee if you just help someone complete their claim form.

How to become a tax credit appointee

Complete the appointee section on the tax credit claim form. You must be over 18 and have a bank account. You don’t have to be a relative.

Your responsibilities

You must:

  • sign the tax credit claim form
  • renew the tax credits claim
  • report any changes which affect how much money the person gets
  • tell the Tax Credit Office if you’re no longer the appointee

Any tax credits the person gets are paid directly into your bank account. If you make a false or misleading application you may be charged a penalty.

Stop being an appointee

Write to the Tax Credit Office within 1 month of when you want to stop being the appointee.

Part 3: Claim on behalf of your child

Your child is under 16

If your child has a baby, you can make the claim for both of them if they live with you. The money will be paid to you.

Your child is over 16

If your child is over 16 and has a baby, they can make the claim themselves. The money will be paid to them.

If the child and baby live with you - you may also be entitled to tax credits.

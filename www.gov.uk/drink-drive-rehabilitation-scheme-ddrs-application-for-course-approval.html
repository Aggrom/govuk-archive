Drink Drive Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS): application for course approval

DDRS aims to provide an educational opportunity for convicted drink-drivers to discourage reoffending and reduce road casualties.

Only applies to England and Scotland

Introduction

The Drink Drive Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS) was introduced to provide an educational opportunity for convicted drink-drivers. Organisations wishing to seek approval for DDRS courses should apply to the Driving Standards Agency (DSA) who will make the approvals decision on behalf of the Secretary of State. Information on how to do this is provided here.

We strongly recommend that organisations read the Statutory Guidance for Approved Course Providers, The DDRS course syllabus and the other relevant documentation before they attempt to compile an application for approval.

Organisations should be familiar with the legal requirements imposed on providers of approved courses, by the legislation relating to the DDRS:

  • Road Traffic Offenders Act 1988
  • The Rehabilitation Courses (Relevant Drink Offences) Regulations 2012
  • Drink Drive Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS): guidance for course providers from June 2013

The application for course approval requires organisations to explain what arrangements they will operate in order to meet the approvals criteria and how the operation of those arrangements will be evidenced (ie recorded).

Make sure you have fully completed all sections before sending it to DSA, as insufficient information will delay your application.

DSA will be unable to consider an application until the application fee is paid in full.

Applicant and areas

Section 1 of the application form concerns the organisation applying for DDRS course approval and where, should approval be granted, they intend to deliver their approved course.

It should be noted that, if course approval is granted, it will be granted to the legal entity whose details have been entered here. If this legal entity ceases to exist, eg a sole trader becomes a limited company or an approved organisation is merged with or acquired by another organisation, then the initial course approval will no longer be valid. The approval cannot be transferred, sold or otherwise passed from one legal entity to another.

If the nature or identity of the legal entity is unclear from the details provided, DSA will seek clarification and no approval will be granted until such clarification is received.

DDRS specified geographic areas

DDRS courses are offered in 22 specified geographic areas in England and Scotland only. Wales will implement its own provision.

Each provider will need to indicate the specified areas for which they seek course approval. You can download a map and list of the different areas.

It should be noted that a prospective course provider will be required to supply evidence demonstrating that they could deliver sufficient course provision to meet demand within those specified geographical area(s). Course approval may be suspended or withdrawn if it appears they are unable to do so.

Each applicant will be able to apply for approval to provide a course in as many of the specified geographical areas as they wish, provided they can satisfy the approval criteria.

Course approval is granted within specific geographical area(s). If a provider wishes to deliver courses in a different geographical area they will have to send a new course application and pay the relevant fees.

Course content and delivery

Section 2 of the application form requests information relating to the content and delivery of the course the prospective provider wishes to be approved. This includes:

  • outlines of course plans
  • how the course fits with the DDRS syllabus and statutory guidance
  • adjustments that the provider will make to the course to allow full learning and participation for those with specific learning needs or disabilities or those whose first language is not English

In addition a prospective provider should supply information on course and trainer evaluation processes. The provider is also required to submit with the course approval application, examples of the learning materials which will be provided to course participants.

All DDRS courses must be approved by the Secretary of State (SoS) and comply with the syllabus.

The course and the course provider must meet certain criteria in order for the Secretary of State to approve the course, these requirements must be maintained throughout the approval period.

These criteria are detailed in the guides:

  • ‘Drink Drive Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS): guidance for course providers’
  • ‘Drink Drive Rehabilitation Scheme (DDRS): course approval evidence requirement’

Facilities and trainers

Section 3 of the application form focuses on the facilities and trainers the prospective course provider intends to use when delivering the approved course. Information required includes:

  • details of each trainer the prospective provider intends to utilise for course delivery, including relevant experience and qualifications
  • the addresses of any confirmed premises at which training will be delivered must also be provided (on a separate sheet, or a separate file if the application is submitted electronically)
  • details of how staff will be deployed across the specified geographic area(s)

Providers must demonstrate:

  • how they will ensure the security of their staff and the safety of participants attending the course
  • that they have considered how they could deliver sufficient course provision to meet demand within the specified geographical area(s)

Administrative arrangements

Section 4 of the application form is composed of a number of statements, which the prospective provider is asked to confirm by placing a tick in each box. If the statement is not true, the box should be left blank and no tick inserted. If it is found that incorrect information has been given, course approval may be suspended or withdrawn.

Other approvals and accreditations

Section 5 of the application form allows prospective providers an opportunity to supply any details of relevant external approvals or accreditations (such as ISO accreditation) which they wish to bring to the attention of DSA in order to support the course approval application.

Payment methods

Section 6 of the application form asks the prospective provider to indicate their preferred method of payment for the application fee, from a choice of cheque, credit/debit card or BACS transfer. If a cheque payment is the preferred method, this should be included with the application form itself; for either of the other options, DSA will contact the applicant to arrange payment.

Fees

There is a statutory fee of £1,000 for each application for DDRS course approval which can be paid by:

  • cheque
  • credit or debit card
  • BACS transfer

Cheques should be sent with the application form. For credit or debit card payment or BACS transfer, DSA will contact the applicant by telephone to arrange payment.

Course providers will be required to submit to DSA a quarterly return indicating the number of offenders who have successfully completed the approved DDRS course.

A statutory fee of £7 for each offender who completes a DDRS course during this period will be payable to DSA. Fees are payable to DSA no later than 14 days after the close of the reporting period. Payments can be made via cheque, credit/debit card or by BACS transfer.

Course fees

The DDRS aims to reduce drink-drive reoffending. To keep fees affordable to the majority of offenders a maximum course fee of £250 has been set by regulation.

Course providers are free to create a structure of course fees to suit their business models subject to the statutory maximum. Providers are encouraged to keep the structure as clear as possible to avoid confusion for the court(s) and offenders.

Statutory course guidance requires that each offender must pay the course fees in full before they complete a course, even if they are paying by instalments.

How to apply for approval

To apply, download the application form, fill it in and send it to DSA.

A completed DDRS course application should contain:

  • all the required information
  • supporting information to show compliance with the requirements for approval under the relevant legislation
  • the relevant fee to enable the application to be processed

Processing your application

DSA will send an acknowledgement email to confirm receipt of your application.

The application will be processed within 30 working days of receipt and the provider will be notified by email of the decision as follows:

  • course approval
  • course approval subject to additional conditions
  • course approval refused

If any additional information is required pre-approval, DSA will contact the designated primary contact, detailing what is required and the date it is required by.

If DSA does not receive the additional information requested within the timescale given, this may prevent DSA from completing the approval process.

Prospective providers also need to be aware that if DSA cannot complete the approval process within the 30 working day period, due to it being incomplete or invalid, then all documentation will be returned to the centre.

In these circumstances, as approval has not been granted, there is no provision for a refund of the approval fee.

Granting course approval

DSA will grant course approval to any prospective provider that demonstrates that it is able to deliver:

  • an appropriate course
  • using appropriate staff and facilities
  • within the defined specified geographic area(s)
  • the necessary administrative infrastructure and processes to meet the approval criteria

Appeal against DSA’s decision

Please note that if course approval is refused or approval is given subject to additional conditions, the provider has the right to appeal against the decision to the First-tier Tribunal.

More information regarding appeal arrangements to the First-tier Tribunal will be provided by DSA.

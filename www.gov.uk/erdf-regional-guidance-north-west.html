Introduction

Under the 2007 to 2013 programme, the region can receive €755.5 million of European Regional Development Fund (ERDF) investment.

Calls for projects

The North West ERDF Programme currently has a live call for projects under Priority 1 of its investment framework.

Priorities

The North West Operational Programme document outlines the investment priorities for the region. It focuses on 4 main priorities:

Priority 1

Stimulating enterprise and supporting growth in target and markets, such as developing high value new enterprise, developing higher added value activity in target regional sectors, increasing sustainable consumption and production.

Priority 2

Exploiting innovation and knowledge, such as exploiting the science and research and development base of the region, encouraging innovation to improve productivity in all companies and developing better management and leadership.

Priority 3

Creating the conditions for sustainable growth, such as exploiting the economic potential of major gateways in Merseyside, developing high quality sites and premises of regional importance and supporting the improvement of the region’s visitor offer and image.

Priority 4

Growing and accessing employment, such as stimulating enterprise in disadvantaged communities and under-represented groups, supporting linkages to key employment areas and supporting employment creation for areas of regeneration need.

The full North West Operational Programme document setting out the strategy of the programme can be found with other documents of interest on the following pages.

Local ERDF guidance

From 1 April 2012, the Department for Communities and Local Government (DCLG) adopted a standardised approach to the management and delivery of the ERDF programmes across England. This includes the introduction of a new suite of documentation - including forms and guidance - which projects awarded funding after 1 April 2012 will need to use and follow.

For projects required to use the new documentation, visit the national guidance guide.

All forms and guidance in existence prior to 1 April 2012 that ERDF-supported projects have used or are currently using, are available from your local Programme Delivery Team.

To access this and for more information contact the ERDF Programme Delivery Team: North West on 030 3444 6520 or email nw.erdfenquiries@communities.gsi.gov.uk.

Local management

In the north-west, DCLG and its local delivery team is responsible for managing the ERDF programme, which is known as the Northwest Operational Programme (NWOP).

The local team allocates NWOP funding through a single appraisal process to different Action Areas within its 4 priority areas.

Local Management Committee

A Local Management Committee (LMC) has been formed to monitor, oversee and advise on the delivery of NWOP. The role and function of the LMC is laid down by European Union legislation.

The LMC is responsible for:

  • setting the direction for NWOP
  • driving NWOP forward
  • ensuring NWOP meets agreed objectives and targets
  • overseeing the strategic review of NWOP

The LMC comprises senior representatives from across the region from:

  • DCLG
  • 4NW
  • sub-regional partnerships
  • local authorities
  • higher education
  • trade unions
  • the private and voluntary sectors

The LMC is assisted by 2 sub-committees:

  • the Performance and Monitoring Sub-Committee
  • the Merseyside Phasing In Sub-Committee.

The Performance and Monitoring Sub-Committee assists the LMC in its duties in reviewing the Programme in more detail.

The Merseyside Phasing In Sub-Committee provides the LMC with further resources and informed comments to focus on Merseyside during its transitional ‘phasing in’ status.

Benefits & credits: Student finance 2012 to 2013

Notes

Part 1: Overview

If your course starts before 1 September 2012 you’ll get a different student finance package - see Student finance 2011-12.

Student loans and grants 2012 to 2013

If you’re a new student and your course starts after 1 September 2012 you can apply for:

  • a loan to pay your tuition fees - up to £9,000
  • a loan to help with your living costs (full-time students only) - up to £7,675
  • a grant for extra help with your living costs if your household income is less than £42,600 (full-time students only) - up to £3,250

There are rules on eligibility and how much money you’ll get.

Loans (plus interest) have to be paid back and repayments start once you finish your course and start earning more than £21,000. You don’t have to pay back any grants you get.

Extra help

You might also be able to get:

  • scholarships and bursaries from your college or university
  • extra financial help like Disabled Students’ Allowances or a Childcare Grant

Generally, this extra help doesn’t have to be paid back.

Part 2: What you'll get

Help with tuition fees

To help pay your tuition fees you can get a Tuition Fee Loan.

Help with living costs

Maintenance Loans and Maintenance Grants are for help with your living costs if you’re a full-time student.

How much you get depends on your household income. This is your parents’ or partner’s income plus your own. It’s assessed if you want:

  • the maximum Maintenance Loan (this amount varies depending on where you live)
  • a Maintenance Grant

Part 3: Eligibility

Eligibility

Eligibility depends on:

  • your nationality or residency status
  • your university or college
  • your course

To qualify for a Maintenance Loan, you need to be under 60 when you start your course - there’s no upper age limit for grants or Tuition Fee Loans.

Nationality or residency status

The following should apply:

  • you’re a UK national or have ‘settled status’ (no restrictions on how long you can stay)
  • you normally live in England
  • you’ve been living in the UK for 3 years before starting your course

You may also be eligible if your residency status is one of the following:

  • refugee
  • humanitarian protection (as a result of a failed application for asylum)
  • migrant worker
  • child of a Swiss national
  • EU national
  • child of a Turkish worker

Your university or college

This should be a university, college or other institution that offers a qualifying course. It must be in the UK and either:

  • funded by the government
  • privately funded but running courses that get government funding

Check with your university or college if you’re not sure whether they meet these conditions.

Your course

This should be one of the following:

  • a first degree, eg BA, BSc or BEd
  • a Foundation Degree
  • a Certificate of Higher Education
  • a Diploma of Higher Education (DipHE)
  • a Higher National Certificate (HNC)
  • a Higher National Diploma (HND)
  • a Postgraduate Certificate of Education (PGCE)
  • Initial Teacher Training
  • further training of youth and community workers

Check with your university or college if you’re not sure whether your course is recognised.

Generally you’ll only get student finance if you’re doing your first higher education qualification. However, you may still get it if, for example:

  • you’ve changed course
  • you’ve withdrawn from your course but decide to start again
  • you’re ‘topping up’ a higher education qualification – for example, you’ve finished an HNC, HND or foundation degree and now want to do an honours degree

Part-time students

Part-time courses need to be least 25% of an equivalent full-time course (for example 4 years instead of 1 year full time). This is known as ‘course intensity’. You can check course intensity with your university or college.

Part 4: How to claim

Sign up or log in to your student finance account to apply online.

The deadline is 31 May 2012 to get your money by the start of term.

If you can’t apply online, call Student Finance England for a form.

Student Finance England
0845 300 50 90

Part-time students

  • apply from summer 2012 – forms will be available then
  • deadline – up to 9 months after the start of the academic year

Application process

It can take up to 6 weeks to process an application and you may be asked to provide more information – eg evidence of identity or household income.

Part 5: Further information

When you’ll get your money

Money is paid as follows:

  • Tuition Fee Loans are paid to your university or college
  • Maintenance Loans and Maintenance Grants are paid into your bank account in instalments – usually at the start of each term

Student finance appeals

You can appeal a decision about the amount and type of student finance you get.

Download ‘How to appeal to Student Finance England’ (PDF, 32KB)

Paying back your student loan

You don’t have to pay back your student loan (plus interest) until you’ve finished your course and start earning more than £21,000 a year.

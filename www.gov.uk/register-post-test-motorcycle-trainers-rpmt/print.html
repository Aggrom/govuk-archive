Register of post-test motorcycle trainers (RPMT), a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

You can apply to join the voluntary register of post-test motorcycle trainers (RPMT) if you train full motorcycle licence holders who:

  • have just passed their test
  • are upgrading to a bigger bike
  • are returning to riding after a break
  • want to bring their skills up to date

When you join the RPMT you can also train fully qualified motorcyclists in the enhanced rider scheme.

Your details will be given to motorcyclists looking for training if you join the register.

Who can join the register

To join the RPMT you must:

  • be 21 or older
  • have had a full category A motorcycle licence for at least 3 years

Part 2: Ways to join the register

There are 2 ways to join the register of post-test motorcycle trainers (RPMT):

  • pass 3 qualifying tests
  • pass a theory test and complete a training course

Option 1 - pass 3 qualifying tests

In this option you have to:

  1. Apply to join the register.

  2. Pass a theory test.

  3. Pass a practical test to prove your ability to ride.

  4. Pass a practical test to prove your ability to instruct.

You don’t have to take the riding ability test if you’ve passed the Driving Standards Agency (DSA) special test with an ‘A’ or ‘B’ grade within the last 2 years.

You’re allowed 3 attempts at the riding ability and instructional ability tests. You have to wait 2 years from when you first passed the theory test before  you can take it again.

You must pass the final test within 2 years of passing the theory test.

Option 2 - pass a theory test and complete a training course

In this option you have to:

  1. Apply to join the register.

  2. Pass a theory test.

  3. Complete a training course accredited by DSA.

You must do the training course within 2 years of passing the theory test. You have to start the process again if you don’t.

Part 3: Apply to join the register

To apply to join the register of post-test motorcycle trainers (RPMT), download the application form and send it to the Driving Standards Agency.

Part 4: Taking the theory test

You can book your instructor theory test when the Driving Standards Agency has accepted your application to join the register.

Documents to take to your test

You must bring both parts of your driving licence - the photocard and the paper counterpart. You must take your signed driving licence and a valid passport if you have an old-style paper licence.

Your test won’t go ahead and you’ll have to pay again if you don’t bring the right documents.

Multiple-choice questions

You’re allowed up to 90 minutes for the multiple-choice part of the test.

You’ll be asked 100 questions in total. There are 25 questions in 4 categories. To pass the multiple-choice part you must get both:

  • an overall score of at least 85 out of 100
  • at least 20 out of 25 in each of the 4 categories

You will fail if you get an overall mark of 85 or higher, but don’t score high enough in each category.

Hazard perception

You’ll then be shown 14 clips that feature every day road scenes. There will be:

  • one developing hazard in 13 clips
  • 2 developing hazards in one clip

A developing hazard is something that may cause you to take some action, such as changing speed or direction.

You can get up to 5 points for each clip. The earlier you notice a developing hazard and make a response, the higher you score. You need to score at least 57 points out of 75 to pass this part.

Part 5: Taking the riding ability test

To book your riding ability test, use the application form that the Driving Standards Agency sends to you when you’ve passed the theory test.

The test lasts around 1 hour.

What to bring to your test

You must bring:

  • your valid Great Britain or Northern Ireland driving licence
  • a suitable motorcycle

You need to bring both parts of your driving licence - the photocard and the paper counterpart.

You must take your signed driving licence and a valid passport if you have an old-style paper licence.

Your test won’t go ahead and you’ll lose your fee if you don’t bring the right documents and a suitable motorcycle.

Rules for the motorcycle you use

You must bring a suitable category A motorcycle for the test. It must:

  • be properly taxed and insured
  • be roadworthy and meet all legal requirements
  • have a power output of at least 35kW

It must not display ‘L’ plates.

The eyesight test

You’ll have to read a number plate from a distance of:

  • 27 metres for vehicles with a new-style number plate
  • 27.5 metres for vehicles with an old-style number plate

New-style number plates start with 2 letters followed by 2 numbers, eg AB51 ABC.

You must use glasses or contact lenses during the whole test if you need them to read the number plate.

You’ll fail the test if you don’t pass the eyesight test.

Riding ability

Before you start the main part of the test you’ll have to:

  • answer 5 vehicle safety questions
  • wheel your motorcycle and use a stand
  • do an emergency stop, a ‘U’  turn or a slow ride

When the main part of the test starts, the examiner will give you instruction over a radio.

You’ll ride in varying road and traffic conditions, including motorways or dual carriageways where possible.

You’ll have to prove your ability to ride safely and legally at all times during the test.

Your test result

The examiner will give you the result at the end of the test.

You’ll pass the riding ability test if you make 6 riding faults or less.

You’ll fail if you make a serious or dangerous fault, or make more than 6 riding faults.

Failing the first or second attempt

You can take the test again if you fail the test at either your first or second attempt.

Failing the third attempt

You have to retake and pass the theory test again if you fail the riding ability test 3 times.

You have to wait 2 years from when you first passed the theory test before you can take it again.

Part 6: Taking the instructional ability test

To book the instructional ability test, use the application form you’re given when you pass the riding ability test.

The test lasts around 1 hour.

What to bring to your test

You must bring:

  • your valid Great Britain or Northern Ireland driving licence
  • a suitable motorcycle

You need to bring both parts of your driving licence - the photocard and the paper counterpart.

You must take your signed driving licence and a valid passport if you have an old-style paper licence.

Your test won’t go ahead and you’ll lose your fee if you don’t bring the right documents and a suitable motorcycle.

Rules for the motorcycle you use

You must bring a suitable category A motorcycle for the test. It must:

  • be properly taxed and insured
  • be roadworthy and meet all legal requirements
  • have a power output of at least 35kW

It must not display ‘L’ plates.

How the test works

The test has 2 parts and assesses your instructional ability. To do this, the examiner will play the role of 1 of the following types of trainee:

  • a newly qualified rider attending for further post test training
  • a rider upgrading their motorcycle and attending for development
  • a rider returning to motorcycling after a long break
  • a rider advised to get training after taking a riding assessment

You have to:

  • identify and prioritise areas of weakness in the trainee’s riding and give appropriate instruction
  • reduce the trainee’s areas of risk by using appropriate coaching methods
  • give a summary of the trainee’s performance at the end of the session, highlighting areas where they need more training or self-development

Part 1

The examiner will ask you do 1 of the following:

  • give a short presentation designed to initially raise awareness of rider risks
  • involve the trainee in an interactive practical machine and pre-ride check session

Part 2

The examiner will ask you to do 1 of the following scenarios:

  • move away, control and slow riding
  • junctions and roundabouts
  • motorways and dual carriageways
  • corners and bends
  • positioning
  • other road users
  • use of speed
  • progress and hesitancy
  • overtaking and filtering
  • separation distance

The examiner will asses you on how you:

  • assess the trainee’s riding
  • communicate with the trainee
  • give appropriate coaching and show methods that would reduce the faults and risks identified
  • communicate a final summary of the trainee’s riding

Your test result

You’ll be told your result at the end of the test.

Passing the test

You’ll get a letter if you pass. It will include a declaration that you must sign before you get a register of post-test motorcycle trainers registration certificate.

You should send this to the Driving Standards Agency with:

  • the correct fee for how long you’re registering for
  • 2 passport-style photos

Failing the test

You’ll get a letter confirming your result if you fail the test. This can be used as the application form for your next attempt.

Failing the first or second attempt

You can take the test again if you fail the test at either your first or second attempt.

Failing the third attempt

You have to retake and pass the theory test and riding ability test again if you fail the instructional ability test 3 times.

You have to wait 2 years from when you first passed the theory test before you can take it again.

Part 7: Managing your registration

You must carry your post-test training certificate with you whenever you’re giving training.

Renewing your registration

Your registration lasts for either 1 year or 4 years.

You should apply to renew your registration at least 2 weeks before your current registration runs out.

You’re responsible for remembering when to renew your registration.

To renew, download the application form and send it to the Driving Standards Agency (DSA) with:

  • the correct fee for how long you’re renewing for
  • 2 passport-style photos

Make cheques and postal orders payable to ‘Driving Standards Agency’.

You can re-register using the same form within 12 months of your registration running out. You won’t have to go through the qualifying process again.

Check tests and monitoring

You must take ‘check tests’ with DSA to make sure that you keep up the standards needed to be on the register.

You must take a check test within the first year of joining the register. DSA will contact you to arrange this.

You’ll get a grading of either ‘satisfactory’ or ‘unsatisfactory’ at each check test.

You can be taken off the register if you get the ‘unsatisfactory’ grade.

DSA can also visit you to monitor the training you give.

Part 8: Documents to record training

The Driving Standards Agency (DSA) will email you the following documents when you first qualify:

  • trainer pack syllabus and notes
  • rider development record
  • rider assessment form
  • DSA certificate of competence

You should use these to deliver and record training, and to issue certificates to riders who complete the enhanced rider scheme.

You should contact DSA if you need to be sent the documents again.

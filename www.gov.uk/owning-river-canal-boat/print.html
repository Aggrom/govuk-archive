Owning a river or canal boat, a guide from GOV.UK

Notes

Part 1: Overview

Before you can take your boat on canals or rivers in Britain, you need:

  • proof that it’s safe, eg a Boat Safety certificate
  • insurance
  • the correct licence or registration

Part 2: Boat safety

Your must get a Boat Safety Scheme certificate before you can get a licence or registration. The only boats that don’t need to get a certificate are privately owned open boats, like canoes and rowboats.

All boats have to be tested every 4 years.

New boats

If your boat is new it should already meet the standards, so you won’t need to check it.

Penalty for not having a certificate

If you don’t get a certificate for your boat, you will be given a penalty. The penalty will depend on which ‘navigation authority’ you’re in.

Part 3: Insurance

In most areas of the UK, as a minimum, you must have ‘third party’ insurance for at least £1 million. You could be fined if you don’t have insurance - the penalty depends on the ‘navigation authority’ in the area you’re in.

The Canal & River Trust website has a list of insurance companies that provide boat insurance.

Part 4: Licences and registrations

When you have insurance and a boat safety certificate, you need to buy the right licence or registration for the waterway you want to use. Contact the relevant ‘navigation authority’ below to get this.

Almost all of Britain’s waterways are run by 3 main organisations.

Almost all canals, plus some rivers, eg the Severn, Trent and Ouse

Register with the Canal & River Trust.

River Thames, River Medway, and the rivers of East Anglia

Register with the Environment Agency.

Norfolk and Suffolk Broads

Register with the Broads Authority.

Other areas

For other areas, contact the Association of Inland Navigation Authorities to find the relevant navigation authority.
